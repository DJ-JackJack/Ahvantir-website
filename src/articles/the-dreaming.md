# The Dreaming

The Dreaming is a plane that runs parallel to the waking world — not above it, not below it, but alongside it, separated from the material by the thin and permeable membrane of sleep. Every mortal who has ever closed their eyes beneath Ahvantir's skies has entered the Dreaming, however briefly and however unknowingly. It is the most intimate of all planes: not a distant realm requiring ships or rituals or the favor of gods, but a place the living visit nightly and remember only in fragments.

The plane's defining quality is that it is not fixed. The Dreaming is shaped by the collective subconscious of all who inhabit it — every dream, aspiration, terror, and unresolved grief that passes through a sleeping mind adds something to the fabric of the place. The result is a plane of infinite extent and unstable specifics, constantly shifting, folding, expanding to accommodate what is dreamed into it. There is no permanent geography in any mortal sense. A meadow dreamed into existence one night may be a labyrinth by the next.

## The Anchors

Not all of the Dreaming shifts. Certain areas hold.

These are called the Anchors of Memory — locations within the Dreaming that have been given such collective cultural or spiritual significance that they resist the flux around them. They are the dreams of entire peoples, held stable by the accumulated weight of shared meaning. The Anchors preserve things the waking world has lost: records, experiences, the felt presence of events that left no physical trace. Dream philosophers argue they constitute a kind of archive — one maintained not by any deliberate archivist but by the simple fact that too many people remember.

[[Nodd the Monarch of Dreams|Nodd]] preserves these Anchors. What that preservation requires in practice is not documented. Whether it involves active effort or whether Nodd's presence simply maintains the conditions in which they can persist is a question the plane's nature resists answering cleanly.

## The Once-Was

The [[Once-Was]] — spirits who linger after death, shaped by what they were in life — drift into the Dreaming in significant numbers. Nodd permits this. The Dreaming is, by Nodd's account and by the observations of practiced dreamwalkers, large enough to hold its ghosts.

Their presence shapes the Dreaming in ways that resist easy categorization. Dreams influenced by the Once-Was carry a distinct quality: a sense of inhabiting someone else's memory, of events carrying more weight than can belong to the dreamer alone. Dreamwalkers who have encountered the Once-Was within the Dreaming report that the experience feels less like an observation and more like an inheritance.

## Sovereignty and Access

The Dreaming is Nodd's domain. Their sovereignty within it is total.

Nodd is not a deity. They do not reside in [[The Ranjergon]], are not bound by the [[Deification in Ahvantir|Divine Gate]], and do not require worship to persist. They are a [[Loci]] — a spirit born not from a creature but from the Dreaming itself, given sentience and agency by the accumulated significance of what that place holds. Their power is rooted in sovereignty over the plane, and within the Dreaming, nothing of consequence occurs without their awareness.

Entry does not require permission. Sleep provides it, imperfectly and incompletely, to anyone who loses consciousness beneath Ahvantir's skies. What deliberate dreamwalking provides — what ritual entry, vision-seeking, and the abilities of Nodd's Chosen enable — is something closer to intention: the difference between drifting through a place and being able to navigate within it.

The distinction matters for those who have tried to do more than navigate. Spirits of chaos — most notably the Dyri and Kuku-Lo — have made attempts to corrupt or exploit the Dreaming's fabric. None have succeeded. Nodd's sovereignty is not a title. It is the condition of the place.

## The Eternal Library

At the heart of the Dreaming — or what dreamwalkers describe as its heart, which is a concept the plane's geography resists — lies a location called the Eternal Library. It is said to contain every story, memory, and thought ever dreamed by the inhabitants of Ahvantir, accumulated across all the time the archipelago has been slept in.

No mortal has found the Eternal Library deliberately. Some have stumbled into a space answering the description and been unable to locate it again afterward. Whether Nodd dwells there, or whether calling it Nodd's dwelling represents mortal intuition about a sovereign's relationship to the heart of their domain, is not established.

## Cosmological Position

The Dreaming occupies a liminal position that resists the categories applied to other planes. Unlike the [[The Astral Sea|Astral Sea]], it does not connect worlds to one another. Unlike the [[Faded Veil]], it does not mirror the material in any literal geographic sense. Unlike [[The Ranjergon]], it is not separated from mortals by any barrier.

It is simply parallel — always adjacent, always inhabited, always full of everyone who has fallen asleep.

This has made it historically the most accessible plane to ordinary mortals and the least recognized as a plane at all. The people of Ahvantir sleep into the Dreaming every night. They do not generally consider themselves planar travelers.
