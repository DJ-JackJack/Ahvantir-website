# Nyxthul

Nyxthul, the Beast of Fury, is a chaotic and ferocious deity within the ranks of the [[Eight Shadows]], embodying anger, violence, and unrestrained wrath. He is the primal force of rage, feeding off the basest instincts of aggression and inspiring destructive tendencies in those who succumb to his influence. Nyxthul despises restraint and discipline, viewing these qualities as weak attempts to tame the natural power of rage. His goal is to unravel the social order in Aru’Mas by inciting violence, causing chaos, and eroding the values of justice and self-control that the [[Order of Valor]] upholds.

Nyxthul’s followers believe that true strength is found in embracing fury and abandoning restraint. Unlike the more subtle cults, followers of Nyxthul often engage in open acts of violence and terror, making his influence palpable and feared across the city.

## Symbolism and Appearance

Nyxthul is often depicted as a towering, monstrous figure wreathed in flames, with sharp claws and wild, animalistic eyes. His most common symbol is a claw mark overlaid with flames, symbolizing unrestrained ferocity and the destructive power of rage. His followers might carry amulets bearing this symbol or, more commonly, tattoo the symbol onto their bodies as a mark of loyalty. This mark is known as the Brand of Fury, believed to grant Nyxthul’s blessing and strengthen his followers’ rage.

## Cults and Followers

Nyxthul’s followers, known collectively as The Bloodtide, are driven by a shared ideology that glorifies rage, chaos, and the rejection of control. The Bloodtide operates with a decentralized structure, consisting of loosely organized cells that act independently but share a common goal of sowing discord and violence across Aru’Mas. They are often found among mercenaries, criminals, and those who have grown disillusioned with the city’s strict order.

## The Bloodtide

The Bloodtide is not a single, unified organization but rather a scattered network of fanatics and misfits drawn to Nyxthul’s ideals of destruction. They are known for their chaotic, unpredictable behavior, and many members see their allegiance to Nyxthul as a way to vent their frustrations and anger. The Bloodtide tends to attract those who have been marginalized, wronged, or feel stifled by the city’s laws.

- Methods of Influence: The Bloodtide uses violence, intimidation, and public displays of anger to achieve its goals. Members are known to incite riots, commit random acts of destruction, and provoke fights in crowded areas, creating a climate of fear and tension in the city. They believe that every act of violence brings them closer to Nyxthul’s ideal of unrestrained fury.
- Inciting Crowds and Riots: The Bloodtide frequently infiltrates gatherings, protests, or other large public events, encouraging attendees to channel any anger or frustration into violent action. They take advantage of any underlying tensions within the crowd, pushing for clashes that disrupt public order and spread chaos.
- Targeting Symbols of Authority: Members of the Bloodtide often focus their aggression on symbols of order, discipline, and justice, particularly those affiliated with the Order of Valor. They attack guard posts, vandalize public monuments, and provoke guards and soldiers, seeking to undermine the city’s sense of security.
- Hidden Shrines in Abandoned Buildings: The Bloodtide’s shrines to Nyxthul are usually hidden in the city’s abandoned warehouses, sewers, or slums—places that symbolize neglect and resentment. These shrines are often crude, marked with blood and claw marks, where followers come to offer their fury to Nyxthul in ritualistic outbursts of violence and destruction.

## Goals and Methods

Nyxthul’s ultimate goal is to shatter the peace and order that the city of Aru’Mas relies on. By encouraging people to act on their basest instincts and embrace violence, Nyxthul seeks to tear down the structured society that the Pathite Pantheon protects. His methods are straightforward: spread rage, incite chaos, and break down all attempts to control or restrain the natural force of fury.

## Strategies to Undermine the Church

- Attacking the Order of Valor’s Recruits and Guard Posts: The Order of Valor, with its commitment to self-discipline, loyalty, and protection, is Nyxthul’s primary target. The Bloodtide often attacks Order of Valor’s recruits and guard posts, provoking the guards into retaliating and drawing them into chaotic battles. By disrupting the Order’s operations, Nyxthul’s followers hope to create a sense of lawlessness in the city.
- Spreading Mistrust of Discipline and Restraint: The Bloodtide tries to influence the city’s youth by glamorizing rage, rebellion, and the rejection of authority. They spread the idea that discipline is a weakness, arguing that true strength lies in abandoning restraint and embracing the power of fury. This tactic especially targets younger members of the city, encouraging them to reject the teachings of [[Alor]] and act on impulse.
- Instigating Conflicts in the Fend: In the Fend, the Bloodtide plays on longstanding resentments and local rivalries to incite violence. They provoke disputes between families, neighbors, and communities, particularly in places where disputes over resources or spiritual pacts exist. This tactic serves to destabilize the Fend, making it more susceptible to Nyxthul’s influence and reducing the support the Fendfolk offer to Aru’Mas.
- Establishing Fight Clubs and Outlets of Violence: The Bloodtide establishes underground fight clubs in various parts of the city, creating spaces where people can engage in brutal, unrestrained combat. These fight clubs not only promote Nyxthul’s ideals but also serve as a training ground for his followers, where they can cultivate their rage and perfect their combat skills.

## Nyxthul’s Temptations and Gifts

Nyxthul’s gifts come in the form of physical and mental enhancements that amplify rage and aggression. His followers see these “blessings” as proof that fury is the path to strength. However, these gifts come at a cost, often driving followers into a state where they can no longer control their own anger, leaving them as instruments of Nyxthul’s will.

- Ragebound Strength: Nyxthul grants his followers an increase in physical strength when they enter a state of fury. This rage makes them more powerful in combat, though it also leaves them unable to distinguish friend from foe, often resulting in violent outbursts even against allies.
- Red Mist of Fury: Nyxthul’s chosen can enter a state known as the “Red Mist,” in which they become nearly immune to pain and reason. While in this state, they can shrug off minor injuries, fueled solely by adrenaline and anger, but are unable to recognize danger or retreat.
- Flame of Wrath: Devoted followers of Nyxthul gain the ability to summon small bursts of flame when in a state of extreme rage. These flames, referred to as the “Flame of Wrath,” ignite anything they touch, symbolizing Nyxthul’s desire to spread destruction wherever his followers go.

## Church Countermeasures Against Nyxthul

The Order of Valor takes the lead in confronting Nyxthul’s followers, as they are often the first targets of the Bloodtide’s aggression. However, the [[Order of Harmony]] also plays a vital role, addressing the underlying causes of anger within the community and providing outlets that channel frustration into constructive activities.

- Training in Mental Discipline and Restraint: The Order of Valor emphasizes mental discipline and teaches its recruits techniques for controlling their emotions in the face of provocation. By instilling these values, the Order aims to protect its members from falling prey to Nyxthul’s influence.
- Public Displays of Unity and Strength: The Order of Valor holds public demonstrations that celebrate the power of self-control and community, reminding citizens of the virtues of Alor. These events reinforce the value of discipline, particularly among the youth, and serve as a counterpoint to the Bloodtide’s glorification of violence.
- The Rite of Tranquility: The Order of Harmony performs the Rite of Tranquility in public spaces, where they lead gatherings in calming meditations and rituals that release frustration without succumbing to anger. This ritual encourages citizens to find healthy ways to express their emotions, counteracting Nyxthul’s influence on those who feel overwhelmed by rage.
- Intervention Programs for At-Risk Youth: Recognizing that the Bloodtide often targets young people, the Order of Harmony runs programs to help at-risk youth find purpose and a sense of belonging without resorting to violence. These programs offer mentorship, skill-building, and education on emotional control, encouraging them to avoid the path of rage and impulsive aggression.

## Cultural Myths and Fear of Nyxthul

Nyxthul’s influence is reflected in numerous stories and warnings told among the people of Aru’Mas, often used to remind people of the dangers of unchecked anger.

- The Tale of the Burning Bandit: One popular tale tells of a bandit leader who once prayed to Nyxthul for strength in battle. Though granted his wish, he lost control over his own rage, attacking his allies as well as his enemies. Consumed by the “Flame of Wrath,” he ultimately burned to death, his fury destroying everything he had fought for. This tale serves as a warning about the dangers of seeking strength through anger.
- The “Red Rage” in the Fend: In the Fend, Nyxthul is known to appear as a terrifying creature that stirs up aggression in the animals and people who live there, a phenomenon the Fendfolk call “Red Rage.” Those afflicted by the Red Rage are said to lose all sense of reason, becoming violent and destructive until the effect fades. Fendfolk offer charms and herbs to ward off this influence, seeking protection from Nyxthul’s chaotic wrath.

## Summary

Nyxthul, the Beast of Fury, is a deity who embodies unrestrained rage and the primal urge for destruction, seeking to erode the structure and peace within Aru’Mas. Through his followers, the Bloodtide, he spreads violence, chaos, and the rejection of discipline. The Bloodtide operates through physical aggression and open rebellion, inciting riots, promoting brutality, and attacking the symbols of order and control. Despite Nyxthul’s influence, the Orders of Valor and Harmony work to contain his followers and protect the city from the chaos he represents. Through public demonstrations, rites of tranquility, and mentorship programs, they strive to reinforce the values of self-control, unity, and peace, preserving the stability of Aru’Mas against Nyxthul’s relentless fury.
