# Asgoraviel

Asgoraviel is a Usurper, a once-mighty Archangel who broke into the realm of Ahvantir after being cast down from the celestial heavens of his origin world. Known for his militant and tyrannical nature, Asgoraviel embodies a warped version of divine justice, enforcing absolute law with no regard for compassion or individuality. His arrival in Aru’Mas sparked a bloody conflict as he sought to claim the city as his domain, declaring it a bastion of chaos that required his "divine order." Though he was ultimately defeated and sealed beneath the city, his presence remains a lingering threat, both spiritually and politically.

## Asgoraviel’s Appearance and Presence

Asgoraviel's form is both awe-inspiring and terrifying, reflecting his celestial origins and the corruption of his fall.

- Appearance: Asgoraviel retains much of his angelic grandeur—standing nearly ten feet tall, clad in radiant but scorched armor, and wielding a massive flaming sword known as Judgment’s Flame. His six wings are tattered and streaked with ash, a stark reminder of his fall. His once-brilliant halo flickers erratically, more like a corona of burning steel than a symbol of sanctity.
- Aura of Oppression: Even in captivity, Asgoraviel’s aura radiates an overwhelming sense of authority and dread. Those who come too close feel a suffocating pressure to obey, as if their very thoughts are bending to his will.

## The War on Aru’Mas

## Arrival in Ahvantir

Asgoraviel’s entry into Ahvantir tore through the spiritual fabric of the archipelago, causing widespread turmoil. Unlike other [[Usurpers]] who subtly infiltrate or manipulate the land, Asgoraviel descended in a blaze of light and fury, proclaiming himself the righteous ruler of Ahvantir. Declaring Aru’Mas a haven of corruption and chaos, he waged war on the city, intent on subjugating its people under his rigid, absolute rule.

## The Conflict

Asgoraviel’s war on Aru’Mas was swift and brutal. His celestial legions, formed from corrupted spirits and mortals coerced into his service, assaulted the city’s defenses relentlessly. His forces wielded divine magic twisted into instruments of tyranny, striking fear into the hearts of even the bravest defenders.

The conflict reached its peak when a coalition of [[Sunspear Legion]]naires, [[Runeguard]], and a group of legendary adventurers confronted Asgoraviel directly. Though his power was overwhelming, the adventurers exploited his arrogance and managed to trap him within a cavern deep beneath the city, sealing him away with powerful wards.

## Asgoraviel’s Imprisonment

## The Cavern of Chains

The cavern where Asgoraviel is imprisoned lies beneath the [[Heartspire]], hidden behind layers of magical barriers and physical fortifications. Known as the Cavern of Chains, it is an imposing, otherworldly space suffused with oppressive energy.

- The Seal: Asgoraviel is bound by runic chains crafted from [[Runic Steel]], etched with wards designed by the [[Order of Wisdom]] and reinforced by celestial magic. The chains pulse faintly, glowing brighter whenever the Archangel attempts to resist his bonds.
- The Cage: Surrounding him is a gilded cage of magical origin, designed to suppress his divine aura and prevent him from influencing the outside world. The cage is guarded by elite members of the Runeguard, who rotate shifts to ensure constant vigilance.

## The Role of the Order of Wisdom

The Order of Wisdom is responsible for maintaining Asgoraviel’s imprisonment. Their scholars periodically inspect the seals, reinforcing the wards and ensuring that no cracks form in the bindings. This task is considered both an honor and a heavy burden, as prolonged exposure to Asgoraviel’s presence can lead to paranoia, nightmares, and even fanaticism.

## The Role of the Runeguard

The Runeguard serves as Asgoraviel’s physical jailers, tasked with guarding the cavern and defending it against any who might seek to free or harm him. Only the most disciplined and loyal members are assigned to this duty, as Asgoraviel’s persuasive power is a constant threat.

## Legends and Rumors

## The Chains That Weaken

There is a persistent rumor that Asgoraviel’s bindings weaken whenever significant chaos or lawlessness occurs within Aru’Mas. Some believe this is evidence that his essence is tied to the city itself, feeding off the imbalance he perceives.

## The Whispered Prophecies

It is said that Asgoraviel speaks in cryptic whispers to those who guard him, promising power and salvation in exchange for their aid. While most resist, there are tales of weaker-minded guards succumbing to his influence, vanishing into the city with no trace.

## The Prophecy of Freedom

An ancient prophecy, whispered among the more superstitious citizens, claims that Asgoraviel will one day break free during a time of great moral collapse in Aru’Mas. Some see this as a warning to uphold the city’s laws and values, while others fear it is an inevitable reckoning.

## Cultural and Political Impact

## [[The Church of the Threefold Path]]

The [[Order of Valor]] and Order of Wisdom hold differing views on Asgoraviel. While both agree on the necessity of his imprisonment, the Order of Valor sees him as a cautionary tale about the dangers of unchecked power, while the Order of Wisdom views him as a complex figure worthy of study, believing there may be lessons to glean from his fall.

## The People’s Fear and Fascination

To the citizens of Aru’Mas, Asgoraviel is both a distant threat and a source of grim fascination. Stories of his war are told as cautionary tales, warning against hubris and tyranny. Some, however, see him as a misunderstood figure, a potential savior wrongfully condemned. This belief is rare but dangerous, as it has led to secret cults seeking to free him.

## Political Tensions

The continued presence of Asgoraviel beneath the city is a point of contention for some factions. Critics argue that his imprisonment endangers Aru’Mas, believing he should be destroyed outright or exiled. Others, particularly scholars, argue that his bindings provide a unique opportunity to study celestial power and the nature of the Usurpers.

## Legends of Asgoraviel’s Powers

Despite being bound, Asgoraviel’s reputation as a fearsome being persists, and tales of his abilities are passed down with reverence and fear.

## 1. Judgment’s Flame

Asgoraviel’s sword, Judgment’s Flame, is said to burn with divine fire that purifies as much as it destroys. It is rumored to still exist within his cage, a symbol of his relentless authority.

## 2. Command of Celestial Legions

Before his fall, Asgoraviel commanded vast legions of celestial beings. Though these legions have been scattered, his whispers are said to call to the remnants, tempting them to join him once again.

## 3. Aura of Subjugation

Asgoraviel’s presence compels obedience and submission, a power so strong that even the most resolute individuals struggle to resist his influence when standing in his presence.

## In Summary: The Tyranny That Waits

Asgoraviel, the Fallen Archangel, remains a shadow over Aru’Mas, a lingering reminder of the city’s strength and its vulnerabilities. His imprisonment beneath the city serves as a testament to the heroism of the adventurers who stopped his conquest, as well as a warning of the dangers posed by tyranny and absolute power.

Though bound, Asgoraviel’s influence continues to ripple through the city, shaping its culture, politics, and even its spiritual practices. Whether he remains a defeated relic of the past or a looming threat for the future, his story is inseparable from the identity of Aru’Mas itself.
