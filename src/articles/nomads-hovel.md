# Nomads Hovel

The Nomad’s Hovel is a little-known and elusive cottage that appears in the wilds of Ahvantir. Few in Aru’Mas are even aware of its existence, and those who claim to have seen it are often met with skepticism. The Hovel manifests unpredictably in remote areas, providing sanctuary to adventurers and travelers in dire need of rest and safety. It is a place of quiet magic and comfort, cared for by a group of enigmatic [[Hearth Spirits|hearth spirits]] known as the Nyrbi.

## The Mysterious Appearance of the Hovel

The Nomad’s Hovel is a modest structure, appearing as a small stone cottage with a thatched roof and a chimney that emits a soft, comforting wisp of smoke. Though unremarkable in appearance, the Hovel exudes warmth and safety. Its interior adjusts to the needs of its guests, offering cozy beds, a crackling fireplace, and freshly prepared food and drink that seem to appear from nowhere.

The Hovel’s appearances are fleeting, and it is never found in the same place twice. It is said to manifest only when needed most—at the edge of a dense forest, in a barren wasteland, or amidst a raging storm. Adventurers lucky enough to stumble upon the Hovel often speak of the inexplicable relief they feel upon crossing its threshold, though they rarely encounter it again.

Unlike the spirits of Claims or the well-documented phenomena of Ahvantir, the Hovel remains largely unknown to the general populace of Aru’Mas. Its existence is more of a whispered legend among seasoned travelers and adventurers than a widely acknowledged truth.

## The Nyrbi: Hearth Spirits of Comfort

While the Nomad’s Hovel is shrouded in mystery, the Nyrbi, the spirits that care for it, are more widely recognized across Ahvantir. These tiny, invisible hearth spirits are known for their deep compassion and delight in helping those in need. Their presence is not confined to the Hovel; the Nyrbi often take up residence in the homes of struggling families, providing unseen assistance to those experiencing severe hardships.

## Nature of the Nyrbi

- Unseen Helpers: The Nyrbi work tirelessly but refuse to be observed. They take great offense to any attempt to see them or their work, including the use of magic such as See Invisibility. Should someone try to observe them, the Nyrbi will immediately stop helping, sometimes leaving entirely.
- Acts of Kindness: In homes where they reside, the Nyrbi might repair broken furniture, sweep floors, mend clothing, or prepare food. They operate silently and discreetly, and their work is often discovered in the form of small, pleasant surprises.
- Sensitive Spirits: While the Nyrbi are inherently kind, they are also sensitive to ingratitude or disrespect. Families or individuals who fail to appreciate their help may find their blessings withdrawn until an apology or offering is made.

## Offerings to the Nyrbi

To thank or appease the Nyrbi, families often leave small, thoughtful gifts such as:

- A bowl of fresh milk or honey
- A piece of polished silver or brass
- Small, handmade trinkets like woven charms or carved figurines

These offerings are usually left in a quiet corner of the home where the Nyrbi are believed to reside, and they are almost always gone by morning.

## The Hovel as a Reflection of the Nyrbi

The Nomad’s Hovel represents the ultimate expression of the Nyrbi’s care and hospitality. Unlike their work in individual homes, where they assist with small tasks, the Hovel is a complete sanctuary, tailored to meet every need of its visitors. Within its walls, food, drink, and warmth are always available. Guests find their beds perfectly prepared, their clothing cleaned and mended, and their wounds tended—all without ever seeing the spirits responsible.

However, the same rules of invisibility and respect apply within the Hovel. Those who attempt to spy on the Nyrbi or their work risk losing the Hovel’s blessings entirely. Offending the spirits can lead to the abrupt disappearance of the Hovel, leaving ungrateful guests stranded in the wilderness.

## Legends and Folktales

Although the Nomad’s Hovel is largely unknown in Aru’Mas, a few stories about its existence circulate among adventurers and travelers. These tales are often dismissed as fanciful exaggerations, but they provide insight into the Hovel’s mysterious nature.

- The Lost Knight: One tale tells of a knight who became lost in a storm and stumbled upon the Hovel. After a night of rest and recovery, the knight attempted to stay indefinitely, but the Nyrbi soon withdrew their care, and the Hovel vanished, forcing him to leave. The story warns against taking the Hovel’s hospitality for granted.
- The Grateful Pilgrim: A penniless pilgrim once left a scrap of bread as thanks for the unseen help they received at the Hovel. It is said that this simple gesture so pleased the Nyrbi that the pilgrim’s journey became miraculously easy, as though guided by an unseen hand.
- The Offended Spirits: In a cautionary tale, a mage tried to use divination magic to observe the Nyrbi at work within the Hovel. The spirits retaliated by banishing the mage from the Hovel and ensuring their magical focus never functioned again. The story serves as a reminder of the Nyrbi’s sensitivity and the importance of respecting their boundaries.

## Cultural Perception of the Nyrbi

While the Nomad’s Hovel is a legend among adventurers, the Nyrbi are more commonly acknowledged in Aru’Mas, particularly among the lower classes and those who have experienced great hardship. They are often viewed as guardians of the downtrodden, providing unseen support when life becomes too difficult to bear.

Families that claim to have been visited by the Nyrbi speak of them with reverence and gratitude. Some households even maintain traditions to invite the spirits’ presence, such as keeping a hearth lit at night or leaving a small bowl of honey by the fire.

The Nyrbi are also seen as symbols of humility and quiet generosity, celebrated in folktales and children’s stories as reminders that even the smallest acts of kindness can make a significant difference.

## In Summary: A Spirit of Hospitality and Mystery

The Nomad’s Hovel and the Nyrbi represent the profound yet understated magic of Ahvantir. While the Hovel itself remains a mystery, appearing only to those in dire need, the Nyrbi’s quiet compassion is a more tangible force, touching the lives of struggling families across the land. Together, they embody the power of unseen kindness, offering comfort, safety, and warmth in a world often fraught with danger and hardship.

Whether as unseen helpers in a humble home or as caretakers of the elusive Hovel, the Nyrbi remind the people of Aru’Mas to value the simple blessings of life and to always respect the quiet forces working tirelessly behind the scenes.
