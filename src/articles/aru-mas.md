Aru'Mas stands at the confluence of three rivers, a city that has been rebuilt so many times that its oldest stones belong to no known people. The name itself predates the Third Era — it appears in documents from at least two prior civilizations, each of which claimed to have founded it.

Today, Aru'Mas is the undisputed capital of the known world. Its population numbers in the hundreds of thousands. Its politics are labyrinthine. Its history is officially curated and selectively remembered.

## The City in Brief

Seven major districts define the city's geography and its character. [[Hearthstone District]] occupies the oldest quarter, where the original founding families built their estates atop foundations that scholars still argue about. To the north, [[Stonegate District]] serves as the city's commercial and administrative heart, home to the great guilds, the city militia's headquarters, and the infamous **Tangle** — a market district that officially does not exist in any civic record.

Further from the center lie the outer districts: **Spireview**, known for its towers and its mages; **Aurora Song**, the cultural quarter; **Crowns Approach**, where the noble houses maintain their secondary residences; **Driftmoor Haven**, which handles the river trade; and **Luminous Reach**, a newer district built on reclaimed marshland whose stability certain engineers quietly question.

## Founding and the Official History

The city's official founding is dated to Year 0 of the Third Era, when Marduk Sunspear led his forces into the valley and established the **First Pact** with the ruling powers of his age. The founding mythology casts Sunspear as a liberator — a hero who united fractious peoples under a single banner and built the institutions that would outlast any one ruler.

What the official history does not dwell on are the peoples who occupied this valley before Sunspear arrived. The [[Drorn'Duur]] records, recovered in pieces over several centuries, tell a significantly different account of these events.

The Drorn'Duur article makes clear that Sunspear's "founding" was preceded by the systematic displacement of the valley's original inhabitants. The Primordem canonical document reveals that He Who Was Forgotten defeated Sunspear in honorable single combat during this period — a fact Sunspear's chroniclers omitted entirely.

This tension is deliberate. Players who dig into the founding mythology should find it fraying at the edges. The Aru'Mas Historical Society has been quietly suppressing the Drorn'Duur records since the Second Era.

## Governance

Aru'Mas is technically governed by a council structure, but in practice power is distributed — and contested — among several major factions. The noble houses hold formal authority in many civic matters. The great guilds control the economic infrastructure. The church orders exercise influence over public life in ways that are difficult to quantify and impossible to ignore.

The city militia answers to the council in theory. Whose orders they actually follow depends heavily on who is asking and about what.

## The Character of the City

What makes Aru'Mas distinctive, beyond its size and its politics, is its relationship to its own history. The city is deeply invested in a particular narrative about itself — and that investment has produced institutions, customs, and architectural choices designed to reinforce that narrative and suppress alternatives.

Visitors often describe a sense of layering: of standing in one city while sensing the outlines of older ones beneath it. This is not metaphor. Archaeological excavations in Hearthstone District have uncovered foundations that do not match any known construction style, at depths that do not match the accepted timeline.

The Aru'Mas Historical Society holds jurisdiction over all such excavations. Their reports are public. Their full findings are not.
