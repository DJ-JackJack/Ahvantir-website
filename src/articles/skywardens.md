# Skywardens

The Skywardens are an elite aerial division within the [[Sunspear Legion]] , renowned for their mastery of high-altitude combat and rapid-response tactics. Tasked with defending Aru’Mas from aerial threats, performing reconnaissance, and offering rapid deployment in emergencies, the Skywardens embody the valor and daring of the [[Sunspear Legion]] . With specialized training and a deep bond with their mounts, they are among the most feared and respected warriors in the skies above Aru’Mas.

## Role and Purpose

The Skywardens play multiple critical roles in the defense and operations of Aru’Mas:

## 1. Aerial Defense

- The Skywardens serve as the primary defense force against threats from above, including rogue spirits, airborne attackers, and multiversal incursions. They patrol the skies over key areas such as the [[Heartspire]], Council Hall, and the [[Spiritsway Passage]] , maintaining Aru’Mas’ sovereignty over its airspace.
- In times of crisis, they are capable of launching rapid, coordinated strikes, using their mounts’ unique abilities to disrupt enemy formations or neutralize threats before they reach the ground.

## 2. Rapid Deployment and Support

- The Skywardens are designed for quick-response missions, allowing them to deploy swiftly across the city or to its borders. They are often the first responders to breaches, fires, or magical anomalies, using their aerial mobility to arrive swiftly at any scene.
- They provide air support during major battles, using ranged attacks and magical abilities to weaken enemy forces from above, making ground combat easier for the main Sunspear forces.

## 3. Reconnaissance and Surveillance

- With their specialized mounts, the Skywardens excel at scouting missions and long-range reconnaissance. They are capable of operating deep into the Ahvantir wilds, gathering intelligence and relaying crucial information back to ground forces.
- Their mounts allow them to blend into the sky or phase partially into the Aether, making them difficult to detect and perfect for spying on enemy positions or tracking rogue entities.

## Skywarden Mounts

The Skywardens are defined by their distinctive and powerful mounts, each uniquely suited for aerial warfare and various combat roles. These mounts are bred, trained, and magically enhanced specifically for the demands of the Skywardens.

## 1. [[Stormtalons]]

- Description: Stormtalons are massive, bird-like creatures with dark plumage that sparks with crackling lightning. They resemble giant eagles infused with storm energy, capable of navigating the harshest of weather conditions.
- Abilities: Stormtalons excel in storm-based attacks, capable of generating lightning strikes that can be directed at enemies from a distance. They can also create localized thunderclaps, disorienting foes and disrupting magic in their vicinity.
- Bond with Skywardens: The bond between Stormtalon and rider is intense, as the rider must be attuned to the elemental forces that power these creatures. Training involves mastering both the beast’s incredible speed and its volatile storm magic, making Stormtalon riders some of the most aggressive combatants in the Skywardens.

## 2. [[Aetherwings]]

- Description: Aetherwings are ethereal, almost translucent birds with shimmering, blue-hued feathers that seem to ripple with astral energy. They possess an elegant, streamlined form, appearing more spirit than flesh.
- Abilities: Aetherwings are capable of phasing into the Aether, temporarily becoming intangible to avoid attacks or pass through obstacles. They can also unleash bursts of raw aetheric energy, creating powerful shockwaves that disrupt enemy magic and break enemy formations.
- Bond with Skywardens: Aetherwing riders require a deep attunement to the spiritual energies of Aru’Mas. The bond with these mounts is more arcane than physical, often involving rituals of spiritual alignment conducted by the [[Order of Wisdom]]. Aetherwing riders are valued for their ability to evade, confuse, and strike unexpectedly.

## 3. [[Drakehawks]]

- Description: Drakehawks are a hybrid of raptor and dragon, with muscular, scaled bodies and fiery wings. They are powerful, heavily armored mounts capable of enduring intense aerial combat.
- Abilities: Drakehawks can breathe fire blasts, making them perfect for both direct attacks and laying down suppressive fire. They are also equipped with sharp talons and a dragon-like tail, which can be used in close-quarters combat during aerial skirmishes.
- Bond with Skywardens: The bond between Drakehawks and their riders is one of strength and dominance; riders must assert their control to fully harness the creature’s aggressive nature. This makes Drakehawk riders some of the most ferocious combatants among the Skywardens, often leading charges and overwhelming enemies with sheer firepower.

## 4. [[Skyblades]]

- Description: Skyblades are sleek, wyvern-like mounts with metallic scales that reflect light in a way that makes them appear almost invisible against the sky. Their long, bladed tails and sharp, razor-edged wings make them formidable in both offense and defense.
- Abilities: Skyblades are designed for aerial precision and agility, capable of executing sharp turns, rapid dives, and blinding speed. They can use their wing edges to slice through magical barriers or enemy constructs, and their bladed tails can deliver devastating strikes.
- Bond with Skywardens: Skyblade riders are trained for precision maneuvers and aerial tactics, often acting as elite scouts or assassins during combat. Their connection to their mounts is based on a mutual understanding of speed, stealth, and lethality, making Skyblades the ideal choice for stealth missions and surgical strikes.

## Training and Organization

Becoming a Skywarden is a rigorous process that requires exceptional skill, bravery, and a strong affinity for aerial combat.

## 1. Recruitment and Selection

- Only the most talented and daring warriors from the Sunspear Legion are chosen to join the Skywardens. Recruits must pass intense physical trials, demonstrate a natural bond with one of the four mount types, and show proficiency in both melee and ranged combat.
- Prospective Skywardens undergo a series of tests called the Sky Trials, which include fighting from moving mounts, engaging targets in mid-air, and surviving high-altitude conditions. These trials are designed to push recruits to their limits, ensuring only the best and boldest are selected.

## 2. Training Regimen

- Skywarden training emphasizes aerial maneuvers, complex formation flying, and tactics that make use of each mount’s unique abilities. This includes drills on precision diving, rapid acceleration, and both ranged and close-quarters combat in mid-air.
- Combat training includes the use of enchanted javelins, bows, and arcane bombs—potent magical devices that can be dropped or launched at enemy forces. Skywardens are also trained to use special aerial harpoons that can tether enemies or pull them into vulnerable positions during combat.

## 3. Structure and Command

- The Skywardens are led by Wing Commander Lysandra Veyne, a legendary warrior known for her unparalleled skill on her Drakehawk, Sundergale. Lysandra’s strategic mind and willingness to engage in daring maneuvers have earned her immense respect within the Legion.
- The Skywardens are divided into flights, each led by a Flight Leader who commands a group of 5-10 Skywardens. Flights are typically composed of riders with similar mounts to maximize cohesion and effectiveness in combat.
- Skywardens operate in pairs, known as Spearteams, focusing on synchronized attacks and mutual support. Spearteams are trained to perform complex maneuvers that blend offense and defense, often leading devastating assaults or quick-strike operations.

## Skywarden Missions and Duties

The Skywardens are tasked with a wide range of missions that reflect their role as Aru’Mas’ premier aerial force:

## 1. City Patrols and Law Enforcement

- The Skywardens conduct regular patrols over Aru’Mas, maintaining airspace security and monitoring for any unauthorized incursions or rogue entities. Their presence acts as both a deterrent to would-be attackers and a reassurance to the citizens below.
- In cases of civil unrest or major public events, the Skywardens perform aerial overwatch, providing support to ground units and using their mounts’ abilities to maintain order or create barriers when needed.

## 2. Expedition Support

- As the primary aerial unit of the Sunspear Legion , the Skywardens play a key role in supporting expeditions beyond the Spiritsway Passage . They often provide scouting services, clear paths through hostile skies, and offer fast extraction in case of emergencies.
- Their deep knowledge of Ahvantir’s wilds, combined with the speed and versatility of their mounts, makes them indispensable for exploring uncharted territories, forging new trade routes, and negotiating with powerful spirits that govern the archipelago.

## 3. Multiversal Defense

- With Aru’Mas acting as a multiversal hub, the Skywardens are often called upon to repel incursions from other realms. They engage in battles against interdimensional threats, rogue entities that breach the city’s wards, and enemy forces attempting to exploit the city’s strategic location.
- Skywardens often collaborate with the [[Order of Valor]] and the [[Runeguard]] , combining their efforts to maintain Aru’Mas’ security both in the skies and on the ground.

## 4. Ceremonial Duties

- As one of the most prestigious units in the Sunspear Legion , the Skywardens also take part in ceremonial events that honor the city’s history and culture. During events like the Ritual of Renewal or the opening of the Spiritsway Passage , they perform aerial displays, showcasing their mounts’ agility and the bond they share with their riders.
- These displays are not just for show; they reinforce the idea of unity between the people, the [[Spirits of Ahvantir|spirits of Ahvantir]], and the military might of Aru’Mas. The sight of Skywardens in synchronized flight is a symbol of the city’s strength and its commitment to maintaining the balance of power.

## Organization and Command Structure

The Skywardens operate under a disciplined, hierarchical command structure designed to maintain efficiency and unity during high-speed operations.

## 1. Wing Commander Lysandra Veyne

- Commander Lysandra Veyne is the current leader of the Skywardens, a famed warrior and tactician who earned her rank after leading several decisive aerial campaigns against rogue spirits and multiversal invaders. Lysandra rides a Stormtalon named Tempestclaw, a bond forged through countless battles.
- Known for her bold strategies and deep understanding of aerial warfare, Lysandra emphasizes team coordination and the importance of the bond between Skywarden and mount. Her leadership style is both inspirational and demanding, pushing her riders to excel in both combat and character.

## 2. Flight Leaders and Units

- The Skywardens are organized into flights, each led by a Flight Leader who commands a unit of 5-10 riders. Flights are typically composed of riders with similar mounts, allowing for specialized tactics that leverage the mounts’ abilities.
- Stormflights consist of Stormtalon riders and focus on lightning-based assaults and crowd control.
- Aetherflights consist of Aetherwing riders, excelling in stealth, reconnaissance, and support missions.
- Drakeflights consist of Drakehawk riders, who serve as the heavy assault units, specializing in firepower and devastating charges.
- Bladeflights consist of Skyblade riders, who act as elite scouts and precision strikers, perfect for surgical strikes and rapid engagement.

## 3. Spearteams

- Skywardens are trained to operate in pairs called Spearteams, emphasizing close cooperation and mutual support during combat. These teams are often composed of two riders with complementary mounts, such as a Stormtalon for offensive power paired with a Skyblade for precision strikes.
- Spearteams are designed to maximize versatility and combat efficiency, with each pair able to perform a variety of roles depending on the situation—whether it’s overwhelming enemy forces with combined attacks or providing rapid extraction in hostile territory.

## Reputation and Public Perception

The Skywardens are held in high regard by the people of Aru’Mas, seen as both protectors and symbols of the city’s aerial might.

## 1. Admiration and Respect

- Citizens admire the Skywardens for their bravery, skill, and dramatic aerial displays. The sight of them patrolling the skies is a reassurance of safety, and their presence at major events is a powerful reminder of the city’s strength.
- Children often dream of joining the Skywardens, inspired by tales of daring raids, aerial rescues, and breathtaking battles fought high above the city’s rooftops.

## 2. Awe and Intimidation

- For outsiders, the Skywardens can be both awe-inspiring and intimidating. Their precision, discipline, and the sheer power of their mounts make them a formidable force, often deterring would-be attackers before battles even begin.
- Within the adventuring community, the Skywardens are both allies and rivals—respected for their skills but envied for the prestige that comes with being part of such an elite unit.
