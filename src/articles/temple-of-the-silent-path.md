# Temple of the Silent Path

Hidden within the winding back-streets of **Temple Way** in **Aru'Mas**, the Temple of the Silent Path is a place of worship dedicated to **He Who Ceases Words**, one of the eleven Primordem spirits. Publicly, it presents itself as a meditation sanctuary. In truth, its inner circle venerates the Primordem and works quietly to weaken the seals of the First Pact.

## Description

The temple is a narrow, unremarkable building wedged between a tanner's shop and a disused warehouse. Its facade is plain grey stone with a single carved symbol above the door — a closed mouth, teeth pressed together. Visitors who know to look for this mark understand what lies inside.

The interior is cool and dark, lit only by thin tallow candles. The main chamber contains no iconography of the Pathite Pantheon — an unusual omission in Aru'Mas — and instead features a stone altar worn smooth by decades of hands pressing against it in silence.

## The Rite of Unmaking

The temple's primary ritual, performed monthly, requires all participants to observe complete silence for three days beforehand. During the rite, the presiding priest — called the **Silentmaster** — recites the Primordem's names backwards while the congregation presses their hands to the altar. Practitioners claim this weakens the spiritual bonds that keep He Who Ceases Words sealed.

{% dmonly %}
The rite genuinely does have a measurable effect. Each performance chips fractionally at one of the seals of the First Pact. The party may encounter evidence of this if they visit during or after a rite: a faint ringing in the ears, candles that won't stay lit, and a deep unease that lingers for days.
{% enddmonly %}
## Known Members

- **Silentmaster Orah** – The current presiding priest. Outwardly a harmless herbalist. Actually a skilled ritual caster.
- Three unnamed acolytes, likely drawn from Driftmoor Haven.

## City Militia Awareness

The city militia has received two anonymous reports about the temple but has not acted — both times, the investigating officer filed the complaint as "minor religious nonconformity." Whether this is negligence or corruption is unknown.
