# Cascades

A day’s walk eastward along the curving coast from Aru’Mas, the Cascades rise in breathtaking splendor, where a series of glittering waterfalls pour down from the steep, rugged mountains above. The waters seem to shimmer with a faint, magical glow, as if infused by the very essence of Ahvantir’s spiritual energies. Cascading over jagged cliffs, the falls split into multiple streams that weave through the lush greenery, creating a perpetual mist that catches the light of the sun or moon, making the entire area appear to be cloaked in a rainbow haze. The constant rush of the water creates a symphony of sound, alternately roaring and whispering, filling the air with the scent of fresh water and the earthy aroma of wet stone and moss.

The base of the Cascades is dotted with crystal-clear pools, their waters cool and inviting. Some locals swear that the pools have healing properties, while others warn that they are also home to mischievous spirits that love to play tricks on unsuspecting visitors. The Cascades are bordered by dense forests of fern-covered trees and wildflowers, creating a vibrant green contrast against the sparkling white of the falling water. Among the flora, strange plants with luminescent petals grow, said to be nourished by the magical mist that blankets the area.

The Cascades are a place of beauty and mystery, often drawing locals, pilgrims, and adventurers alike. It is said that at dawn, the sun’s rays strike the uppermost part of the falls, causing the water to seem as though it is made of liquid gold. At night, under the light of Miras, Toris, and Keltas, the falls take on an eerie, silvery glow, transforming into a surreal landscape that is both captivating and unsettling.

## Folktales and Legends of the Cascades

The Cascades are a rich source of folktales and legends among the people of Aru’Mas, inspiring both awe and caution. Here are some of the most well-known stories associated with this mystical place:

## 1. The River Maidens' Blessing

One of the most cherished legends of the Cascades is that of the River Maidens, benevolent spirits who are said to dwell within the sparkling waters. According to local folklore, the maidens appear as ethereal figures cloaked in mist, with hair that flows like water and eyes that reflect the colors of the rainbow.

If a traveler finds themselves lost or weary near the Cascades, the River Maidens might emerge to offer guidance, shelter, or even healing waters from the purest pools. Those who treat the falls with respect and reverence—by offering small gifts of flowers, herbs, or songs—are said to be rewarded with good fortune, safe passage, and even visions of the future. It’s common for pilgrims to visit the Cascades at dawn, hoping to receive a blessing of renewal from the maidens, believing that drinking from the enchanted waters at the right moment can cure ailments and grant wisdom.

## 2. The Echoing Spirit

While the River Maidens are revered, there is another, darker spirit said to haunt the Cascades: the Echoing Spirit, a restless entity believed to be the remnant of a betrayed lover. According to the tale, a young man, driven by grief and madness after his beloved vanished at the Cascades, leaped from the highest fall into the depths below, his cries of heartbreak echoing throughout the cliffs. His spirit is said to linger, forever searching for answers among the falling waters.

The Echoing Spirit manifests as a disembodied voice, calling out names in the mist and mimicking the sound of lost loved ones. Travelers passing too close to the upper falls at night might hear the haunting cries and feel compelled to follow the voice, only to find themselves dangerously close to the slippery edges of the cliffs. Locals warn that the spirit sometimes leads wanderers into the pools below, where they could be pulled under by strong currents, never to return.

## 3. The Cascade Wyrm

Among the scarier tales of the Cascades is the legend of the Cascade Wyrm, a mythical water serpent that is said to dwell in the deepest pools beneath the falls. Described as having scales like blue sapphires and emerald eyes that glow in the dark, the Cascade Wyrm is rumored to be both a guardian and a threat.

Some say it is a spirit of ancient vengeance, only awakened when the waters are disturbed by greed or violence. If angered, the Wyrm is said to rise from the depths, causing the waters to surge violently and pulling offenders into its watery lair. Conversely, those who approach the pools with humility and offer prayers to the spirits of the falls may catch a glimpse of the Wyrm’s shimmering form as it swims beneath the surface, leaving behind a trail of blue light. In this form, the Wyrm is said to grant protection to those who treat the waters with respect, serving as a guardian spirit of the Cascades.

## 4. The Waters of Truth

An old belief held by the people of Aru’Mas is that the Cascades are a place where secrets cannot be kept. It is said that if someone stands beneath the central fall, known as the Heart’s Pour, and speaks aloud the name of a liar, the waters will echo back the truth. This ritual is often attempted by desperate lovers, betrayed friends, or even those seeking justice, hoping to find clarity or expose hidden truths.

However, the waters demand a price: those who lie in return may find themselves struck by debilitating dizziness or left with a strange burn on their tongue. This tale keeps the Cascades as a place of fearful reverence, a reminder that the truth can be as dangerous as it is freeing.

## 5. The Spirit of the Pool of Reflection

One of the quieter pools beneath the Cascades is known as the Pool of Reflection, a still body of water surrounded by ferns and vibrant flowers. It is said that this pool can show not only the reflections of the present but glimpses of one’s past regrets or even potential futures.

According to lore, those who gaze into the pool on a night when Keltas is full may see visions that reveal hidden paths or unresolved conflicts. However, the pool is also said to reveal haunting memories and force the viewer to confront fears and mistakes they’ve long buried. Those who are brave enough to accept what they see in the pool’s depths often leave the Cascades with renewed purpose, while those who cannot face their reflections might be struck with melancholy, unable to move forward.
