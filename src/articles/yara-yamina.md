# Yara Yamina

The legend of Yara Yamina, the Witch of Whispers, has grown over decades into a story that transcends mere folklore. Her tale reflects the fears, failures, and superstitions of the people of Ahvantir, but at its core lies a profound mystery: who or what truly returned to [[Aru'Mas]] that day? Scholars, storytellers, and spiritualists have attempted to piece together the truth from fragmented records, witness accounts, and the chilling folklore that surrounds her.

## The Yamina Family's Tragic Legacy

## Fleeing the Dying Sun

The Yamina family’s world, whose name is now forgotten or erased by time, was a realm of desperation and decay. The dimming sun turned a once-vibrant planet into a twilight wasteland, plunging it into chaos. Refugees like the Yaminas fled through unstable planar passages, desperate to escape famine, undead hordes, and the suffocating curse that strangled their homeland. The sun glowed a pale purple, casting death and despair across the land. Among these refugees, the Yamina family stood out. Vanya Yamina, a quiet woman with sharp eyes and a soft voice, was regarded as resilient despite her frailty. Her husband, Elvenor, was a man of logic, seeking reason in a world gone mad. Together they shielded their daughter, Yara, from the worst of their homeland’s horrors—or so they thought.

## The Spirit’s Grasp

Many believed the Yaminas’ curse came not from their homeland but from Vanya herself. It began with whispers. Vanya would wake in the night trembling, clutching her arms as if fending off unseen hands. She claimed to see a shadow stalking their family, feeding on their grief and fear. At first, her husband dismissed these claims as trauma manifesting in delusions. But soon, others began to feel it: an oppressive cold in the air, an eerie silence whenever Vanya spoke of the “shadow.” The [[Order of Harmony]] intervened, cleansing the Yamina home with rituals to banish spirits. But the darkness always returned. Over the years, the spirit’s focus narrowed, haunting Vanya relentlessly. Her neighbors grew distant, and rumors swirled: had Vanya brought this curse with her? Had she made a pact in her homeland? Was she the shadow’s anchor?

## Yara’s Disappearance

When Yara vanished, the city mourned a child they barely knew. She was only eight years old, a quiet, observant girl who kept to herself. Some whispered that Vanya’s madness had consumed her daughter, while others believed the spirit had finally claimed what it wanted. Vanya insisted the latter was true, screaming into the streets that “it took her, it took my Yara!” The people of Aru’Mas, skeptical and weary, grew colder toward Vanya. She became a pariah, a ghost haunting the edges of society. When her body was found at the cliffs above the city, theories abounded: suicide, murder, possession. But no one could deny the eerie coincidence of Yara’s sudden return the very next day.

## The Return of Yara

The Yara who emerged from the wilderness was a girl transformed. She stumbled into Aru’Mas filthy and frostbitten, her limbs trembling but her smile unnaturally wide. That smile chilled everyone who saw it—a cruel grin that seemed more predatory than joyful. The frostbite on her fingers and toes blackened them beyond recognition, but she didn’t seem to feel pain. Her eyes, once bright with youthful curiosity, were clouded and empty, staring past everyone she spoke to. When asked about her disappearance, Yara would only say: “I walked with the shadow. It taught me many things. It’s still here.” As she grew, her behavior worsened. Yara lashed out with frightening strength, broke a boy’s arm with a single shove, and seemed to thrive on the fear she caused. Crops near her home wilted, and food brought into her house spoiled. Her family’s name became synonymous with tragedy and dread.

## The Witch of Whispers: A Living Legend

## The Sightings

- Travelers describe her as a pale, gaunt woman with frostbitten limbs and a chilling, empty gaze.
- Her presence is often accompanied by unnatural cold and faint, unintelligible whispers carried on the wind.
- Shadows around her seem to move unnaturally, as though alive.

## Powers and Abilities

The Witch of Whispers is said to wield terrifying powers:

- Breath Stealing: She can suffocate her victims by drawing the breath from their lungs.
- Soul Corruption: Her whispers drive her prey to despair or madness.
- Frost Magic: She commands the cold, freezing rivers and creating ice storms.
- Spirit Binding: She summons shadowy figures to do her bidding.

## Legends and Beliefs

The people of Ahvantir tell many stories about Yara, some rooted in fear, others in superstition:

- The Spirit’s Vessel: The most common belief is that Yara is the vessel of the shadow that haunted her mother.
- The Curse Returned: Others believe Yara carries the curse of her homeland, spreading death and cold wherever she goes.
- A Lost Soul: A sympathetic few see her as a victim, trapped between life and death.

## Yara’s Influence on Ahvantir

## Cultural Impact

Her tale serves as a warning about the dangers of breaking pacts, ignoring curses, or turning a blind eye to suffering.

## Superstitions

Offerings of warmth—scarves, gloves, or flasks of spiced wine—are left in the wilderness to appease her spirit.

## Scholarly Debate

Academics at [[Arrandak Academy]] and the [[Order of Wisdom]] argue over the nature of her powers and whether she is a spirit, a cursed mortal, or something else entirely.

## The Unanswered Questions

Despite the lore surrounding her, Yara remains an enigma:

- What truly happened to her during her disappearance?
- Is she still mortal, or has she become something more—something eternal?
- Does she retain any part of the innocent child she once was?
