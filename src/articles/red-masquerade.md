# Red Masquerade

The Red Masquerade is a mysterious and feared vampiric cult that operates in the shadows of Aru’Mas, moving unseen through the city’s streets, back alleys, and darkened corridors. With roots that stretch back centuries, the cult is known for its blood magic, covert rituals, and an elaborate hierarchy designed to remain hidden while exerting significant influence over the city’s underworld.

The Red Masquerade views itself as a secret society devoted to the pursuit of eternal life, forbidden magic, and control over the forces of Ahvantir. It is rumored that the cult's founders made pacts with ancient blood spirits, binding themselves to these entities in exchange for supernatural power and immortality. The cult’s presence in Aru’Mas is a dark reminder of the city’s dangerous allure, where ambition and magic intertwine in both noble and sinister ways.

## Structure and Organization

The Red Masquerade is organized with a strict hierarchy, operating much like a secret order. At its core, it is an occult brotherhood devoted to vampiric rituals, blood magic, and the subjugation of mortal and spiritual life in pursuit of its goals.

## 1. The Crimson Council

- At the top of the hierarchy is the Crimson Council, a small group of ancient vampires and powerful blood mages who dictate the cult’s strategy and oversee its broader operations. The council consists of five members, each with their own specific role:
- The Blood Regent: The nominal leader of the Red Masquerade, responsible for maintaining the pact with the Blood Spirit of Thirst, an entity believed to empower the cult’s rituals.
- The Veilmaster: The master of secrecy, responsible for managing the cult’s network of spies, informants, and disguised agents. The Veilmaster ensures the cult’s activities remain hidden from the ruling council and the [[Sunspear Legion]].
- The Bloodwright: The head of the cult’s magical practices, overseeing the rituals and ceremonies that enhance the members’ vampiric powers. The Bloodwright is also responsible for recruiting and training new blood mages.
- The Keeper of Masks: The controller of the cult’s masquerade events, using them as both a recruitment tool and a means to seduce influential figures in Aru’Mas. These events are elaborate affairs marked by opulence, secrecy, and the underlying threat of blood rituals.
- The Nightblade: The commander of the cult’s assassins and enforcers, responsible for carrying out covert killings, kidnappings, and silencing those who threaten the Red Masquerade’s existence.

## 2. The Masked Seraphs

- Beneath the Crimson Council are the Masked Seraphs, mid-level operatives who serve as the cult’s enforcers, spies, and ritual leaders. They often lead small cells of cultists, manage safehouses, and oversee operations in specific districts of Aru’Mas. Masked Seraphs typically wear elaborate red masks to conceal their identities during cult gatherings, adding to the air of mystery that surrounds the Red Masquerade.

## 3. The Bloodbound

- The Bloodbound are initiates who have undergone the cult’s initial rituals, gaining minor vampiric abilities such as enhanced strength, heightened senses, or limited blood magic. Bloodbound members act as the cult’s foot soldiers, informants, and ritual assistants, carrying out most of the cult’s mundane tasks, including gathering information, luring victims, and procuring supplies for rituals.

## Goals and Objectives

The Red Masquerade operates with several distinct goals, each contributing to its broader agenda of power, control, and immortality.

## 1. Pursuit of Eternal Life

- The cult’s primary aim is to unlock the secrets of true immortality through the use of blood magic and the forbidden pacts it maintains with blood spirits. Its rituals are designed to prolong life, augment vampiric powers, and bind new members to the cult’s cause.
- The cult’s ultimate objective is to create a Blood Ascendant, a being said to be the embodiment of blood magic and immortality. It is believed that if this entity can be created, the cult’s control over both the city’s underworld and the spiritual landscape of Ahvantir will be unassailable.

## 2. Control of the Underworld

- The Red Masquerade maintains a significant presence in Aru’Mas’ underworld, manipulating various criminal networks, smugglers, and black markets to further its agenda. It controls much of the city’s illicit blood trade, using it as both a source of sustenance and a means to corrupt and control those who fall under its influence.
- The cult uses its resources to fund bribes, blackmail, and assassinations, ensuring that those in positions of power either remain unaware of its presence or are too compromised to act against it.

## 3. Spiritual Dominance

- The Red Masquerade seeks to weaken the influence of the Old Pacts that protect Aru’Mas, intending to replace these spiritual agreements with their own blood pacts. By corrupting or subjugating the [[Spirits of Ahvantir|spirits of Ahvantir]], the cult aims to establish dominance over the region’s mystical forces, effectively rewriting the spiritual rules in favor of their vampiric ambitions.

## Interaction with Aru’Mas

The Red Masquerade has established several methods to operate within Aru’Mas without drawing direct attention from the authorities. While the cult’s activities are covert, its influence is felt across the city in various ways:

## 1. The Masquerade Balls

- One of the cult’s most insidious tools for recruitment and manipulation is the Masquerade Ball, a lavish event held secretly in different parts of the city. These gatherings are marked by opulence, mystery, and danger, often attended by members of the nobility, wealthy merchants, and even adventurous scholars who are lured by rumors of forbidden magic.
- These events are designed to seduce potential recruits, offering them a glimpse of the pleasures of vampirism and the power that comes with it. The balls serve as a cover for rituals, blood feasts, and clandestine meetings with those who might be useful to the cult’s plans.
- Many attendees are unaware of the true nature of the Red Masquerade, believing it to be a hedonistic secret society rather than a vampiric cult. Some who attend are marked by the cult, subtly influenced through blood magic to act in the cult’s interests without realizing it.

## 2. Covert Manipulation of Politics

- The cult uses its resources to infiltrate the nobility, Merchants’ Guild, and even the lower ranks of [[The Church of the Threefold Path|the Church of the Threefold Path]]. It seeks to corrupt officials who can aid its goals or protect it from investigation.
- Members of the Crimson Council have been known to bribe officials to divert investigations or to provide false reports to the [[Sunspear Legion]]. In some cases, the cult has used blackmail to silence potential whistleblowers, using the threat of exposure or ruin to keep its operations hidden.

## 3. Blood Trade and the Black Market

- The Red Masquerade controls a significant portion of the black market in Aru’Mas, especially when it comes to the trade of blood vials, forbidden magical ingredients, and occult artifacts related to blood magic. This trade funds the cult’s operations while also creating dependencies among Aru’Mas’ criminal elements and those who seek the cult’s wares.
- The cult’s grip on the blood trade has led to the creation of “Blood Dens,” hidden locations where initiates, cultists, and even desperate adventurers can obtain vampiric powers or forbidden knowledge at a terrible cost.

## Conflict with Aru’Mas Authorities

Despite its clandestine nature, the Red Masquerade is known to the ruling council of Aru’Mas and the Sunspear Legion, though much about the cult’s inner workings remains a mystery. The city’s authorities are constantly seeking to undermine the cult’s influence and eradicate its presence, but the Masquerade’s secrecy and ability to blend into the fabric of Aru’Mas make it a formidable adversary.

## 1. [[Runeguard]] Investigations

- The Runeguard, the arcane division of the Sunspear Legion, has conducted numerous investigations into the cult’s activities, attempting to break its hold over the city’s magical black market and to disrupt its blood rituals. The Spellbreakers carried by the Runeguard are often used to dismantle the cult’s magical defenses during raids, but the cult’s network of safehouses and hidden chambers makes it difficult to conduct decisive strikes.

## 2. Church of the Threefold Path

- The [[Order of Harmony]] within the Church of the Threefold Path has a vested interest in combating the Red Masquerade, as the cult’s use of blood magic directly threatens the city’s spiritual balance. The [[Order of Valor]] often joins the Runeguard in efforts to hunt down the cult’s members, but the Masquerade’s ability to corrupt and disguise itself within the city has made it a persistent danger.
- Priests and clerics from the Order of Harmony regularly perform rituals of purification to counteract the lingering corruption left by the cult’s rituals, but the presence of the Masquerade continues to fester beneath the city’s surface.
