# Runeguard

The Runeguard are the arcane defenders and enforcers of [[Aru'Mas]] , charged with maintaining the city's magical security, upholding the Old Pacts, and overseeing the use of arcane magic within the city’s borders. As both warriors and mages, the Runeguard combine martial prowess with runic magic, making them one of the most versatile and respected units within the city’s defense forces. They are tasked with both internal security and external operations, often acting as mediators between the spiritual and mortal realms.

## Role and Purpose

The Runeguard play multiple critical roles in the defense and administration of Aru'Mas :

## 1. Magical Law Enforcement

- The Runeguard are responsible for enforcing laws related to arcane magic within Aru'Mas , regulating everything from spellcasting in public spaces to the sale and possession of enchanted items. They ensure that all magic-users, whether residents or visitors, adhere to the city’s regulations.
- They often work alongside [[The Church of the Threefold Path|the Church of the Threefold Path]] to uphold the spiritual balance defined by the Old Pacts, investigating magical disturbances, breaches of the pacts, and potential abuses of arcane power.

## 2. Spiritual Wardens and Pact Keepers

- The Runeguard play a critical role in maintaining the city's spiritual harmony, serving as both wardens of the city’s boundaries and mediators between the citizens and the [[Spirits of Ahvantir|spirits of Ahvantir]].
- They oversee the rituals that reinforce the Old Pacts, working in tandem with the [[Order of Harmony]] to renew and protect the wards that surround Aru'Mas . Runeguard members are also tasked with patrolling the areas around the [[Spiritsway Passage]] , ensuring that no hostile spirits breach the city’s boundaries.

## 3. Arcane Defense and Combat

- The Runeguard are the primary arcane combat specialists in Aru'Mas , deployed to counter magical threats ranging from rogue wizards to spiritual entities. They are often called upon to handle magical breaches, rogue summoners, and dangerous spellcasters who pose a threat to the city.
- During major conflicts, the Runeguard act as the arcane artillery of the [[Sunspear Legion]] , capable of launching devastating runic attacks from a distance while protecting allied forces with powerful wards.

## Runeguard Gear and Equipment

The Runeguard are defined by their use of arcane-enhanced equipment that blends martial and magical capabilities, allowing them to adapt to a variety of situations.

## 1. Runic Armor

- Runeguard members wear runic armor, a blend of traditional plate and mail infused with enchanted runes. The armor is designed to channel and amplify the wearer’s magical abilities while providing substantial physical protection.
- The runes etched into the armor can be customized based on the Runeguard’s specialization, enhancing specific magical effects, boosting elemental resistance, or strengthening protective wards. The armor’s enchantments also allow it to absorb incoming spells, reducing damage and occasionally reflecting weaker attacks back at the caster.

## 2. Spellbreakers

- The signature weapon of the Runeguard is the Spellbreaker, a uniquely designed sword that functions as both a melee weapon and a magical conduit. The blade is inscribed with intricate runes that allow it to channel arcane energy, dispel magical effects, and disrupt spiritual entities.
- Spellbreakers can be used to amplify the user’s spells, making offensive magic more potent or defensive wards more resilient. The weapon’s unique design allows it to cut through arcane wards, magical barriers, and even bind spirits in battle.

## 3. Sigilbands

- Runeguard members wear sigilbands, enchanted bracers covered in runic symbols that enhance their spellcasting abilities. Sigilbands can store spell charges, allowing the user to cast spells even when physically restrained or in situations where other forms of magic might be suppressed.
- These bracers also provide a measure of warding against hostile spells, dispersing curses or magical toxins before they can take hold.

## Training and Organization

The Runeguard’s training regimen is one of the most demanding and versatile in Aru'Mas , requiring both martial and magical skills.

## 1. Recruitment and Selection

- Potential Runeguard members are selected from among the most talented battlemages, spellcasters, and magically inclined warriors of the [[Sunspear Legion]] and various adventurers’ guilds. Candidates must demonstrate proficiency in both combat and magic, with a focus on discipline and versatility.
- Recruits undergo a rigorous initiation called the Trial of the Runes, which tests their ability to channel runic magic in high-stress combat scenarios. These trials often include fighting against summoned spirits, dispelling complex magical traps, and maintaining wards while under attack.

## 2. Training Regimen

- Runeguard training is divided into two primary components: Runic Combat and Wardcraft.
- Runic Combat focuses on using runic weapons, casting offensive spells, and engaging in melee combat. Recruits learn to combine swordplay with spellcasting, striking at enemies while simultaneously weaving spells.
- Wardcraft emphasizes defensive magic, including the creation and maintenance of protective wards, the dispelling of enemy spells, and the binding of spiritual entities. Runeguard members are trained to identify and counter various magical effects, making them the first line of defense against arcane threats.
- Training also includes ritual magic, teaching Runeguard members how to conduct and maintain the complex rituals that protect Aru'Mas ’ borders and uphold the Old Pacts.

## 3. Structure and Command

- The Runeguard is led by the Runelord, currently Runelord Eleindra Yvael, a formidable mage-warrior known for her expertise in runic magic and her diplomatic skills in dealing with the spirits of Ahvantir. Eleindra’s leadership has seen the Runeguard play a key role in both military and spiritual operations, earning her respect among the city’s elite.
- The Runeguard is organized into Cohorts, each led by a Cohort Captain who commands 10-20 Runeguard members. Cohorts are typically assigned to specific districts or areas of responsibility, such as the [[Heartspire]], the Runeguard Citadel, or the Spiritsway Passage .
- Within the Cohorts, the Runeguard are further divided into Squads, smaller groups of 4-6 members that specialize in certain areas, such as Ritual Magic, Combat Spells, or Spirit Binding. This structure allows the Runeguard to maintain flexibility and adaptability in both defensive and offensive operations.

## Missions and Duties

The Runeguard is involved in a variety of missions that require precision, magical expertise, and a deep understanding of both arcane and spiritual dynamics:

## 1. Patrols and Magical Regulation

- The Runeguard conduct regular patrols throughout Aru’Mas, particularly in areas where magical disturbances are common, such as the [[Far-Farewell Market District]] or the arcane guild halls. During these patrols, they ensure that all spellcasters comply with the city’s laws and regulations regarding magic use.
- They also perform inspections of magical shops, workshops, and public ritual sites to ensure that all enchanted items and spells conform to legal standards.

## 2. Ritual Maintenance and Spiritual Mediation

- One of the Runeguard’s most important duties is the maintenance of the rituals that uphold the Old Pacts and protect the city from spiritual incursion. They work closely with the Order of Harmony to perform these rituals, reinforcing the wards that keep rogue spirits at bay.
- In addition to ritual maintenance, the Runeguard often act as mediators in disputes involving spirits, negotiating terms of peace or compensation for accidental breaches of the Old Pacts. Their diplomatic skills are as crucial as their combat abilities, as many spirits respect strength but demand a balance of respect and ritual observance.

## 3. Response to Magical Threats

- The Runeguard are the primary responders to magical threats within the city, whether it’s a rogue summoner unleashing a bound demon, a multiversal entity crossing into Aru’Mas, or a breach in one of the city’s wards.
- They are trained to engage both mundane and magical enemies, making them versatile defenders who can adapt to unexpected challenges. In major conflicts, they work alongside other Sunspear units, offering arcane artillery, magical suppression, and supportive wards to protect allied forces.

## 4. Multiversal Monitoring and Enforcement

- As a hub of multiversal activity, Aru’Mas attracts a wide variety of magical entities, some of whom pose a threat to the city’s stability. The Runeguard are tasked with monitoring and, if necessary, expelling dangerous multiversal visitors, using both diplomacy and force.
- They also regulate the movement of arcane artifacts that enter the city from other realms, ensuring that none possess curses, dangerous enchantments, or magical anomalies that could disrupt the city’s balance.

## Reputation and Public Perception

The Runeguard are viewed as both protectors and enforcers by the citizens of Aru'Mas , respected for their dedication to the city's safety and the upholding of arcane law.

## 1. Respect and Dependability

- The Runeguard are widely respected for their discipline, professionalism, and magical expertise. Citizens feel safer knowing that the Runeguard are always patrolling the city’s streets and maintaining its arcane defenses. Their presence is especially reassuring in the more arcane-centric districts, like the Far-Farewell Market District , where magical incidents are common.
- The Runeguard’s role in upholding the Old Pacts and protecting the city’s spiritual balance has earned them admiration among both the magical community and ordinary residents. Their regular interactions with spirits and enforcement of arcane laws make them the de facto keepers of order when it comes to magical affairs.

## 2. Cautious Respect and Wary Distance

- While the Runeguard’s dedication to maintaining magical law is respected, their strict enforcement can create a sense of wariness among some citizens, particularly hedge mages, spiritualists, and those who prefer to operate in the gray areas of magic.
- Merchants dealing in rare artifacts or experimental enchantments sometimes view the Runeguard as a potential hindrance to their business. The fear of confiscation, fines, or even imprisonment for unauthorized magic use keeps some magic-users wary of the Runeguard’s authority.

## 3. Mystique and Reverence

- The Runeguard’s involvement in rituals and spiritual mediation lends them an aura of mystique. Their presence at major rituals, especially those that uphold the Old Pacts, is often seen as sacred, and some citizens believe that the Runeguard’s rituals are the only thing standing between the city and spiritual chaos.
- Many citizens hold a reverence for the Runeguard's rituals, viewing them as not only defenders but also as arcane priests who maintain the spiritual safety of Aru’Mas.

## Missions and Relationships with Other Factions

The Runeguard’s responsibilities often overlap with those of other key organizations in Aru’Mas, requiring collaboration, negotiation, and sometimes competition.

## 1. Collaboration with the Church of the Threefold Path

- The Runeguard work closely with the Order of Harmony, one of the three orders within the Church of the Threefold Path. Together, they perform crucial rituals that uphold the city’s spiritual and arcane defenses. This partnership ensures that both arcane and spiritual magic are maintained in harmony.
- The Runeguard's relationship with [[High Priest Alabaster Sayn]] is both cooperative and strategic. Alabaster understands the necessity of strong arcane defenses but is wary of the Runeguard’s sometimes forceful methods. However, he acknowledges that their unwavering presence is crucial in maintaining the Old Pacts.
- Joint missions between the Runeguard and the Order of Harmony often involve both magical and spiritual threats, blending the strengths of both factions to protect the city.

## 2. Interactions with the Council and the Monarch

- As enforcers of arcane law, the Runeguard are directly accountable to the ruling council and the current monarch, King [[Voren Ilderas]] . Their enforcement of magical regulations is often dictated by council edicts, making them both protectors and instruments of the city’s political agenda.
- King Voren has a vested interest in maintaining the balance of magic within Aru’Mas, making the Runeguard one of his favored military branches. He appreciates their discipline and efficiency, often turning to them when dealing with sensitive political threats that require subtle force.

## 3. Overlapping Roles with the [[Nightcloaks]]

- The Runeguard’s focus on magical regulation and the Nightcloaks ’ emphasis on covert operations often leads to overlapping missions. While the Nightcloaks excel at stealth and assassination, the Runeguard are called upon to provide arcane support, acting as both magical shields and offensive forces when covert missions require extra firepower.
- While the two factions generally maintain professional respect for one another, there is a subtle rivalry when it comes to handling arcane criminals. The Nightcloaks prefer to operate in secrecy, while the Runeguard prefer direct intervention, sometimes leading to conflicting strategies.

## 4. Relationship with Adventurers’ Guilds

- Adventurers’ guilds operating within Aru’Mas often encounter the Runeguard during expeditions, artifact recovery missions, or investigations involving rogue magic. The relationship between the two groups varies; guilds generally respect the Runeguard’s authority but often find their strict regulations restrictive.
- The Runeguard, in turn, views adventurers with a mix of admiration and caution. While they recognize the value of adventurers in handling unpredictable threats, they remain wary of potential breaches of the Old Pacts or unauthorized magic use.
