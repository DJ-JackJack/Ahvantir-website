# The Astral Sea

Between worlds there is the Astral Sea.

It is not empty. [[Starleaper Fleet|Starleaper Fleet]] navigators — the closest thing Ahvantir has to a community of sustained astral travelers — describe it as a medium with its own properties, its own currents and weather, its own hazards distinct from anything encountered on the material side. The cosmological diagram maintained by Aru'Mas planar scholars calls it the scaffolding between worlds. That architectural framing is the most accurate available: a structure that holds other planes in relation to one another and provides the medium through which the distance between worlds is traversed.

The Starleaper Fleet's ships cross it. Others have crossed it before the Fleet existed. Marduk Sunspear crossed it.

## Properties of the Astral

The most documented property of the Astral Sea is its indifference to mortal biology. A body in the Astral does not age. It does not grow hungry or thirsty in any physical sense. Biological processes suspend in the Astral and hold, waiting, for however long the crossing takes.

What the Astral does not suspend is the mind.

Marduk Sunspear's fleet spent forty-nine years crossing the Astral Sea after the destruction of their homeworld. Their bodies did not starve during those forty-nine years. Their minds continued to register every sensation of starvation regardless — a psychological suffering without physical cost, sustained across half a century of transit. The fleet that made landfall on Ahvantir's shore was not a weakened force in the conventional military sense. It was a population that had endured fifty years of unrelenting psychological starvation. This fact is absent from Aru'Mas's official history. Those who know it tend to find it changes their reading of the Landing War considerably.

## Navigation

The Astral Sea has currents. This is not metaphor. Fleet navigators are trained specialists in reading those currents and tracking "pathways between worlds" — consistent routes and eddies that reduce crossing time or improve arrival precision. These currents are not constant; they shift in patterns that the Fleet tracks across generations of recorded transit data.

The Sea also has storms. "Void storms" is the term Starleaper crews use for the most dangerous atmospheric phenomena encountered during crossing — events capable of damaging enchanted vessels and disorienting even experienced crews. Time distortions have also been documented: conditions in which the relationship between shipboard time and elapsed time at the point of departure becomes unreliable. Fleet vessels are fitted with dimensional shields specifically engineered to resist both.

A persistent rumor among Fleet sailors holds that the city maintains an Astral Lighthouse somewhere in the Sea — a beacon, possibly older than the Fleet itself, that allows vessels to navigate back to Ahvantir regardless of how far they have traveled. Fleet command has neither confirmed nor denied this. That all six Starleaper vessels have consistently returned suggests reliable navigation back to Ahvantir is possible. The mechanism remains the Fleet's most tightly guarded operational question, if it exists at all.

## Ahvantir's Position

[[The Ranjergon]] notes that Ahvantir sits "at a nexus of planar, spatial, and dimensional travel." That nexus position expresses itself most directly through the Astral Sea: currents within the Sea converge on Ahvantir in ways that make the archipelago unusually accessible from a wide range of other worlds. Traders, refugees, scholars, and those pulled through tears in reality by accident rather than intention have all arrived by routes that passed through the Sea. This convergence predates Aru'Mas by a margin no mortal record reaches — the city did not create its position. The position shaped the city.

Marduk Sunspear found Ahvantir because the Astral Sea delivered him there. Whether that was fortune, the currents doing what currents do, or something more deliberate is a question Ahvantir's theological and philosophical traditions have argued without resolution for over four centuries.

## Known Hazards
- **Void storms** — violent atmospheric phenomena capable of structural damage to enchanted vessels
- **Time distortions** — variable relationship between transit time and elapsed time at origin or destination
- **Planar eddies** — currents that can deflect vessels from intended courses
- **Resident entities** — the nature and extent of any intelligent presences within the Astral Sea itself is not established in current Aru'Mas records
