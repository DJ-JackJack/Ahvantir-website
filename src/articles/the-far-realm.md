# The Far Realm

The cosmological diagram maintained by Aru'Mas planar scholars places the Far Realm at the outside edge of everything else. This is a navigational choice, not a statement about proximity. The Far Realm is not far in the sense of being distant. It is outside in the sense that it does not belong to the same order of existence as the planes around it.

Conventional planar cosmology proceeds through a logic: the Material Plane at the center; the inner and outer planes arranged around it in patterns corresponding to forces they embody. This logic has a shape — a structure that holds. The Far Realm has no relationship to that structure.

## What Is Known

Very little is known about the Far Realm with confidence. The category exists — and the category is agreed upon across the range of planar traditions that converge in Aru'Mas, which is no small agreement — because something must account for the phenomena that fall outside every other classification.

The Far Realm does not correspond to elemental forces, to emotion, to the moral axes that organize the outer planes, or to the logic of spirit, deity, or dream. Scholars who have studied it primarily study absences — the ways in which specific entities, conditions, or events cannot be explained by any known planar framework, and the pattern that emerges when you lay those absences next to each other. That pattern, or what might be a pattern, is what "the Far Realm" names.

Whether any Far Realm presence exists within Ahvantir's spiritual fabric — whether any entity operating in the archipelago can be traced back to it — is not established in any public record. Those who believe it is present tend to identify the same characteristic signs: influence that operates outside the known rules of spirit interaction or divine mediation; effects that cannot be traced to any known source; the particular quality of wrongness that trained observers learn to distinguish from ordinary strangeness.

## Cosmological Position

[[The Ranjergon]] is separated from the mortal world by the Divine Gate. [[The Dreaming]] runs parallel to the material. The [[The Astral Sea|Astral Sea]] provides the scaffolding between known worlds. Each of these planes connects to the others in ways that follow the logic of connection.

The Far Realm does not connect in those ways. Attempts to develop a systematic account of it have tended to produce accounts that are internally consistent and completely irreconcilable with every other systematic account of the same subject. Among scholars of planar philosophy, this is considered diagnostic rather than merely inconvenient.
