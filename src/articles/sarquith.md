# Sarquith

Sarquith, the Whisperer of Doubt, is one of the [[Eight Shadows]]—a dark deity whose insidious influence preys on insecurity and uncertainty, particularly among those who seek purpose and understanding. His power manifests through doubt, indecision, and the undermining of faith, causing individuals to question not only their beliefs but also their own abilities and connections. Sarquith is a shadow in the minds of Aru’Mas’ people, spreading seeds of uncertainty that hinder their resolve and warp their perceptions of reality.

Sarquith is most often invoked in hushed whispers or through unmarked shrines concealed in dark alleys, abandoned buildings, or remote areas of the Fend. Those who fall under his influence rarely see themselves as part of a cult but are instead lured by his power to resolve inner conflicts, only to find themselves in a spiral of doubt.

## Symbolism and Appearance

Sarquith’s followers depict him as a shadowy figure with blurred, indistinct features. His form appears fluid and ever-changing, often represented with a half-covered face or a hood that conceals all but his eyes—symbols of hidden knowledge and concealed motives. The Cloak of Shadows, a symbol of Sarquith, is sometimes worn by his followers or etched subtly into hidden shrines. The Cloak represents both the comfort of anonymity and the isolation of doubt, which Sarquith brings upon his followers.

## Cults and Followers

While Sarquith does not have an open or widely recognized cult, his influence can be found among two main groups within Aru’Mas and the Fend: the Silent Unseen and the Veiled Seekers. Both groups operate discreetly, using subtle means to achieve Sarquith’s goals of destabilizing faith in the Pathite Pantheon and promoting inner turmoil among the people.

## The Silent Unseen

The Silent Unseen are an underground cult within Aru’Mas that seeks to weaken the influence of the Pathite Pantheon by infiltrating the Church and spreading doubt among its followers. Members of the Silent Unseen often hide in plain sight, appearing as ordinary citizens or, occasionally, as low-ranking Church members. They pose as everyday worshippers, but their true purpose is to subtly sow distrust within the city.

- Methods of Influence: The Silent Unseen work gradually, engaging in quiet conversations with Church members, spreading rumors, and planting subtle but powerful doubts. They use phrases like, “Are we really free?” and “What if there is no purpose?” to lead others down a path of uncertainty.
- Targeting the Vulnerable: They often seek out those who are struggling or vulnerable, especially followers of the [[Order of Wisdom]] who might be wrestling with questions of purpose and destiny. They offer alternate perspectives, suggesting that life may lack meaning or that the teachings of [[Orenn]] are flawed.
- Hidden Shrines: The Silent Unseen maintain small, hidden shrines to Sarquith within abandoned parts of the city. These shrines are places where followers can seek his “guidance,” often finding themselves consumed by greater doubts after visiting. Shrines are typically decorated with reflective surfaces or shadows cast to distort perception, reinforcing Sarquith’s hold over their thoughts.

## The Veiled Seekers

The Veiled Seekers are a group that operates primarily within the Fend, where faith in the Pathite Pantheon is less pervasive. The Fendfolk, already reliant on precarious pacts with spirits, are more easily influenced by Sarquith’s philosophy that life lacks certainty and that traditional beliefs only offer a false sense of security.

- Questioning Spiritual Pacts: The Veiled Seekers prey on the Fendfolk’s relationships with local spirits, casting doubt on the reliability and intentions of these spirits. By questioning the efficacy of these pacts and suggesting that spirits act purely in self-interest, they create rifts between the people and the spiritual protectors of the land.
- Encouraging Self-Reliance to an Extreme: Unlike the Silent Unseen, who aim to destabilize the Church’s influence, the Veiled Seekers promote extreme self-reliance, fostering suspicion toward any form of outside guidance. They encourage Fendfolk to abandon faith in all but themselves, eroding communal support structures and leaving individuals isolated and vulnerable.
- Shadowed Sanctums: The Veiled Seekers maintain “Shadowed Sanctums” hidden within dense forests and caves. These hidden meeting places are meant for Veiled Seekers to meditate on “pure doubt,” encouraging their members to accept that life’s questions may have no answers. Sarquith’s influence is particularly strong in these sanctums, where followers are led into contemplative states that deepen their uncertainty and isolation.

## Goals and Methods

Sarquith’s ultimate aim is to corrupt the unity of the people of Aru’Mas by breaking down the foundations of faith and shared purpose that bind them together. He does not seek direct confrontation but works instead through gradual erosion, using his followers as conduits for doubt. His methods are subtle, relying on psychological manipulation rather than overt displays of power.

## Strategies to Undermine the Church

- Disrupting Teachings and Rituals: The Silent Unseen infiltrate public rituals and ceremonies of the Church, introducing questions and contradictory ideas that seem innocuous but ultimately lead to disillusionment. For example, they may quietly question the efficacy of the [[Order of Harmony]]’s blessings, casting doubt on [[Vesira]]’s power to heal.
- Encouraging Division and Skepticism: By leading people to question Orenn’s teachings about interconnectedness and destiny, Sarquith seeks to divide individuals from their communities. Followers often use “what-if” scenarios to plant the idea that destiny is an illusion, subtly separating individuals from the sense of belonging the Order of Wisdom promotes.
- Targeting Leaders with Subtle Persuasion: Both the Silent Unseen and Veiled Seekers may attempt to befriend or influence members of the Church leadership, particularly those with a contemplative or intellectual nature. Through “harmless” philosophical discussions, they plant seeds of doubt even in high-ranking individuals, causing them to waver in their own beliefs.

## Sarquith’s Temptations and Gifts

Sarquith’s power is not one of overt blessings but rather the ability to grant a perverse sense of clarity through doubt. He offers his followers insights that seem profound but ultimately undermine their resolve and isolate them from others. Those who seek Sarquith’s favor may be given the following abilities:

- Veil of Uncertainty: Followers of Sarquith are capable of casting a veil over themselves that makes their motives seem indistinct, allowing them to blend into crowds and avoid detection. This “veil” creates an aura of inconsequence, making them easy to overlook or forget.
- Words of Wavering: Sarquith’s followers can subtly influence others, causing them to question their own words, actions, or beliefs. This ability manifests as a whisper that leaves their targets with a lingering sense of insecurity, planting doubts about their decisions.
- Cloak of Shadows: Dedicated followers gain a Cloak of Shadows that allows them to step into and meld with nearby shadows, symbolizing their mastery over the art of concealment and deception.

## Church Countermeasures Against Sarquith

The Order of Wisdom and Order of Harmony are most active in combating Sarquith’s influence, as his methods often target the mental and spiritual wellbeing of Aru’Mas’ citizens.

- The Rite of Reflection: The Order of Wisdom hosts regular gatherings where people are invited to reflect upon and discuss their uncertainties in a safe, communal environment. These gatherings counteract the sense of isolation Sarquith promotes and help citizens find strength in shared purpose.
- Blessing of Clarity: The Order of Harmony performs the Blessing of Clarity on those who seem plagued by doubt. This rite is designed to bolster one’s confidence and faith, breaking the grip of Sarquith’s influence.
- Vigilant Oversight of Newcomers: The [[Order of Valor]] keeps a close watch on any newcomers within the Church, particularly those whose words or actions subtly conflict with Pathite teachings. This vigilance helps protect the Church from infiltration by the Silent Unseen and ensures that rituals and teachings remain uncompromised.

## Summary

Sarquith, the Whisperer of Doubt, is a dark, insidious force within Aru’Mas, quietly eroding the values of unity, purpose, and faith that the Pathite Pantheon upholds. His cults, the Silent Unseen and Veiled Seekers, work to undermine trust in the Church, preying on the vulnerable, spreading discord, and isolating individuals from their communities. Through the efforts of the Church, particularly the Orders of Wisdom and Harmony, Aru’Mas resists Sarquith’s influence, though his subtle, pervasive doubt remains a constant shadow over the city’s heart.
