# Opening of the Gate

The opening of the [[Spiritsway Passage]] is an event of immense spiritual and political significance in Aru’Mas, occurring only under specific and carefully controlled circumstances. Whether to allow passage for a sanctioned expedition, receive a high-ranking emissary, or renew an ancient pact, the ritual is regarded as a momentous occasion, steeped in solemnity, anticipation, and no small measure of awe. The event is often marked by a citywide pause, with citizens gathering to witness or participate in the rituals that shape the boundary between the civilized world of Aru’Mas and the wild expanse of Ahvantir.

## The Preparations

The [[Order of the Keystone]] begins preparations well in advance, transforming the area surrounding the Spiritsway Passage into a sanctified ground. The paved plaza before the gate is lined with arcane runes, freshly redrawn with a blend of charcoal, powdered crystal, and herbs meant to amplify the spiritual energy of the ceremony. The air grows thick with the scent of incense, a mix of lavender, sage, and spiritweed—herbs believed to appease and harmonize the spirits beyond the gate.

Rows of blue and white lanterns—colors symbolizing harmony and wisdom—are hung around the perimeter, their soft glow creating an ethereal ambiance. Citizens gather in the plaza, their expressions a mix of reverence, excitement, and trepidation. Many bring offerings, such as flowers, coins, or small charms, which they leave at the base of the gate as symbols of respect to the [[Spirits of Ahvantir|spirits of Ahvantir]].

The air is filled with murmurs—children pointing at the glowing runes, traders whispering bets on the outcome of the expedition, and adventurers tightening their gear in preparation for the unknown. [[The Church of the Threefold Path]] is also present, with members of the [[Order of Harmony]] distributing protective talismans to those preparing to cross through the passage, while the [[Order of Wisdom]] monitors the energy of the wards.

## The Ritual Begins

As the appointed hour arrives, the mages of the Order of the Keystone take their positions around the base of the gate. Wardweavers kneel at each corner, their hands raised toward the glowing runes, chanting in low, rhythmic tones that resonate deeply within the stones. Their incantations are a blend of old Ahvantirian dialects, known only to those intimately familiar with the Old Pacts.

The Gatecallers, clad in robes adorned with the sigils of the passage, stand directly in front of the towering stone doors. They form a precise line, each extending a hand toward the massive keystone above. As their voices rise in unison, a sudden surge of arcane energy ripples through the air, causing the runes to glow brighter and the ground beneath to tremble gently. The surrounding crowd falls silent, transfixed by the raw power of the ritual.

The keystone sigil above begins to glow with an intense, radiant light, spreading its energy like a beacon that pulsates rhythmically in time with the chants. The Gatecallers channel their combined magical force into the stone, focusing their power on the massive stone doors. As they chant the final words of the opening spell—an invocation of spiritual harmony, strength, and valor—the doors respond with a deep, resonant hum.

Slowly, with a grinding sound that reverberates across the plaza, the obsidian-veined granite begins to shift. The passage opens not with haste, but with ceremonial deliberation, the ancient stone doors parting to reveal the shimmering threshold beyond.

## The Threshold Revealed

As the doors open, the Veil of Concordance becomes visible, a thin, silvery barrier that separates the world of Aru’Mas from the wilds of Ahvantir. The Veil ripples like liquid moonlight, flickering gently as it attunes to the change. Beyond it lies the untamed expanse of Ahvantir, marked by dense forests, distant mountains, and an eerie calm that seems to watch the city with a silent, primal curiosity.

A gust of wind rushes in through the opening, carrying with it the rich scents of pine needles, damp earth, and the distant tang of ozone, a reminder of the unpredictable storms that often rage across the archipelago. The wind is more than just air; it carries with it the whispers of spirits, the faintest hints of ethereal voices that seem to weave through the crowd, brushing against those present with a sense of distant longing.

## The People’s Reaction

The citizens of Aru’Mas react to the opening of the Spiritsway Passage with a mix of emotions that reflect the city’s deep cultural ties to the archipelago beyond.

- Awe and Reverence: Many of the city’s inhabitants watch the ritual with expressions of awe, their faces lit by the faint glow of the runes. To the common people, the opening of the passage is not only a spiritual event but a reminder of the ancient pacts that keep Aru’Mas safe. Parents hold their children aloft, allowing them to catch a glimpse of the threshold to the unknown, whispering stories of past expeditions and legendary heroes who returned—or vanished—into the wilds.
- Anticipation and Excitement: For adventurers and members of the various guilds, the moment the gate opens is a call to action and glory. They gather near the front, ready to step into Ahvantir, some to test their mettle, others in search of lost knowledge or powerful artifacts. Their hearts beat faster, eyes filled with excitement, even as they grasp the potential dangers awaiting them on the other side.
- Fear and Apprehension: There are those among the crowd who bow their heads or make signs of protection and warding, murmuring prayers to the spirits for the safe return of those about to cross. For some, the passage represents an ominous risk—an opening not only for brave adventurers but also for unforeseen dangers that might cross back into the city.

The mages of the Order, though composed and focused, remain acutely aware of the significance of this moment. They are the guardians of the threshold, tasked not only with ensuring safe passage but also with holding back the potential chaos that could breach the Veil.

## Crossing the Passage

Once the passage is fully opened, the Pactkeepers step forward, ensuring that the spiritual energies of the Veil are stable. They make gestures of appeasement, offering chants and gestures that placate any spirits lingering at the threshold. They signal to the gathered adventurers, emissaries, or other approved individuals that the way is clear.

Those who cross the threshold experience a sudden shift in reality as they pass through the shimmering Veil. The sensation is like stepping into a different world—an immediate sense of pressure and energy, as if the wilds themselves are reaching out to assess each person’s intentions. Many report feeling a momentary tingle of cold or a brush of warmth, signs of the spirits’ touch, as they pass into the untamed wilds of Ahvantir.

## The Ritual of Closure

Once the last approved traveler has crossed, the Order of the Keystone begins the Ritual of Closure. The Gatecallers reverse their incantations, and the Pactkeepers offer a final round of blessings, ensuring that the Veil remains intact as the gate closes. The stone doors of the Spiritsway begin to move once more, their grinding sound a heavy reminder of the boundary being sealed once again.

The keystone sigil pulses one final time, its glow dimming as the stone doors come together with a reverberating thud. The runes settle back into place, their light fading to a soft, protective glow. The silence that follows is deep and resonant—a moment of spiritual finality, as if the very land is acknowledging the sealing of the passage.

The people of Aru’Mas linger for a moment, watching as the gates close, the energy of the event still palpable in the air. Some depart with renewed purpose, ready to share stories of the event; others remain a bit longer, contemplating the mysteries that lie beyond and the heroes who have dared to venture there.

For the mages of the Order, it is another successful ritual completed, a day of duty fulfilled. For the citizens, it is a reminder of the city’s strength and its place at the boundary of worlds—a gate both to the unknown and to the heart of the city’s enduring spirit.
