# Alor

## The Sentinel and Shield

Alor, known as the Sentinel and Shield of the People, is the deity of protection, courage, justice, and resilience. Within [[The Church of the Threefold Path|the Church of the Threefold Path]] in Aru’Mas, Alor embodies the ideals of a steadfast defender, a figure who stands as a guardian for those in need and a force against wrongdoing. Alor’s followers see him as a symbol of strength, integrity, and moral clarity, and his teachings focus on the duty to protect others and uphold justice with unwavering resolve.

Worshippers of Alor, particularly members of the [[Order of Valor]], view him as the ultimate example of what it means to serve the greater good selflessly. He represents not only physical protection but also the moral armor that allows individuals to make difficult choices for the benefit of others.

## Symbols, Iconography, and Sacred Spaces

Alor’s presence in Aru’Mas is represented through his symbols, his temples, and the imagery used by his followers. His iconography embodies the ideals of strength, courage, and the determination to defend.

## 1. Symbols and Iconography

- The Spear and Shield: The spear symbolizes Alor’s readiness to face threats directly, while the shield represents his defensive nature. Together, they signify his role as both protector and warrior, able to both stand guard and take decisive action when necessary. This symbol is often engraved on armor, shields, and banners within the Order of Valor.
- The Everwatching Eye: An eye set within a shield represents Alor’s vigilance, as he is believed to watch over his followers and the city, seeing threats before they can reach the vulnerable. This symbol is worn as a pendant by his followers and is placed at strategic points in the city to act as a “watchful eye” against danger.
- Iron Gauntlet: Alor’s followers use the image of an iron gauntlet to represent his strength and resolve. The gauntlet symbolizes the protection and honor they pledge to uphold, often used in oaths and ceremonies.

## 2. Temples and Sacred Spaces

- The Hall of Shields: The primary temple dedicated to Alor, known as the Hall of Shields, is a fortified structure where the Order of Valor conducts its training, ceremonies, and worship. The Hall houses a large altar with a towering shield, which symbolizes Alor’s protection over the city. The hallways are adorned with shields and spears, and its walls are lined with stories of past heroes who embodied Alor’s virtues.
- The Shrine of Oaths: This shrine is located at the center of the city, where people come to take oaths of loyalty, justice, and protection. Those joining the Order of Valor swear their vows here, invoking Alor’s witness as they commit themselves to the defense of Aru’Mas. Citizens also visit this shrine to make personal promises of loyalty to family, friends, or community.
- The Sentinel’s Keep: A watchtower just outside the city, the Sentinel’s Keep is both a military outpost and a holy site where the most devout followers of Alor keep vigil. Pilgrims visit the Keep to honor Alor’s teachings of vigilance, duty, and readiness, seeing the landscape from this vantage point as Alor himself might. The Order holds seasonal ceremonies here to renew their pledge to guard the city.

## Teachings and Philosophy of Alor

Alor’s teachings emphasize the values of protection, honor, and standing up for the oppressed. He teaches his followers that courage is not just physical bravery but also moral strength in the face of adversity.

## 1. Protection and Duty to the Vulnerable

- Duty Above All: Alor teaches that the primary duty of his followers is to protect those who cannot protect themselves, regardless of personal cost. Members of the Order of Valor see this as their highest calling, often risking their lives to defend the innocent.
- Resilience in Adversity: Alor’s doctrine emphasizes resilience, teaching that adversity should be met with an unbreakable spirit. His followers are encouraged to endure hardships with determination, understanding that they are guardians of the people and must remain strong even in times of crisis.

## 2. Courage and Integrity

- Courage in Action and Thought: Alor’s followers are taught that true courage means facing fear and acting in the face of danger. This courage is not limited to physical challenges but includes moral courage—the ability to make difficult choices and stand up for what is right.
- Upholding Honor and Truth: Integrity is a cornerstone of Alor’s teachings, and his followers are expected to conduct themselves with honesty and fairness. They are taught to never abuse their power or position and to act with respect toward all people, viewing honor as a form of personal protection that shields one’s soul.

## 3. Justice and Righteous Judgment

- Fair and Impartial Judgment: Alor’s followers are instructed to judge situations fairly, considering all sides before reaching a decision. Alor’s doctrine emphasizes impartiality and the need to pursue justice without personal bias, holding every life as valuable and worthy of protection.
- Standing Against Injustice: Alor teaches that passivity in the face of injustice is as harmful as committing the act itself. His followers are expected to stand against any form of oppression, bullying, or abuse of power, even if it requires them to confront authority figures or allies.

## Rituals and Ceremonies in Alor’s Honor

The Order of Valor conducts various rituals to honor Alor, seeking his blessings for strength, protection, and courage. These ceremonies are performed publicly and privately, serving as reminders of the Order’s responsibilities to Aru’Mas.

## 1. The Oath of the Iron Gauntlet

- New recruits and members of the Order of Valor take the Oath of the Iron Gauntlet when they join. During this ceremony, they kneel before the High Warden, who holds a ceremonial iron gauntlet over them, representing Alor’s protection and strength. The oath binds them to protect Aru’Mas, uphold justice, and serve selflessly, symbolizing the strength they will embody as Alor’s warriors.

## 2. The Vigil of Honor

- The Vigil of Honor is a ceremony held in the Hall of Shields where members of the Order honor fallen comrades and reaffirm their commitment to Alor’s teachings. During the vigil, members keep silent watch by candlelight, each holding a shield with their hand upon their heart, as a symbol of unwavering loyalty. The vigil is held annually, reminding the Order of those who sacrificed their lives for the city’s protection.

## 3. The Rite of the Watchful Eye

- The Rite of the Watchful Eye is a seasonal ceremony performed at the Sentinel’s Keep, where members of the Order stand vigil overnight, taking shifts to guard the city’s borders. This ritual is seen as a renewal of their pledge to be ever-vigilant and ready to act at a moment’s notice. Citizens sometimes join the ceremony as a show of unity and trust in Alor’s protection.

## 4. The Blessing of Arms

- Before going into battle or embarking on a significant mission, the Order performs the Blessing of Arms ceremony. The High Warden blesses the warriors’ weapons, invoking Alor’s strength and protection. This ritual instills courage and focus in the warriors, as they believe their weapons carry Alor’s power, shielding them and enhancing their resolve in combat.

## Worship of Alor in Daily Life

Alor’s teachings and values resonate throughout the lives of Aru’Mas’ citizens, influencing their sense of duty, courage, and justice. His followers incorporate his philosophy into their daily actions, especially in how they interact with others and conduct themselves.

## 1. Small Acts of Protection

- Followers of Alor often take small protective actions, whether it’s helping someone carry a heavy load, standing up for someone in need, or offering guidance to those who are struggling. These small gestures of protection are seen as reflections of Alor’s principles, reinforcing the idea that everyone has a role to play in safeguarding the community.

## 2. Honoring Promises and Oaths

- Honoring promises and commitments is a sacred act to Alor’s followers. Breaking a vow is viewed as a significant transgression, as it goes against the principles of integrity and honor. Many citizens make personal vows at the Shrine of Oaths, viewing this as a way to solidify their intentions and to show respect for Alor’s teachings.

## 3. Daily Reflection and Gratitude

- Followers of Alor often engage in a brief, daily moment of reflection, offering gratitude for the safety of their loved ones and strength in their own lives. This practice is especially common among the city’s guards, soldiers, and members of the Order of Valor, as it reminds them of their commitment to protect and serve.

## 4. Alms for the Displaced and Vulnerable

- Many worshippers of Alor feel a responsibility to support the city’s less fortunate. Acts of charity, such as donating food, clothing, or money, are common among Alor’s followers, as they seek to offer protection and support to those in difficult circumstances. This practice reinforces Alor’s teachings that protecting others goes beyond physical strength and includes aiding those who are struggling.

## Myths and Legends of Alor

The people of Aru’Mas tell stories of Alor’s courage and justice, using these tales to reinforce his values and provide guidance in challenging situations.

- The Tale of Alor’s Shield: One popular legend tells of a time when Alor descended to Ahvantir to protect a village under siege by dark forces. Alor’s shield was so powerful that it reflected the attackers’ assaults back upon them, saving the village without the need for violence. This tale emphasizes the value of defense over aggression, inspiring followers to protect others without unnecessary harm.
- The Legend of the Broken Spear: Another tale tells of a brave knight who tried to defend a mountain pass alone, but her spear broke in the struggle. When she prayed to Alor, he mended her spear with a single touch, allowing her to stand firm. This legend is told as a lesson that courage and resolve attract Alor’s blessings, even in the most hopeless circumstances.

## Alor’s Influence on Aru’Mas

Alor’s teachings profoundly impact the moral and ethical fabric of Aru’Mas, creating a city that values honor, courage, and protection. His influence shapes not only the members of the Order of Valor but also the attitudes of the city’s citizens, inspiring them to live with integrity, care for their neighbors, and respect those who stand guard over them. Alor’s presence is a guiding force, encouraging the people of Aru’Mas to embody the virtues of a protector and uphold the tenets of justice.

To the people of Aru’Mas, Alor represents more than a warrior god—he is the spirit of unity, honor, and resilience. His teachings remind them that true strength lies not in personal glory but in the selfless defense of others, a value that unites the community in shared responsibility and unwavering resolve.

Like all Greater Deities, Alor resides in [[The Ranjergon]] — the Deific Plane — from which he observes the mortal world and extends his influence through the faithful who serve him.
