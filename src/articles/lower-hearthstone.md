# Lower [[Hearthstone]]

> **District Split — R4**
> Lower Hearthstone and [[Upper Hearthstone]] were once a single district. See [[Open Questions]] for the full resolution.
## Overview

Lower Hearthstone sits south of the Spillway — the grittier but no less vibrant half of the original Hearthstone. When the Spillway expanded and forced a choice, Lower Hearthstone declined the walls that Upper Hearthstone built. That decision kept the district porous, community-focused, and deeply tied to the Spillway's fate.

Its residents are defined by resilience and a refusal to look away from hardship. Many families have ties reaching into the Spillway proper, and the district's culture reflects that proximity — colourful, creative, adaptive.

## Architecture & Streets

Homes are smaller and more modest, often built with reclaimed materials. The streets are lively but less polished, with colourful murals and street art reflecting the creativity and determination of the residents.

## Community Life

The Lower Hearth is known for its vibrant open-air markets and spontaneous street fairs — handcrafted goods, exotic foods, merchants, performers, and artisans. Despite economic and social challenges from Spillway proximity, the strong sense of community means neighbours look out for each other.

## Key Sites

- **The Spillgate Bazaar** — A semi-permanent market near the Spillway border. Practical tools to handcrafted jewellery.
- **The Lantern Walk** — A path lit by hanging lanterns at night; a safe and inviting route through the district, symbolic of the residents' determination to find light in the darkness.
- **The Riverbend Commons** — Where Lower Hearthstone meets the Spillway. A modest amphitheatre used for performances, debates, and storytelling. Residents of both areas gather here.

## Relationships with Other Districts

- [[Upper Hearthstone]] — The shared history is real; the wall between them is also real. Community efforts still cross the divide.
- [[The Spillway]] — The Spillway is not just a neighbour — it's family, for many Lower Hearthstone residents. The district's character was shaped by choosing solidarity over separation.
- [[Temple Way]] — Access to spiritual life, though the relationship is more transactional than Upper Hearthstone's.
