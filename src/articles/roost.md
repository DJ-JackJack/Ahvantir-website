# Roost

The Roost is one of the most iconic and imposing structures in Aru’Mas, second only to the [[Heartspire]] in stature and significance. Situated within the city’s military district, it serves as the primary base of operations for the [[Skywardens]], the elite aerial unit of the [[Sunspear Legion]]. The Roost stands as both a sanctuary and a proving ground, where riders forge unbreakable bonds with their mounts and hone their skills in aerial combat, reconnaissance, and patrols. As one of the defining landmarks of Aru’Mas, the Roost embodies the city’s strength, resilience, and connection with the skies.

## Architecture and Design

The Roost’s architecture reflects the elegance and strength of the Skywardens and their mounts. Constructed from reinforced stone imbued with protective runes, the tower rises above the surrounding cityscape, visible from nearly every district of Aru’Mas. The structure features intricate carvings of soaring creatures and symbols representing the four Skywarden mounts: [[Stormtalons]], [[Aetherwings]], [[Drakehawks]], and [[Skyblades]]. The tower’s segmented design serves functional and strategic purposes, with each level dedicated to a specific aspect of Skywarden training and operations. Its base houses the living quarters and training grounds for recruits, while the upper levels include the Sky Stables, launch platforms, and tactical command rooms. Despite its formidable presence, the Roost is designed with an open-air aesthetic, incorporating high arches and vast balconies to accommodate the creatures and their need for space and freedom.

## The Sky Stables

At the pinnacle of the Roost lies the Sky Stables, a massive, open-air platform sheltered by towering arches. This sanctuary for unbonded mounts is maintained by a dedicated team of handlers, caretakers, and specialized veterinarians, ensuring that every creature receives proper care and training.

- Stormtalons: Their perches are reinforced and spaced apart, allowing them to endure the gusts and storms they prefer.
- Aetherwings: Housed in elevated stalls with panoramic views, these nimble creatures require a sense of openness to remain comfortable.
- Drakehawks: Heavily reinforced platforms support these powerful and territorial creatures, with separate feeding areas to accommodate their substantial dietary needs.
- Skyblades: Compact, cliff-like perches provide security while maintaining their agility.

The Sky Stables also connect to launch platforms, which extend outward from the tower. These platforms are used for training flights, formation drills, and tactical exercises, ensuring that both mounts and riders are prepared for active duty. During these sessions, the skies above Aru’Mas are alive with the movements of the city’s aerial guardians.

## The Barracks of Aspiring Skywardens

The lower levels of the Roost serve as barracks for new recruits. These aspiring Skywardens, a mix of young hopefuls and experienced soldiers from the [[Sunspear Legion]], undergo rigorous training to earn their place among the elite. Life in the barracks is demanding, with recruits adhering to a strict regimen of physical training, aerial maneuvers, and combat strategy lessons. Each day consists of endurance exercises, flight drills, and lectures on aerial combat tactics, animal bonding, and Skywarden traditions. Meditation and reflection are encouraged to hone mental discipline and strengthen the rider’s connection with their future mount.

## The Ritual of Bonding

The Ritual of Bonding is a sacred tradition that solidifies the partnership between rider and mount. Conducted in a dedicated chamber within the Roost, the ritual is illuminated by soft, glowing crystals and adorned with tapestries depicting legendary Skywardens and their mounts. During the ceremony, the recruit presents a personal token, often a feather or item symbolizing their connection to their chosen mount. Once alone together, rider and mount establish their bond in a silent, instinctual exchange that transcends language. Only when this deep connection is recognized does the mount accept the recruit as its rider. After the ritual, the newly bonded pair is assigned a Mentor Skywarden, an experienced rider who guides them through advanced training, ensuring they develop into a cohesive and formidable unit.

## Daily Life and Training

A day in the Roost begins at dawn with aerial drills. Recruits practice formations, emergency landings, and tactical maneuvers under the supervision of veteran Skywardens. Training includes combat simulations, reconnaissance exercises, and endurance challenges, preparing both rider and mount for real-world missions. Meals are taken in the shared mess hall, where recruits, mentors, and seasoned Skywardens gather. Evenings are spent in study, reflection, or informal gatherings, where recruits share insights and experiences. These moments foster the unity and resilience that define the Skywardens.

## The Roost’s Role in Aru’Mas

To the citizens of Aru’Mas, the Roost is more than a military stronghold; it is a symbol of protection and honor. Skywardens are a source of inspiration, with their aerial displays during festivals drawing awe and admiration. These performances, showcasing daring maneuvers and aerial formations, celebrate the unity between mount and rider and pay homage to the people they safeguard. Beyond ceremonial displays, the Roost plays a vital role in city defense, reconnaissance, and emergency response. Skywardens patrol the skies, ensuring the safety of Aru’Mas from external threats, smuggling operations, or potential spirit incursions. Their swift response capabilities make them an essential force in times of crisis.

## Legends of the Roost

The Roost is steeped in tales of legendary Skywardens and their mounts. Stories such as Alden the Steadfast and his unbreakable bond with the Drakehawk Ironwing, or Lira the Swift and her Skyblade Windwhisper, inspire future generations. These legends reinforce the values of dedication, loyalty, and skill that define the Skywardens.

## A Place of Growth, Challenge, and Legacy

For recruits, veterans, and citizens alike, the Roost represents transformation, courage, and commitment. Each bond between rider and mount is a testament to patience, empathy, and trust, ensuring that the skies of Aru’Mas remain vigilantly watched over. The sight of the Roost against the skyline, with wings launching from its platforms, serves as a constant reminder of the boundless spirit of Aru’Mas—a beacon of unity between sky and earth, guardian and people. ..
