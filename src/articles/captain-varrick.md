# Captain Varrick

Captain Varrick the Undaunted is a vengeful Once-Was spirit bound to the Harbor District of Aru’Mas. Wrongfully executed for treason centuries ago, his death was marked by injustice and betrayal, leaving his spirit restless. Now, Varrick haunts the harbor, watching over sailors and ships, delivering retribution to those who perpetuate acts of betrayal or corruption near the docks. To many, he is a dark yet necessary figure, embodying the cost of loyalty and the weight of justice.

## Varrick’s Life and Death

Captain Varrick was a respected sailor and merchant who served the city of Aru’Mas with distinction. Known for his daring voyages and unflinching loyalty, Varrick transported goods and passengers across treacherous seas and astral routes. His reputation was impeccable—until a powerful rival merchant accused him of treason. The charges, fabricated to eliminate Varrick as competition, claimed he had colluded with pirates to attack city vessels.

Despite his pleas of innocence and the lack of evidence, Varrick was condemned to death by hanging. His ship, The Tempest’s Call, was scuttled in the harbor before his eyes, a final act of humiliation. His last words, spoken with unyielding defiance, were a curse on those who had wronged him and a vow to protect the harbor he loved from corruption. When his body was cut down, it disappeared into the waves, and his restless spirit began to linger.

## Manifestation

Captain Varrick’s spectral form is imposing, a shadowy figure that reflects the dignity and fury of his final moments.

- Appearance: Varrick appears as a tall man cloaked in a spectral captain’s coat. His face is obscured, shrouded in shadow, but faint, glowing eyes peer out with a piercing intensity. His lower body fades into a rippling mist that resembles ocean waves.
- Sound and Presence: His arrival is often heralded by the eerie creaking of ship rigging, the crash of unseen waves, or the mournful tolling of bells. Sailors describe an oppressive weight in the air when he is near, as though the harbor itself holds its breath.
- His Ghostly Ship: On stormy nights, a faint image of The Tempest’s Call can be seen drifting in the harbor, its masts swaying in the windless dark. The ship is said to vanish the moment it is approached.

## Behavior and Purpose

Captain Varrick remains in the harbor not out of sorrow, but to guard the integrity of the city’s trade and the lives of those who work the seas. His spirit is both a protector and an avenger, acting against acts of treachery while silently guiding honest sailors to safety.

## Protector of Sailors

Varrick is known to shield ships from harm in moments of great peril. Sailors caught in sudden storms often speak of a shadowy figure on the waves guiding them back to safety, while their ships are surrounded by an inexplicable calm. These interventions are rare but unforgettable, often interpreted as Varrick honoring those who conduct themselves with honor and loyalty.

## Avenger of Betrayal

Conversely, Varrick is merciless toward those who commit treachery in the harbor. Acts of piracy, smuggling, and corruption are said to provoke his wrath, manifesting as sudden misfortunes:

- Ships mysteriously sinking at anchor
- Crew members plagued by unsettling dreams of drowning
- Cargo spoiled or inexplicably lost to the depths Those who persist in such acts risk seeing Varrick himself, his glowing eyes boring into them as he whispers warnings of doom.

## Restless Wanderer

When not actively intervening, Varrick drifts silently through the harbor, watching over the bustling activity. His presence is a constant reminder of the city’s past mistakes and the cost of greed and injustice.

## Cultural and Spiritual Significance

## Symbol of Justice

Varrick’s story has become a cautionary tale about the dangers of corruption and the importance of fairness. Sailors and merchants alike invoke his name to remind themselves to conduct business honestly, saying, “May Captain Varrick keep you honest.”

## Offerings to the Captain

To honor or appease Varrick, sailors often leave offerings at the water’s edge:

- Rum or wine: Poured into the sea as a gesture of respect.
- Coins: Tossed into the harbor as an offering for safe passage.
- Lanterns: Lit on the docks in his memory, particularly on stormy nights.

## The Harbor’s Guardian

Despite his vengeful nature, Varrick is viewed as a protector of the harbor. His name is spoken with reverence, and his presence is seen as both a warning and a blessing. Many believe that as long as he lingers, Aru’Mas’s harbor will remain prosperous and safe from calamity.

## Legends and Tales

## The Ghostly Warning

One famous tale tells of a merchant captain who planned to betray her crew and sell them into slavery. On the eve of her plan, she was visited by Varrick, who appeared in her quarters and spoke a single word: “Remember.” Shaken, she confessed her plot to the crew and begged for forgiveness. That night, the storm that had threatened to capsize their ship suddenly abated, and they reached the harbor safely.

## The Lost Cargo

In another story, a smuggler’s ship vanished without a trace, its crew and cargo swallowed by the sea. Days later, crates bearing the smuggler’s markings washed ashore, each one empty but for a single gold coin engraved with a crude image of a ship sinking beneath the waves. To this day, it is said that anyone who finds one of these coins is marked by Varrick’s curse.

## Superstitions and Practices

## Avoiding Varrick’s Wrath

To avoid provoking the captain’s spirit, sailors adhere to several customs:

- No Treachery in the Harbor: Betrayals and underhanded dealings are believed to summon Varrick’s ire.
- Respect the Waves: Disrespecting the sea, such as polluting the water or mocking spirits, is thought to anger him.
- Honoring Agreements: Keeping promises, particularly those made at sea, is seen as essential to earning Varrick’s favor.

## The Lantern’s Glow

Dockworkers sometimes claim to see a faint lantern light moving across the harbor at night. This is interpreted as a sign that Varrick is patrolling the waters. Many take it as a good omen, believing it signifies that the harbor is under his watchful eye.

## In Summary: The Undying Captain

Captain Varrick the Undaunted is both a relic of Aru’Mas’s turbulent history and a spectral figure of immense cultural significance. His unyielding presence in the harbor is a reminder of the cost of greed and betrayal, a warning to those who would defy justice, and a quiet protector for those who live and work by the sea.

To the people of Aru’Mas, Varrick’s legacy is complex: a ghost of vengeance and honor, whose undying watch ensures that the harbor remains a place of prosperity—and a place where wrongs are never forgotten.
