# The Ethereal

The Ethereal is not traveled to. It is passed through.

Unlike the [[The Astral Sea|Astral Sea]], which is a medium with navigable distance and documentable hazards, or [[The Dreaming]], which is a destination accessible through sleep, the Ethereal is more accurately described as a condition of transition — the between-space that exists at the edges of all things, encountered not by seeking it out but by moving from one state of existence into another.

## What It Is

Aru'Mas scholars have produced more definitions of the Ethereal than any other plane in the cosmological record. This is partly because it is difficult to study directly. It is partly because it keeps appearing where it is not expected.

The most durable description — attributed to the planar philosopher Arrin Vast and appearing in derivation throughout subsequent scholarship — holds that the Ethereal is the membrane of reality: the thin and dimensionless boundary between the material world and everything that is not it. It has no interior geography in the conventional sense. When one is in the Ethereal, one is between — between the physical and the immaterial, between waking and dreaming, between the present arrangement of things and what is adjacent to it.

This means the Ethereal appears at transitions. The [[Shattered Strand]], where the [[Faded Veil]] presses closest to the material surface and the two realities thin against each other, sits at an Ethereal boundary — the plane makes itself felt wherever the material world's integrity weakens. The brief disorientation reported by travelers using [[Tetherjumps|Tetherjump]] pylons — described in multiple accounts as passing through "a veil of liquid light" with "the edges of reality blurring" — is consistent with a momentary Ethereal transit, though Aru'Mas scholars are divided on whether that interpretation is correct.

## Spirits and the Ethereal

The Ethereal is significant to spirit scholars primarily because it appears to be the medium through which spirits move between their native context and the material world. A spirit of the deep earth does not simply appear on the surface of Ahvantir; it passes through the Ethereal to do so, or through something functionally indistinguishable from it. This is inference more than documented fact — spirits have not generally provided detailed accounts of how they cross.

What is documented is that during the [[History of Ahvantir|Divine War]], Ahvantir's native spirits moved between the material and immaterial sides of the battlefield with a speed and fluidity that divine forces could not easily counter. Their capacity to engage and disengage from physical reality was described by observers as one of the defining tactical realities of the conflict. That this ability involved the Ethereal — or relied on the same properties the Ethereal describes — is the working interpretation of most contemporary planar scholars.

## Unknown Properties

Whether the Ethereal has permanent residents — entities that do not merely pass through it but inhabit its between-space — is not established. Certain reported encounters with entities that could not be placed within any known spirit taxonomy, and that seemed to persist at locations where material-to-immaterial transitions occur frequently, have been interpreted by some scholars as evidence of Ethereal inhabitants. None of these encounters have been rigorously documented.
