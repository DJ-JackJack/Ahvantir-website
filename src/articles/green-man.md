# Green Man

The Green Man is a mighty forest deity and Warden of the [[Neverwood]], the dense and mystical forest that lies between Aru’Mas and the [[Cascades]]. He is one of the most prominent Wardens in Ahvantir, known for his deep control over the forest’s plant life and the strong ties he holds with the spirits and creatures within his domain. Often depicted as a man-like figure made of leaves, vines, and bark, the Green Man embodies both the nurturing and untamed aspects of nature.

While he is primarily considered a benevolent spirit, the Green Man is also a trickster, using illusions, shifting paths, and misdirection to confound those who disrespect the ancient ways or fail to offer proper tributes upon entering the Neverwood.

## Appearance and Presence

The Green Man’s form is ethereal, taking on the shape of a humanoid figure composed of the forest’s elements. His body is woven from mossy bark, leaves, and vines, with antlers often sprouting from his head like the branches of an ancient oak. His eyes glow with a soft, emerald light, suggesting both wisdom and a hint of mischief.

The Green Man’s presence is not always physical; it often manifests as a rustling in the leaves, the sudden bloom of flowers, or a gentle breeze carrying laughter. Those who enter the Neverwood with respect might see glimpses of him in the corner of their eye, while those who trespass or desecrate the land might feel a more foreboding presence, with shadows lengthening and paths twisting into confusion.

## Personality and Morality

The Green Man is complex, embodying the dual nature of the wild—he is both a nurturing guardian and a playful deceiver. His behavior varies greatly depending on the situation and the attitudes of those who enter his domain:

## 1. Kind and Protective

- To those who honor the spirits and show respect for the natural world, the Green Man is a benevolent protector. He often aids travelers who have become lost in the forest, guiding them back to safety through animal messengers, sudden openings in the underbrush, or even appearing briefly to offer guidance.
- The Green Man is known to bless healers, hunters, and herbalists who honor the balance of the Neverwood, providing them with rare herbs, guiding animals to their snares, or offering drops of sap with healing properties. He encourages the growth of new life, urging the forest’s flora and fauna to thrive.
- At the heart of his nature lies a deep respect for the balance of life—those who seek to live in harmony with the forest’s cycles find him to be an ally and a source of wisdom.

## 2. Mischievous and Capricious

- The Green Man’s mischievous nature comes to the forefront when he encounters arrogance, disrespect, or carelessness within his domain. He is known to play pranks on travelers who fail to leave offerings, from shifting trails that loop endlessly to causing supplies to mysteriously go missing or become unusable.
- His trickster aspect is not meant to harm but to teach lessons about humility and reverence for the land. He enjoys testing the patience of travelers, creating phantom voices that call out to them or causing paths to lead back to the same tree repeatedly until a proper offering is made.
- In extreme cases, when faced with defilement or severe breaches of pacts, the Green Man can become wrathful. He may summon aggressive vines, unleash swarms of insects, or animate the trees themselves to repel the offender.

## 3. Keeper of the Forest’s Laws

- As the Warden of the Neverwood, the Green Man enforces the spiritual laws that govern his Claim. He upholds the Old Pacts with a sense of duty, ensuring that both the forest and its spirits are respected. Those who break pacts within the Neverwood face the Green Man’s judgment, which can range from forced atonement rituals to the declaration of a Blood Debt.
- The Green Man’s punishments are often tailored to the offense and offender. A careless logger might be overwhelmed by rapidly growing brambles that snag their tools and clothing, while a poacher might find themselves surrounded by the watchful eyes of wolves and owls until they leave the forest.

## Offerings to the Green Man

Entering the Neverwood without making an offering to the Green Man is seen as a grave insult to the Warden and the spirits of the forest. Common offerings are left at the forest’s edge or at shrine-trees scattered throughout the Neverwood, marked with carvings of leaves and antlers.

## 1. Accepted Offerings

- Honey: The sweetness of honey symbolizes the harmony of life and is among the most favored offerings of the Green Man.
- Sweet Cakes: Baked goods, especially those made from the wild fruits of the forest, are considered symbols of abundance and goodwill.
- Animal Bones: Hunters and trappers often leave the bones of their kills, symbolizing respect for the cycle of life and death within the forest.
- Well-Worn Garments: These offerings symbolize the journey and toil of the person making the offering. The Green Man values the experience and authenticity represented by worn clothing, seeing it as a sign of humility and a willingness to share a part of one’s life.

## 2. Rejected Offerings

- New Clothing: The Green Man finds the offering of new garments distasteful, associating it with vanity and insincerity. Those who leave new clothing as offerings often find their journey filled with minor inconveniences—misplaced items, tangled shoelaces, and delays caused by sudden overgrowth or curious animals that hinder progress.
- Artificial or Mass-Produced Goods: The Green Man only values offerings that hold genuine meaning or connection to the giver. Artificial or mass-produced items are seen as lacking spiritual value and often result in confusion, lost paths, or other playful pranks as a sign of disapproval.

## Legends and Tales of the Green Man

The Green Man is one of the most storied [[Spirits of Ahvantir|spirits of Ahvantir]], featuring prominently in the folklore of the Neverwood. His legend is shaped by both the benevolent and trickster aspects of his personality, making him a beloved and feared figure among the people of Aru’Mas and the surrounding lands.

## 1. The Friend of Lost Children

- One of the most famous tales tells of how the Green Man is especially protective of lost children. It is said that if a child becomes lost in the Neverwood and cries out in fear, the Green Man will appear to comfort them, often taking the form of a friendly animal or a glowing spirit that guides them safely back to the edge of the forest.

## 2. The Punishment of the Arrogant Hunter

- Another popular tale is that of an arrogant hunter who claimed he could conquer the forest without respecting its laws. Entering without an offering, the hunter was quickly surrounded by vines that trapped his legs, while the underbrush grew thick and impassable around him. The Green Man appeared as a shadowy figure among the trees, laughing softly as the hunter was forced to leave his quarry behind and retreat empty-handed.

## 3. The Keeper of the Heart’s Wisdom

- It is said that the Green Man holds the wisdom of the forest and can offer profound insights to those who reach the Heart of the Neverwood, the spiritual center of his Claim. Many believe that seeking the Green Man’s counsel here can reveal hidden truths, heal emotional wounds, or provide guidance on difficult life decisions.

## Interactions with Mortals

The Green Man is a popular figure among the people of Ahvantir, especially healers, herbalists, hunters, and pilgrims seeking spiritual guidance:

## 1. Favored by Healers and Herbalists

- Healers often venture into the Neverwood to collect rare herbs, leaving offerings in exchange for the Green Man’s guidance. The Warden is known to favor those who heal with natural remedies, leading them to hidden groves where magical plants grow.
- In return, the Green Man sometimes imparts knowledge of secret remedies or enhances the potency of herbs gathered in his forest, making him a revered figure among practitioners of natural magic.

## 2. Hunters and Gatherers

- Hunters and gatherers who respect the forest’s balance often find themselves favored by the Green Man. He blesses their traps, guides animals toward them, and ensures that the bounty of the forest is plentiful for those who honor its cycles.
- Those who hunt recklessly or take more than they need, however, quickly find their luck turning. Game becomes scarce, traps mysteriously break, and even basic directions within the forest become confusing.

## 3. Pilgrims and Spiritual Seekers

- Spiritual seekers often visit the Neverwood to make pacts with the Green Man, seeking his blessing for personal growth, protection, or guidance on spiritual matters. Pilgrims often spend days in meditation within the forest, leaving offerings and waiting for a sign of approval from the Warden.
- The Green Man is also known to offer lessons through his mischief. Travelers who find themselves misled or confused are often encouraged to reflect on the lessons of humility, patience, and the need to honor nature as a force of both nurture and challenge.
