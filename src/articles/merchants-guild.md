# Merchants Guild

The Merchants’ Guild of Aru’Mas is a powerful organization that oversees the city’s commerce, trade, and economic policies, serving as one of the three pillars of the city’s government. As the sole entry point into the Ahvantir Archipelago, Aru’Mas is a vibrant hub of multiversal trade, making the Merchants’ Guild one of the most influential forces within the city’s political and social landscape. The guild ensures that trade flows smoothly and profitably, leveraging its power not only to enhance the city’s wealth but also to expand its connections across different realms.

The Merchants’ Guild is not only a commercial entity but a political faction with a seat on the ruling council of Aru’Mas, represented by the Grand Speaker, who acts as the voice of the city’s working class, traders, artisans, and entrepreneurs.

## Structure and Organization

The Merchants’ Guild is a well-structured and hierarchical organization, designed to balance the interests of different commercial sectors while maintaining overall control of the city’s economic affairs.

## 1. Grand Assembly

- The Grand Assembly is the highest governing body of the guild, composed of influential merchants, ship captains, artisans’ representatives, and trade magnates. The assembly meets quarterly to discuss major economic policies, vote on trade agreements, and set the guild’s strategic goals.
- The Grand Assembly also elects the Grand Speaker, who leads the guild and serves as its representative on the ruling council of Aru’Mas.

## 2. Guild Councils

- Below the Grand Assembly are the Guild Councils, which represent different commercial sectors, such as:
- Shipping and Trade Council: Oversees port operations, multiversal shipping routes, and the import/export of goods.
- Artisans’ Council: Represents craftspeople, including blacksmiths, weavers, alchemists, and other skilled artisans.
- Market Council: Manages the major markets and bazaars, ensuring fair trade practices and the regulation of commercial permits.
- Mercenary and Security Council: Handles the hiring of mercenaries and protection services for traders, ensuring safe transport of goods within the city and beyond its borders.
- These councils are responsible for managing day-to-day operations within their respective sectors, reporting to the Grand Speaker and the Grand Assembly regarding major issues or developments.

## 3. Local Representatives

- Each of the city’s districts has a Local Representative of the Merchants’ Guild, who acts as a liaison between local merchants and the central guild leadership. These representatives manage permits, mediate disputes, and ensure compliance with guild regulations.
- Local Representatives play a vital role in ensuring that the guild remains connected to the daily activities of Aru’Mas, maintaining both economic stability and the loyalty of the working class.

## Economic Influence

The Merchants’ Guild exerts significant influence over Aru’Mas’ economic landscape, not only managing the flow of goods and trade but also shaping the city’s political and social policies. The guild’s priorities often align with the goal of making Aru’Mas a prosperous and competitive multiversal trading hub.

## 1. Control of Trade Routes

- The guild controls the city’s access to major multiversal trade routes, negotiating with merchants from various realms to establish mutually beneficial trade agreements. These routes bring exotic goods, magical artifacts, and rare resources into Aru’Mas, making the city one of the most diverse marketplaces in the multiverse.
- The Merchants’ Guild is also responsible for port security and the regulation of ships entering and leaving the harbor, working closely with the [[Sunspear Legion]] to ensure that both legal and illicit trade are managed to the guild’s advantage.

## 2. Regulation of Markets

- The Merchants’ Guild regulates the bazaars and markets of Aru’Mas, ensuring fair trade practices and preventing monopolies or price manipulation that could destabilize the economy. The guild sets guidelines for taxation, tariffs, and trade permits, balancing the need for revenue with the goal of maintaining a competitive market.
- The Grand Bazaar of Aru’Mas is a central hub for commerce, where merchants from across realms sell their goods. The bazaar is known for its vibrant mix of exotic wares, magical items, and rare artifacts, all of which are subject to the guild’s oversight.

## 3. Mercenary Contracts and Security

- Given the dangerous nature of multiversal trade, the Merchants’ Guild has a dedicated Mercenary and Security Council that handles the hiring of mercenaries, guards, and bodyguards for both traders and cargo. These contracts are often lucrative, attracting adventurers and mercenaries seeking work within the city.
- The guild maintains a cooperative relationship with the [[Order of Valor]] and the [[Runeguard]], ensuring that security measures align with the city’s broader defense strategies, particularly in the face of potential threats from rogue spirits, hostile factions, or multiversal bandits.

## [[Grand Speaker Artrice Vanderbane]]

The current Grand Speaker of the Merchants’ Guild is Artrice Vanderbane, a shrewd and ambitious leader known for her sharp mind and strategic acumen. Artrice has a reputation as a deal-maker, adept at negotiating complex trade agreements and navigating the city’s intricate political landscape.

## Background

- Artrice rose to prominence within the guild through her success as a textile magnate, known for importing rare fabrics from distant realms and integrating them into the city’s fashion trends. Her business acumen earned her a seat on the Artisans’ Council, where she quickly gained a reputation for bold decisions and innovative trade strategies.
- Elected as Grand Speaker after successfully brokering a major trade agreement that opened new markets to Aru’Mas, Artrice is respected for her ability to balance the interests of the guild’s various factions while advancing the city’s economic goals.

## Leadership Style

- Artrice’s leadership style is pragmatic, decisive, and adaptable. She is not afraid to take calculated risks, viewing the multiversal market as both an opportunity and a challenge to be conquered. She is known for her ability to forge alliances with other power brokers within the city, leveraging her position to expand Aru’Mas’ influence across trade networks.
- Artrice is skilled at maintaining unity within the guild, managing internal disputes efficiently while promoting a vision of economic growth and prosperity for the city as a whole. Her strategies often focus on innovation, encouraging artisans and traders to develop unique goods and services that can compete in the broader multiversal market.

## Interactions with Key Figures

- King [[Voren Ilderas]]: Artrice maintains a professional and cooperative relationship with King Voren, recognizing the need to align the guild’s interests with the city’s broader policies. While Voren’s cautious approach to governance sometimes conflicts with Artrice’s more aggressive economic strategies, the two share a mutual respect for each other’s roles in maintaining Aru’Mas’ stability and prosperity.
- [[High Priest Alabaster Sayn]]: Artrice and High Priest Alabaster Sayn have a complex relationship, characterized by a mix of diplomatic cooperation and occasional tension. While Artrice supports many of the church’s outreach programs and healing services, she is wary of any spiritual policies that could restrict trade or disrupt market activities. The two often negotiate compromises, with Artrice pushing for economic pragmatism and Alabaster advocating for spiritual balance.
- [[Scholar Princess|Princess Lira]] Ilderas: Artrice has a respectful but wary relationship with Princess Lira, whose focus on spiritual diplomacy and arcane research often contrasts with Artrice’s emphasis on trade and economic expansion. While Lira’s scholarly approach has occasionally aided the guild in securing magical goods or resources, Artrice is cautious about Lira’s influence over magical policy, especially when it intersects with trade regulations.
- [[Heir Apparent|Crown Prince Kaelen]] Ilderas: Artrice’s interactions with Kaelen are often defined by their shared interest in expansion and strength. Kaelen’s ambition for military growth aligns with Artrice’s vision of a dominant trade network, making them potential allies when it comes to securing trade routes or expanding the city’s influence. However, Artrice is careful to maintain her independence, wary of Kaelen’s more aggressive tendencies that could disrupt the guild’s delicate balance.

## Functions and Services

The Merchants’ Guild not only manages the city’s economy but also provides a variety of services and benefits to its members and the wider population of Aru’Mas.

## 1. Trade Permits and Licenses

- The guild issues trade permits and business licenses, regulating commercial activities within the city. It ensures that merchants comply with guild standards, pay appropriate taxes, and adhere to the city’s trade laws.

## 2. Financial Services

- The Merchants’ Guild operates a central Bank of Aru’Mas, offering financial services such as loans, investments, and currency exchange. This bank facilitates multiversal trade by allowing merchants to conduct transactions with diverse currencies from other realms.

## 3. Dispute Resolution

- The guild provides mediation and arbitration services for commercial disputes, ensuring that conflicts are resolved efficiently and fairly. These services are available to both guild members and independent merchants, reinforcing the guild’s role as an impartial economic regulator.

## 4. Trade Fairs and Events

- The Merchants’ Guild organizes regular trade fairs, exhibitions, and special market events that attract traders and buyers from across the multiverse. These events are not only economic opportunities but also serve as cultural showcases that highlight the diverse goods, magical artifacts, and exotic wares available in Aru’Mas.
- Major trade events include:
- The Grand Market Festival: Held annually, this is a week-long celebration where the Grand Bazaar transforms into a bustling hub of commerce and entertainment. The festival features rare items, auctioned artifacts, and multiversal delicacies, drawing merchants, adventurers, and dignitaries from other realms.
- The Artisan's Fair: A seasonal event that focuses on handcrafted goods, alchemical innovations, and magical inventions produced by the city’s artisans. The fair encourages collaboration between the Artisans’ Council and [[Order of Wisdom]], promoting unique creations that showcase Aru’Mas’ magical craftsmanship.
- The Mercantile Gala: A more exclusive event, where high-profile traders, guild leaders, and influential citizens gather to network and negotiate lucrative deals. The gala is known for its opulence, guarded by mercenaries from the Order of Valor to ensure the safety of rare and valuable goods on display.

## Political Role and Influence

As one of the three ruling bodies in Aru’Mas, the Merchants’ Guild plays a vital role not only in economic policy but in shaping the political landscape of the city. Its influence extends beyond commerce, affecting laws, social policies, and diplomatic relations.

## 1. Seat on the Ruling Council

- The Grand Speaker, currently Artrice Vanderbane, holds a permanent seat on the ruling council of Aru’Mas, representing the guild and its interests. This position allows the guild to directly influence key decisions, such as trade regulations, economic development, and even matters of diplomatic policy.
- The guild uses its political clout to advocate for laws that favor commerce, such as relaxed tariffs, multiversal trade agreements, and policies that encourage entrepreneurship. It also works to maintain economic stability, particularly during crises that might affect trade routes or market confidence.

## 2. Diplomacy and Trade Relations

- The guild serves as Aru’Mas’ primary diplomatic arm in multiversal trade negotiations, often leading talks with foreign traders, trade consulates, and dignitaries. These negotiations are critical to maintaining the city’s position as a premier trading hub and ensuring that the guild remains profitable.
- Artrice Vanderbane’s diplomatic skills are particularly valuable in this area; she is known for her ability to secure advantageous trade deals and build long-lasting relationships with key economic partners. Her approach is characterized by a blend of strategic compromise and assertive bargaining, which has solidified the guild’s reputation for reliability and success.

## 3. Balancing Power and Pacts

- While the Merchants’ Guild is focused on economic growth, it must also navigate the Old Pacts that define the city’s spiritual boundaries and relationships with the [[Spirits of Ahvantir|spirits of Ahvantir]]. The guild must be cautious not to disrupt these pacts through its activities, as spiritual repercussions could threaten the city’s stability.
- The guild often collaborates with [[The Church of the Threefold Path|the Church of the Threefold Path]] and the Runeguard to ensure that its actions do not violate spiritual laws. This collaboration is vital for maintaining harmony, as the guild’s expansionist tendencies occasionally clash with the more cautious spiritual policies advocated by the church.

## Challenges and Controversies

The Merchants’ Guild, despite its wealth and influence, is not without its challenges and controversies. As an organization that prioritizes profit and commerce, it sometimes faces criticism from other factions within the city.

## 1. Allegations of Corruption

- The guild has faced accusations of corruption and monopolistic practices, particularly when it comes to controlling trade permits and enforcing regulations. Some merchants believe that the guild’s leadership favors certain factions or sectors, resulting in uneven competition.
- Artrice Vanderbane has worked to improve the guild’s transparency, implementing new policies for permit allocation and encouraging open-market competition. However, suspicions of favoritism and backdoor deals persist, especially among smaller traders who struggle to compete with larger guild-backed enterprises.

## 2. Conflict with the Adventurers’ Guilds

- The Merchants’ Guild often finds itself at odds with the adventurers’ guilds, whose members sometimes operate independently of the guild’s regulations. Adventurers, known for smuggling rare artifacts or bypassing trade permits, can disrupt the guild’s control over high-value goods and magical items.
- Despite these conflicts, the guild recognizes the value of adventurers in securing trade routes and procuring rare goods from Ahvantir’s wilds. The relationship is one of pragmatic cooperation, with the guild occasionally sponsoring adventurers in exchange for exclusive trade rights or rare items.

## 3. Spiritual Resistance

- The guild’s aggressive expansionist policies occasionally provoke resistance from spiritual entities tied to the Old Pacts. Spirits of trade routes or specific market districts have been known to withdraw their favor or cause disruptions when they believe the guild’s activities threaten the balance of the city’s spiritual landscape.
- To address this, the guild often consults with spiritual mediators from the Church of the Threefold Path, particularly the [[Order of Harmony]], to negotiate with offended spirits and maintain a delicate balance between commerce and spirituality.
