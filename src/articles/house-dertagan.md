# House Der'Tagan

The Iron Mantle of [[Aru'Mas]]

Motto: “Honor in Discipline, Strength in Unity”
Species: Orcs (High-Orcs)
District Influence: [[Stonegate]], [[Luminous Reach]]
Allied Orders: [[Order of Valor]], [[Sunspear Legion]]

## Overview

House Der'Tagan is one of the six noble lineages descending from the companions of [[Marduk Sunspear]]. Formed from a cadre of high-orc warriors known for their tactical brilliance and ironclad discipline, the house remains synonymous with the defense and order of Aru’Mas to this day.

Nicknamed “The Iron Mantle,” their name evokes both armor and burden—the weight of duty worn proudly. The house maintains a deep and formal relationship with the Order of Valor, and their blood runs thick in the ranks of the [[Sunspear Legion]]. In the city’s collective imagination, Der'Tagan represents the shield that holds firm when all else breaks.

## Political Role

Der'Tagan voices are commanding presences in the Council of Aru'Mas, particularly during debates over civic security, military funding, and law enforcement jurisdiction. Their vote carries influence not through subtlety or trade manipulation but through their stature as protectors of the realm.

They are key contributors to operations in the Luminous Reach and Stonegate districts, where their officers oversee not only patrols and Skywarden rotations but also the training of civic guardians. Though they rarely entertain philosophical matters, their approval is often a requirement for the passage of military or defensive reforms.

## Reputation and Public Perception

Among the populace, House Der'Tagan is seen as a double-edged blade—comforting in times of danger, yet daunting in peacetime. They are admired for their unwavering sense of justice and incorruptibility, but some citizens quietly chafe under their firm, hierarchical worldview.

In poorer districts like [[The Spillway|the Spillway]], the Der'Tagan uniform often evokes fear rather than safety. Yet even there, many acknowledge that when true danger arises, it is the Iron Mantle who stands their ground while others flee.

## Customs and Doctrine

The children of Der'Tagan are raised in strict regimens of mental and physical discipline. Emotional control, tactical thinking, and personal sacrifice are core values instilled from youth. Internal duels of honor—ceremonial and closely governed—are still practiced to resolve disputes and test merit.

They maintain ancestral shrines within their stronghold and honor their dead with public rituals of remembrance. Although spiritual diplomacy is largely left to other houses, Der'Tagan often seeks counsel from the [[Order of Wisdom]] before pursuing action that may upset the city’s spiritual balance.

## Legacy and Holdings

The Der'Tagan compound in Stonegate is as much a fortress as a noble residence. It includes barracks for elite Sunspear troops, a dueling ground open to the public for major trials, and classrooms where promising civilians are sometimes trained in martial arts and civic discipline.

Beyond that, they maintain a close grip on Aru’Mas’ defensive infrastructure, including several strategic towers along the city’s outer walls and a network of signal posts whose origins trace back to field outposts the Der’Tagan ancestors built during the Landing War — temporary fortifications that were absorbed into the city’s permanent defensive infrastructure as Aru’Mas grew around them.

## Notable Members

- Warden-General Rhazgor Der'Tagan – Head of house and seasoned commander. His voice commands silence in the Council; his presence calms entire battalions.
- Captain Yelra Der'Tagan – Leader of the Skywarden division in Luminous Reach. Efficient, razor-focused, and rumored to have once turned down a generalship to remain airborne.
- Sergeant-Tutor Bruknall Der'Tagan – Veteran drillmaster of both noble heirs and elite adventurers. Said to be able to tell a recruit’s flaws within three strides.

## Cultural Symbolism

The crest of House Der'Tagan displays a bronze gauntlet grasping a vertical spear, superimposed on a fortified city gate. Their colors—dark crimson, steel gray, and bronze—symbolize sacrifice, vigilance, and unyielding defense. The phrase “Stand, though the storm breaks all” is often whispered in the training yards, though it is not their official motto. ..
