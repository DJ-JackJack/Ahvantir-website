# Abrax

In the magical lands of Ahvantir, where spirits drift through trees and rivers, there is one very special spirit—a loyal, friendly dog named Abrax. Abrax was once a big, scruffy hound with fur as soft as a cloudy night and kind, warm eyes. He belonged to a family who loved him dearly, and he was especially fond of their youngest child, a little girl named Lira. She would wander off on little adventures, and Abrax was always there by her side, keeping her safe.

One day, while chasing butterflies, Lira wandered too far into the forest and got lost! As the shadows grew longer, she felt scared and began to cry for help. Just then, Abrax appeared, bounding through the trees to her side. He licked her hand, lay close to her, and kept her warm until the morning. At dawn, he guided her safely home. Since then, it’s said that Abrax became a guardian spirit, watching over any lost or frightened child, offering comfort and guiding them home.

## How Abrax Helps Lost Children

Abrax is a friendly, gentle spirit who appears as a big, warm dog with a soft, fluffy coat and a wagging tail. When children get lost or feel scared, he finds them and keeps them company, his presence calm and comforting. He’ll nudge them gently with his nose, let them lean on him if they’re tired, and guard them against any dangers that might be nearby. If they’re too frightened to move, he’ll simply lie down beside them, letting them rest, until morning light or help arrives.

When the time comes to lead them home, Abrax gently nudges them along, following paths known only to him. And when they reach safety, he gives one last wag of his tail and quietly disappears, leaving a lingering warmth and perhaps a little pawprint as a reminder of his visit.

## Some Stories of Abrax and the Lost Kids

The children of Ahvantir know many stories of Abrax, and each one is passed down like a comforting lullaby, reminding them that they are never truly alone in the woods.

- The Little Boy in the Rain: One stormy night, a young boy named Emris wandered away from his home. The rain made it hard to see, and Emris felt scared and alone. Just then, he felt a soft nose nudging his hand and saw a big, warm dog looking up at him, as if to say, “Don’t worry, I’m here.” Abrax stayed with Emris through the rain, nudging him gently along the way until he found himself safe in his village again. When he looked back to thank his new friend, all he saw were little pawprints in the mud.
- The Sleepy Twins: Mina and Milo, a pair of adventurous twins, were playing hide-and-seek at the edge of the forest when they wandered too far and couldn’t find their way back. As the night grew darker, they began to feel frightened. Suddenly, a big, scruffy dog with warm eyes and a wagging tail appeared. Abrax had found them! He gave them each a friendly lick, then led them on a winding path through the trees until they saw the glow of home. When they turned to thank him, Abrax had already slipped away, leaving only two little pawprints where he’d been.
- The Girl by the River: Mira, a curious girl who loved exploring the forest, once wandered too far and found herself by a swift river she didn’t recognize. She felt a little scared until she noticed a big, gentle dog beside her, his tail wagging as if to reassure her. Abrax sat by her side, keeping her company through the night. When the morning came, she saw her parents across the river. Abrax gave a soft bark, nudging her forward, and watched as her parents safely guided her home.

## Prayers and Offerings to Abrax

Though not a deity, Abrax has become a beloved spirit in Ahvantir, especially for parents who worry about their adventurous children. It’s said that Abrax watches over lost and frightened children like a loyal guardian, and many parents say a small prayer to him each night, asking, “Abrax, watch over them, and guide them back to me.” These prayers have become a part of life in Ahvantir, comforting parents who feel better knowing their children are in Abrax’s gentle care.

In the markets of Aru’Mas, small shops and stalls sell little dog figurines crafted from wood or clay, each carved to look like a scruffy, happy dog—just like Abrax. Parents buy these figurines as offerings, placing them on windowsills or carrying them when their children are away from home. Some even place a small figurine at the edge of the forest, believing that Abrax will see it and know he is needed.

## Amulets for Children

For children, the market stalls offer tiny amulets with carvings of a dog’s pawprint or the soft, watchful eyes of Abrax. These amulets are simple but cherished, tied onto little strings and worn like necklaces or bracelets. Children believe that wearing an Abrax amulet means they’ll be safe if they ever wander too far or feel scared. They see these amulets as a promise from Abrax, knowing that if they ever need him, he’ll be there with a wagging tail and a warm heart.

## A Blessing from Abrax

To comfort their children, many parents in Ahvantir have a gentle blessing they say, asking Abrax for his guidance and protection:

"Wherever you wander, wherever you roam,

May Abrax the Friendly bring you safely home.

When shadows grow long and you can’t find your way,

May the Guardian Dog come to light up your day.”

This blessing has become a lullaby for little ones, a reminder that if they ever find themselves lost or afraid, there’s a loyal friend watching over them. Abrax may be a spirit now, but to the people of Ahvantir, he is as real and as loved as any living creature. With each little figurine, amulet, and prayer, they thank him for his watchful presence, knowing that Abrax, the Guardian Hound, is always near.
