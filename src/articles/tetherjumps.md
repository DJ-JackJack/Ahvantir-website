# Tetherjumps

Tetherjumps are an innovative and magical form of rapid transportation in Aru’Mas, harnessing the city’s ancient ley line network to facilitate near-instantaneous travel between designated locations. These arcane pylons offer a fast, efficient, and thrilling way to traverse the city, serving as a vital component of the city’s infrastructure and a testament to its mastery of multiversal magic.

## Overview of Tetherjumps

The Tetherjump network consists of arcane pylons strategically placed across Aru’Mas, connecting major districts, key landmarks, and important government buildings. Each pylon is a tall, crystalline structure, infused with ley energy and covered in shifting runes that correspond to its connected destinations. At its core, each Tetherjump is powered by arcane sigils that tap into the ley lines running beneath the city, channeling this potent energy into teleportation magic.

These pylons allow users to “jump” from one point to another within seconds, offering seamless travel for officials, traders, guild members, and adventurers who need to move quickly between locations. The system is not only fast but also regulated, with built-in safety measures to prevent misuse and ensure only authorized individuals can access the more restricted pylons.

## How Tetherjumps Work

Using a Tetherjump is a simple but thrilling process that combines arcane technology, spiritual alignment, and a touch of the unexpected:

## 1. Activation and Connection

- To initiate a jump, travelers step onto a runic platform at the base of the Tetherjump pylon. The platform is a circular space inscribed with glowing runes that resonate with the city's ley energy. Travelers can feel a subtle hum of magic underfoot, a faint tingling that signals the pylon's connection to the ley lines.
- At the center of the platform stands a crystal terminal, which acts as the control interface for selecting the desired destination. Users interact with the terminal by placing their hand on its surface, which causes a display of holographic runes to appear, listing the available destinations.
- Authorized users, such as city officials, guild members, and those carrying the appropriate Tetherjump tokens, are able to access the system. Tokens are small, arcane-infused charms issued by the [[Runeguard]], encoded with a user’s identity and permissions.

## 2. Charging and Alignment

- Once the destination is selected, the pylon begins to charge, drawing arcane energy from the ley lines below. The runes on the platform start to glow brighter, shifting colors to match the energy signature of the target destination. This process, called alignment, typically takes only a few seconds but is crucial to ensure a safe and accurate teleportation.
- During alignment, a protective aura forms around the traveler, creating a temporary shield against the unpredictable effects of ley line energy. The air around the platform becomes charged, with sparks of blue and silver light flickering in the air, creating an atmosphere that is both electrifying and slightly unsettling.
- As the energy reaches its peak, a soft chime signals that the jump is ready.

## 3. The Jump

- When the jump is initiated, the traveler experiences a moment of weightlessness as their body is enveloped in a burst of ethereal light. The sensation is like being pulled gently through a narrow tunnel of shimmering energy, with the edges of reality blurring and shifting around them.
- Within seconds, the traveler reappears at their destination, landing on a corresponding runic platform at the target pylon. The entire process is disorienting but surprisingly smooth, akin to passing through a veil of liquid light.

## 4. Stabilization and Exit

- Upon arrival, there is a brief moment of disorientation as the traveler’s senses readjust to their new surroundings. The protective aura dissipates gradually, leaving behind a faint smell of ozone and a lingering tingle from the teleportation magic.
- The runic platform at the arrival point flashes briefly to confirm a successful transit, and the traveler is free to step off and continue their journey.

## Types of Tetherjumps and Access Levels

The Tetherjump network in Aru’Mas is divided into different types of pylons, each serving a specific function and audience:

## 1. Public Tetherjumps

- These pylons are accessible to the general public and are strategically located in major districts like the Far-Farewell Market, [[Heartspire]], and Guild Row.
- Public Tetherjumps are designed for convenience and high capacity, with larger runic platforms capable of handling groups of travelers. They are frequently used by traders, tourists, and residents looking to move quickly between the city’s bustling areas.
- Public pylons have more limited destinations, usually restricted to popular areas within the city. They operate with a small fee, often paid using multiversal currency or special tokens that can be purchased from kiosks near the pylons.

## 2. Restricted Tetherjumps

- These pylons are reserved for official use, accessible only to government officials, the Runeguard, and authorized guild leaders. They are located in sensitive areas like the Council Hall, Runeguard Citadel, and military barracks.
- Restricted Tetherjumps are equipped with additional security measures, including arcane wards, identity checks, and monitoring by the Runeguard. They allow for quick deployment of personnel to critical locations in times of emergency or diplomatic urgency.
- Access requires a special arcane token encoded with the user’s rank, purpose, and permissions, preventing unauthorized use.

## 3. Emergency Tetherjumps

- Designed for rapid evacuation or emergency response, these pylons are located in areas prone to danger, such as the [[Sunspear Legion]] barracks, the [[Order of Valor]] headquarters, and certain key outposts along the city’s borders.
- Emergency Tetherjumps can be activated by designated personnel and are designed for quick deployment of troops, medical evacuations, or urgent responses to spiritual breaches. They are equipped with powerful stabilizers to ensure safe transit even during magical anomalies.

## Tetherjump Tokens

To use the Tetherjump network, travelers need Tetherjump tokens, small, magical devices that grant access to the system. These tokens are personalized to the user, allowing for secure teleportation while preventing unauthorized access.

## Types of Tokens

- Public Tokens: Available for purchase at kiosks near public Tetherjumps, these tokens allow access to public pylons only. They have limited charges and expire after a set period.
- Official Tokens: Issued to government officials, members of the Runeguard, and guild leaders, these tokens allow for access to restricted and emergency pylons. They are encoded with the user’s identity and rank, making them highly secure and difficult to counterfeit.
- Adventurer Tokens: Granted to adventurers who have proven themselves within Aru’Mas, these tokens offer more flexibility, allowing access to both public and restricted Tetherjumps. They are often used by members of the city’s adventurers’ guilds and special expeditions authorized by the council.

## Public Perception and Cultural Significance

The Tetherjump network is both loved and feared by the people of Aru’Mas. It represents the city’s mastery of arcane technology, a symbol of its status as a multiversal hub capable of blending magic with practicality.

- Admired for Speed: For merchants, adventurers, and officials, the Tetherjumps are seen as a vital asset, allowing for quick travel that is unmatched by any other form of transportation. Merchants often use them to deliver goods swiftly, while adventurers appreciate the ability to reach distant guild halls or respond to sudden calls for aid.
- Feared for the Unknown: The experience of teleportation, though brief, is disorienting and sometimes unsettling. There are stories of rare glitches—instances where travelers arrive feeling as if they were briefly somewhere else during the jump, encountering strange whispers or visions. These occurrences are rare but have contributed to the mystique (and occasional fear) surrounding the Tetherjump system.
