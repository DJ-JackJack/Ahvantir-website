# Bloom of Ephemera

The Bloom of Ephemera is a rare and beautiful flower that appears only under specific mystical conditions tied to Ahvantir’s three moons—Miras, Toris, and Keltas. This delicate plant, highly prized for its magical potency and ethereal beauty, blooms only when the moons align in a particular phase, casting a unique blend of light that seems to coax the flower from the earth. This lunar event is rare, happening only a few times each year, and causes the Bloom to flower precisely at midnight and wither by dawn.

The Bloom’s petals are a luminescent blue-purple, shimmering like stardust in the moonlight, and its fragrance—wildly intoxicating and mysterious—has a reputation for drawing in even the most cautious travelers. Coveted by alchemists, spiritualists, and spellcasters, the Bloom of Ephemera is renowned for its unique properties, including the ability to amplify enchantments and induce visions. However, those who seek it must contend with its toxic defenses and the danger of its allure.

## Properties of the Bloom of Ephemera

The Bloom of Ephemera is revered not only for its aesthetic allure but also for its powerful mystical effects. However, its use and handling require expertise, caution, and often specific rituals to prevent its potentially dangerous side effects.

## 1. Enchantment Amplification

- Power Conduit: When used as a component in spells or alchemical processes, the Bloom of Ephemera acts as a powerful conduit, enhancing enchantment and illusion spells. Spells cast with components derived from the Bloom of Ephemera gain +1 to their spell save DC and a +1 bonus to any associated attack rolls.
- Enchantment Catalyst: Potions or oils infused with Bloom essence can enhance items with temporary enchantments that persist for a full lunar cycle. For example, an amulet steeped in Bloom essence might grant its wearer advantage on Charisma checks or resistance to psychic damage during this period.

## 2. Visions and Prophetic Insight

- Dream-Inducing Aroma: The Bloom emits a faint, intoxicating fragrance known to induce lucid dreams and visions in those who inhale it. Spiritualists and diviners often harvest the petals to prepare incense for enhancing divination spells and rituals. When burned, the incense can induce visions, revealing glimpses of future events, hidden truths, or insights into the spirit realm.
- Risk of Overexposure: Inhaling too much of the Bloom’s fragrance can cause confusion, disorientation, and a temporary inability to distinguish reality from illusion. Extended exposure has driven some to obsession, as they seek to prolong the visions or chase glimpses of the spirit world.

## 3. Toxic Defenses and Magical Warding

- Dangerous Toxins: The Bloom has natural toxins in its petals and stems that can cause hallucinations, nausea, and disorientation if ingested or absorbed through the skin. This effect has led to the flower being known as the “Dreamsnare,” as those exposed to its raw form may find themselves lost in a waking dream.
- Spirit Warding: The Bloom’s aura naturally wards off malevolent spirits, who avoid areas where it grows. Items infused with its essence, or those carrying dried blooms, are often protected from spirit possession or phantom influences, making it popular among travelers and spirit-sensitive individuals.

## Collection and Usage of the Bloom of Ephemera

Due to its ephemeral nature and dangerous properties, collecting and using the Bloom of Ephemera requires skill, reverence, and great care.

## 1. Ritual of Harvesting

- Lunar Alignment: The Bloom of Ephemera appears only when all three moons—Miras, Toris, and Keltas—align in a specific phase, casting a unique light that draws the flower to bloom. This alignment is a rare event, occurring only a few times each year. The Bloom appears at midnight, when the combined lunar light is strongest, and withers by dawn, leaving no trace.
- Protective Charms: Due to the flower’s intoxicating and toxic effects, gatherers wear protective amulets or charms with protective runes. Gloves and masks are essential to avoid hallucinations caused by direct contact, and experienced gatherers often work in pairs for safety.
- Offerings and Blessings: Harvesters often leave small tokens or offerings—like a vial of [[Radiant Moonglow]] or an iron coin—as a sign of respect to the spirits of the area. This practice is believed to reduce the risks associated with gathering the Bloom.

## 2. Preparation for Use

- Essence Extraction: Alchemists first distill the Bloom’s essence, removing its toxins to create a purified substance for potions, incense, and enchantments. Raw petals are extremely potent, and even alchemists avoid handling them without proper preparation.
- Controlled Infusion: Infusions of the Bloom essence are kept small to prevent chaotic magical reactions. With careful preparation, its essence can safely enhance an item’s enchantments for a month, after which the effects fade with the next alignment of the moons.

## 3. Cultural Reverence and Taboos

- Sacred Status and Danger: The Bloom of Ephemera is revered for its beauty and power but regarded with caution. Local legends warn against harvesting the Bloom without permission from a shaman or spiritualist, as improper handling could anger the spirits and bring misfortune.
- Ritual Blessings: Spiritualists who work with the Bloom often perform blessings before each use, offering incantations or tokens to honor the spirits of the land. These practices are believed to mitigate the dangers of the Bloom and maintain harmony with Ahvantir’s natural magic.
