# Once-Was

Once-Was Spirits are spectral echoes of beings—human, animal, or otherwise—who died in violent, emotional, or unexpected ways. Unlike the powerful Wardens or the watchful [[Hearth Spirits]], Once-Was Spirits are weaker, yet they remain deeply tied to the circumstances of their death. These spirits linger as fragments of memory and emotion, haunting the location, person, or cause of their demise. Driven by grief, rage, or unresolved longing, Once-Was Spirits can be either vengeful or protective, cursing those who remind them of their untimely end or warning others away from the dangers that claimed their lives.

The appearance and actions of Once-Was Spirits are shaped by the specific circumstances of their deaths. Each spirit carries a story, a poignant tale of loss or betrayal that explains why they are still bound to the mortal realm. Locals often tell these stories as cautionary tales or legends, imparting both fear and reverence for the tragedies that these spirits embody.

## Characteristics of Once-Was Spirits

Once-Was Spirits vary widely in appearance and behavior, influenced by the trauma, emotion, and location of their deaths. Though generally weaker than other spirits, their presence can be unsettling or even dangerous to those who encounter them.

## 1. Emotional Residue and Bound Locations

- Tied to Place or Object: Once-Was Spirits are almost always bound to the location or object associated with their death. This connection keeps them rooted in the mortal realm, unable to leave until their unresolved feelings are addressed or they are properly laid to rest.
- Distorted Echoes of Former Selves: These spirits are often shadowy, translucent, or flickering forms that retain some semblance of their appearance in life. However, their features are usually distorted by the emotions tied to their death, with expressions of sorrow, anger, or fear eternally imprinted on their faces.
- Aura of Emotion: Each Once-Was Spirit emanates an aura tied to their death. A child lost in a storm might radiate cold sadness, while a warrior betrayed in battle might exude simmering anger. Travelers and locals alike can sense these emotions upon entering the spirit’s territory, often feeling a chill, unease, or sudden melancholy.

## 2. Vengeful or Protective Tendencies

- Vengeful Spirits: Once-Was Spirits who died due to betrayal, injustice, or violence are often vengeful, targeting individuals or groups that remind them of the circumstances of their death. Such spirits may try to scare intruders away, cause accidents, or curse those they perceive as threats. For example, a murdered merchant might curse thieves who come near his stall, causing their belongings to vanish or their luck to sour.
- Protective Spirits: Once-Was Spirits who perished while protecting others, or who died due to accidents, may act protectively. These spirits often warn travelers of dangers or discourage them from repeating the mistakes that led to their deaths. A drowned sailor might cause the water to grow rough near a hidden reef, while a lost traveler’s spirit might create lights to lead others safely through treacherous terrain.

## 3. Weakness and Lingering Resentment

- Limited Power and Influence: Once-Was Spirits are not as powerful as other spirits in Ahvantir. They cannot manipulate the physical world as extensively as a Warden or Locus but may influence minor phenomena—whispered voices, cold spots, or the faint sound of footsteps. Their power is usually tied directly to the emotion of their death.
- Lingering Resentment and Unresolved Purpose: Most Once-Was Spirits are trapped in cycles of resentment, unable to pass on due to unresolved emotions. Some crave vengeance or justice, while others simply want to be remembered, honored, or understood. When mortals help them achieve closure, they may finally find peace and leave the mortal realm.

## Types of Deaths That Inspire Once-Was Spirits

The deaths that produce Once-Was Spirits are often tragic, violent, or laden with intense emotion. Some spirits are born from sorrow, others from betrayal, and still others from bravery. Here are some examples of the types of deaths that inspire these spirits:

## 1. Betrayal and Murder

- Example Story: The Ghost of Tarnis, the Betrayed Warrior
- Tarnis was a warrior who fought bravely for his people but was betrayed by his closest friend, who left him to die in battle to gain favor with an enemy leader. Tarnis’s spirit now haunts the field where he fell, appearing as a shadowy figure with eyes filled with rage and sorrow. Those who approach feel an overwhelming sense of distrust and unease. Tarnis’s spirit lashes out at warriors or mercenaries, cursing them with misfortune in combat until they acknowledge his betrayal and leave an offering of friendship at his final resting place.
- Common Manifestations: Spirits tied to betrayal may create feelings of paranoia, distrust, or dread. They might manipulate shadows, create eerie whispers, or even stir visions of the betrayal they endured, replaying fragments of their death to those nearby.

## 2. Accidental Deaths and Tragic Loss

- Example Story: Eira, the Child Lost in the Snow
- Eira was a young girl who became lost during a harsh winter storm and froze to death just a mile from her village. Her spirit lingers in the forest where she was last seen, appearing as a faint, blue glow or the sound of a child’s laughter. Eira is a protective spirit, guiding lost travelers back to safety. However, those who ignore her gentle guidance often encounter misleading paths and biting cold, as the spirit becomes frustrated and withdraws her help.
- Common Manifestations: These spirits are often mournful, leaving behind an aura of sadness and longing. Their presence may be felt as an unnatural chill, a lingering fog, or distant sounds—like laughter or crying—echoing through the air. Protective spirits tend to appear in softer, gentler forms, while those with unfulfilled wishes may create illusions that lead travelers astray.

## 3. Heroic Sacrifice and Unfulfilled Duty

- Example Story: Thera, the Sentinel of the Mountain Pass
- Thera was a brave soldier who gave her life defending her village from invaders. Despite her efforts, the village was eventually overrun, and Thera died feeling her sacrifice was in vain. Her spirit now haunts the mountain pass she defended, standing guard to prevent intruders from entering. She often confronts travelers with ghostly whispers, urging them to prove their intentions. Those who refuse or act suspiciously may be met with phantom winds or sudden rockslides, as Thera seeks to defend her territory even in death.
- Common Manifestations: Spirits of sacrifice are often protective but may also be strict or demanding, testing those who enter their domain. They may cause cold drafts, heavy fog, or minor natural disruptions like falling rocks. People sense a strong presence, as if being watched or judged.

## 4. Death by Deception or Trickery

- Example Story: Lorn, the Spurned Lover
- Lorn was a young man who fell in love with a deceptive enchantress who led him deep into the forest before abandoning him. He became lost and died from exhaustion and despair, feeling utterly betrayed. His spirit lingers, drawing others into the woods with faint, alluring music, mirroring the deception he experienced. Those who follow often become lost, and only those who resist the lure or recognize it as a trap escape the forest unscathed.
- Common Manifestations: Spirits tied to trickery often mimic the means by which they were deceived, creating illusions, sounds, or alluring visions that lure travelers off safe paths. Their presence may feel enticing yet unsettling, and they often leave behind a sense of regret and confusion.

## 5. Grief and Loss of Loved Ones

- Example Story: Kaia, the Mourning Mother
- Kaia lost her children to a sudden illness that swept through her village. Her grief was so profound that it lingered long after her death, transforming her spirit into a sorrowful presence in the village’s graveyard. Kaia’s spirit watches over children and young mothers, protecting them from harm. However, if disrespected, she can become vengeful, causing those who disturb the graves to feel sudden bouts of sorrow or fever, as if sharing in her loss.
- Common Manifestations: Spirits of grief create an overwhelming sense of sadness, loss, or mourning. They may cause people to feel drained, evoke phantom sounds like weeping or lullabies, and often appear near cemeteries or places tied to their loved ones.

## Cultural Interaction and Perception of Once-Was Spirits

Once-Was Spirits are both feared and respected by the people of Ahvantir, who have learned to coexist with these troubled remnants of the past. Local tales and legends are passed down to help others understand and avoid disturbing these spirits.

## 1. Reverence and Storytelling

- Cautionary Tales: Each Once-Was Spirit has a story, often told as a cautionary tale to warn travelers and young people of the dangers that created the spirit. These stories emphasize respect, remembrance, and caution when encountering locations where Once-Was Spirits are known to dwell.
- Offerings of Peace: Locals leave small offerings—such as flowers, tokens, or candles—at sites associated with Once-Was Spirits to show respect and ensure their own safety. These offerings are a gesture of goodwill, acknowledging the spirit’s suffering and helping them find peace.

## 2. Seeking Peace and Closure

- Helping the Spirit Move On: In rare cases, mortals may attempt to help Once-Was Spirits find closure, allowing them to leave the mortal realm. This may involve acknowledging the spirit’s grievances, setting right the wrongs that caused their death, or simply honoring their memory. Spiritualists and shamans sometimes conduct cleansing rituals to ease the spirit’s burden.
- Avoidance and Precaution: While some spirits are helpful, many people avoid places known to harbor Once-Was Spirits due to the unsettling or dangerous effects. Signs of a spirit’s presence—cold spots, echoes, or unusual weather—are often heeded as warnings.

## Summary of Once-Was Spirits’ Role in Ahvantir

Once-Was Spirits are spectral remnants, born from untimely or tragic deaths that leave behind emotional scars. They linger in the mortal realm, often bound to the locations or objects tied to their deaths, replaying their final moments as cautionary tales. Through their haunting auras, warnings, and influences, Once-Was Spirits serve as both guardians and reminders of loss, reflecting the delicate boundary between life and death in Ahvantir. Whether feared, honored, or pitied, they remain an essential part of the spiritual landscape, embodying the lasting echoes of life’s most poignant moments.
