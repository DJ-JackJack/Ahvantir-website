# Currency of [[Aru'Mas]]

The coinage of Aru'Mas reflects the city's deep cultural roots, economic sophistication, and artistic craftsmanship. Coins are ranked by value from least to most valuable, each with a unique design that embodies an aspect of the city's history and ethos.

## Copper Chips

- Material & Appearance:
- Shape: Triangular
- Design: One side bears the crest of the [[Merchants Guild|Merchants' Guild]], symbolizing commerce and community, while the other side displays the symbol for harmony, representing the balance essential to the city’s success.
- Size: Small and lightweight, making them easy to carry in bulk.
- Value:
- The lowest denomination in Aru'Mas, used for everyday necessities.
- Common Uses:
- Basic Purchases: Used to buy food, small tools, or materials.
- Informal Transactions: Street vendors and performers commonly accept Chips.
- Gratuities: Often given as tips or small tokens of appreciation.

## Silver Lions

- Material & Appearance:
- Shape: Pentagonal
- Design: One side depicts the head of a lion, symbolizing strength and courage, while the reverse features a shield and sword insignia, representing defense and justice.
- Size: Slightly larger than Copper Chips but still easy to carry.
- Value:
- Equivalent to 10 Copper Chips.
- Common Uses:
- Daily Commerce: Ideal for medium-value purchases such as clothing, tools, and professional services.
- Payment for Craftsmanship: Artisans and tradespeople often prefer Lions for their services.
- Offerings: Used in modest spiritual offerings or guild-related rituals.

## Gold Crowns

- Material & Appearance:
- Shape: Octagonal
- Design: One side is minted with the face of the current monarch, King [[Voren Ilderas]], reflecting the crown's authority, while the reverse bears a rune meaning "hope", symbolizing prosperity and optimism.
- Size: Larger than Silver Lions and distinct in shape, Crowns exude prestige.
- Value:
- Equivalent to 10 Silver Lions or 100 Copper Chips.
- Common Uses:
- High-Value Transactions: Crowns are used for buying luxury goods, commissioning art, or funding minor projects.
- Wealth Reserve: Often stored as a sign of wealth by nobles and guildmasters.
- Noble Commerce: Preferred currency in significant dealings among the upper classes.

## Platinum Shields

- Material & Appearance:
- Shape: Round
- Design: The obverse features the crest of [[The Church of the Threefold Path|the Church of the Threefold Path]], symbolizing spiritual unity and guidance, while the reverse shows the three moons in alignment, representing cosmic order and rare occurrences.
- Size: The largest and heaviest coin, reflecting its exceptional value.
- Value:
- Equivalent to 10 Gold Crowns, 100 Silver Lions, or 1,000 Copper Chips.
- Common Uses:
- Extraordinary Transactions: Used for purchasing estates, financing large trade deals, or as diplomatic gifts.
- Prestige and Ceremony: Shields are often presented as tributes or during ceremonial exchanges.
- Treasury Holdings: Rarely seen in daily circulation, Shields are stored in vaults as symbols of immense wealth and stability.

## Foreign Currency Exchange in Aru'Mas

- Appraisal Process:
- Foreign coins must be brought to a Merchants' Guildhall for appraisal. The guild assesses the coin's weight, purity, and origin to determine its value in the local economy.
- Appraisal Fee:
- A flat fee of 1 Gold Crown per batch of foreign coins is charged for this service.
- Standardized Value:
- Once appraised, foreign currency is converted into equivalent local coinage, ensuring seamless integration into Aru'Mas's economy.
- Trade Flexibility:
- After appraisal, foreign coins of the same type are freely accepted in transactions across the city.
