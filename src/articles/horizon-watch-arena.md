# Horizon Watch Arena

The Horizon Watch Arena is a renowned proving ground and entertainment venue located in the heart of Aru’Mas. Managed by the city’s Adventurers’ Guild, the arena is a place where aspiring adventurers test their skills, fierce creatures captured beyond the city’s walls are displayed, and citizens of Aru’Mas gather for thrilling exhibitions of combat prowess and survival. It serves as both a testing ground for adventurers aiming to earn the right to explore beyond the [[Spiritsway Passage]] and a source of excitement and entertainment for the city’s residents.

## Overview

The Horizon Watch Arena is a colossal structure built from enchanted stone and reinforced with arcane wards, making it one of the most secure places in the city. Its oval shape allows for optimal viewing from all angles, with seating arranged in tiers to accommodate up to 10,000 spectators. A vast network of magical wards ensures the safety of the audience, containing even the most dangerous of combatants.

The arena’s name, "Horizon Watch," reflects its role as a gateway to greater challenges, preparing adventurers to confront the dangers of Ahvantir and the multiverse beyond. It also symbolizes the city’s spirit of exploration and its emphasis on readiness and resilience.

## Structure and Layout

The Horizon Watch Arena is divided into multiple sections, each with specific functions to support both combat trials and entertainment events:

## 1. The Battleground

- The main combat area of the arena is a massive, sandy pit, customizable through powerful magic. Depending on the needs of the event, the battleground can transform into different terrains, including rocky hills, swampy marshes, or even simulated forest clearings.
- Arcane runes inscribed around the arena’s perimeter are used to summon monsters, conjure obstacles, and control environmental effects like fog, rain, or magical darkness. These effects make each trial unique and add an element of unpredictability for the adventurers.
- The battleground’s enchantments also include containment wards, which ensure that even the most aggressive monsters or spellcasters cannot harm spectators. These wards are maintained by the [[Runeguard]], who oversee safety during all events.

## 2. The Adventurers’ Hall

- Attached to the arena is the Adventurers’ Hall, a facility where aspiring adventurers register for tests, receive briefings, and prepare for combat trials. The hall serves as the main interface between the Adventurers’ Guild and those seeking to prove their mettle.
- The hall features training rooms, weapon storage, and magical healing facilities provided by the [[Order of Harmony]], ensuring that combatants are fully prepared and tended to before and after trials. Healers are on hand to stabilize wounded adventurers, as injuries are common despite the protective wards.
- Adventurers who complete the trials successfully often earn badges of valor, which grant them increased access to the guild’s resources and further their standing within the guild. These badges are required for higher-level quests and for obtaining passage beyond the city walls.

## 3. The Observation Deck

- The Observation Deck is a section of the arena reserved for officials, nobles, and prominent members of the Adventurers’ Guild. It offers a superior view of the combat area and is often used by guild leaders, council members, and wealthy patrons who seek to sponsor promising adventurers.
- The deck is equipped with magical scrying orbs that allow spectators to observe the battles from different angles, zooming in on critical moments of combat. These orbs are also used to analyze the performance of adventurers, providing valuable feedback and ensuring fairness in trials.

## 4. The Menagerie Vaults

- Beneath the arena lies the Menagerie Vaults, a series of reinforced holding cells where the creatures used in the trials are kept. These vaults are designed to contain a wide variety of beasts, from wild animals captured in the Ahvantir wilds to exotic monsters brought in from other realms.
- The cells are lined with arcane barriers that suppress the creatures’ magical abilities and keep them contained until they are released into the arena. The vaults are maintained by beastmasters who specialize in handling and controlling dangerous creatures.
- The creatures used in the trials are rotated regularly, ensuring that no two trials are ever the same. Adventurers must be prepared to face a wide range of challenges, including magical beasts, undead horrors, and elemental entities.

## 5. The Spectator Stands

- The Spectator Stands are tiered seating areas that offer excellent views of the arena. The stands are often filled with a mix of excited citizens, adventurers’ guild members, and nobles seeking entertainment.
- Vendors move through the stands, selling snacks, drinks, and souvenirs related to the arena's most famous combatants. The atmosphere is lively, with the crowd cheering for impressive feats, close battles, and daring displays of skill.
- Some sections of the stands are enchanted with protective barriers, providing additional safety for spectators. These sections are usually reserved for nobles and officials, ensuring their safety during particularly dangerous exhibitions.

## Types of Events

The Horizon Watch Arena hosts a variety of events designed to test adventurers and entertain the public:

## 1. Combat Trials

- The Combat Trials are the arena’s main events, designed to test the skills of adventurers looking to earn guild membership or the right to explore beyond the city. Combatants face progressively difficult challenges, with obstacles, creatures, and simulated hazards that mirror the dangers of Ahvantir.
- The trials are divided into tiers, with each tier representing a higher level of difficulty. Completing a tier earns adventurers guild recognition, and those who conquer the highest tier often receive exclusive quests, rare rewards, and official commendations.

## 2. Monster Exhibitions

- In addition to the trials, the arena hosts Monster Exhibitions, where captured creatures are displayed and showcased in a more controlled environment. These exhibitions allow adventurers and spectators to study various creatures’ behavior, strengths, and weaknesses.
- Some exhibitions involve beastmasters demonstrating control techniques or showcasing rare and exotic monsters from the archipelago and the multiverse. These events are both educational and entertaining, often drawing large crowds eager to see creatures they would never encounter otherwise.

## 3. Team Battles

- The arena also hosts Team Battles, where groups of adventurers work together to defeat a series of challenges. These events are particularly popular with spectators, as they showcase both tactics and teamwork, emphasizing the value of cooperation in the wilds of Ahvantir.
- Team Battles often feature unique objectives, such as capturing a magical artifact, surviving waves of enemies, or defending a position against overwhelming odds. They are designed to mimic scenarios adventurers might face beyond the city’s borders.

## 4. Special Events and Festivals

- During city-wide festivals or holidays, the arena holds special events that feature prominent adventurers, rare creatures, or even multiversal guests. These events are often tied to the City Guard, the Merchants’ Guild, or [[The Church of the Threefold Path|the Church of the Threefold Path]], incorporating their unique perspectives and contributions to the city.
- The arena also plays a central role in the Ritual of Renewal, where the combat trials become part of the ceremonial process that reaffirms the Old Pacts and strengthens the city’s connection to the [[Spirits of Ahvantir|spirits of Ahvantir]].

## Reputation and Culture

The Horizon Watch Arena is more than just a testing ground; it’s a cultural landmark that embodies the spirit of adventure that defines Aru’Mas.

## 1. Symbol of Readiness

- The arena is seen as a rite of passage for adventurers, symbolizing their transition from hopeful explorers to seasoned challengers ready to face the dangers of the archipelago and the multiverse. Passing the trials here is considered a badge of honor, earning respect and recognition among the guilds and the broader community.
- For the citizens of Aru’Mas, the arena represents readiness and resilience, a place where the next generation of defenders is forged, ensuring the city’s continued strength and safety.

## 2. Entertainment and Community

- The Horizon Watch Arena is a beloved entertainment venue, drawing crowds from all walks of life. It serves as a place where citizens can gather to cheer for their favorite adventurers, share stories, and celebrate the bravery and skill of those who protect the city.
- The arena is also known for fostering a sense of community among the adventurers who train and compete there. Many adventurers form lasting bonds with their comrades after surviving trials together, and successful participants often find new allies for future expeditions.

## 3. Rumors and Legends

- The arena is not without its share of mystery and legend. Tales circulate about hidden trials, secret rewards granted by the guild’s highest-ranking members, and even rumored challenges set by powerful spirits to test the worth of adventurers who seek their favor.
- Some whisper that the arena has a secret lower level, accessible only to those who have earned the highest rank within the guild. This hidden level is said to hold the most dangerous creatures and mystical challenges, where only the boldest adventurers dare to tread.
