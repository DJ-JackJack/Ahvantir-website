# Tirilan

Tirilan, the Ever-Flowing, is the deity of change, cycles, and transformation, embodying the natural flow of time, the shifting of seasons, and the endless ebb and flow of existence. They are a neutral and constant force, neither favoring creation nor destruction, but embracing both as essential components of the cycle of life. Tirilan represents growth, decay, death, and rebirth, seeing each stage as an inseparable part of the whole. Followers of Tirilan believe in embracing change rather than resisting it, accepting the impermanence of all things and finding beauty in every phase of life’s journey.

Tirilan’s presence is felt in the shifting seasons, the life and death of plants and animals, and even in the weather patterns that move across Ahvantir. Their influence teaches mortals that nothing remains stagnant; just as the land changes, so too must people, communities, and beliefs adapt. Worshipers of Tirilan are known for their introspective and adaptable nature, viewing their own lives as a microcosm of the greater cycles that Tirilan governs.

## The Cult of the Flowing Tide

The Cult of the Flowing Tide is a dedicated following that honors Tirilan’s teachings by embracing transformation in all forms. Members of the Flowing Tide do not have a rigid hierarchy or centralized structure, as they believe that true devotion to Tirilan requires fluidity and adaptability. The cult is largely informal, united by a shared philosophy and series of rites that vary based on the current season and the life stage of each individual.

The Flowing Tide attracts those who see value in continuous self-renewal, who respect the inevitability of change, and who seek peace with the transient nature of life. Followers are often healers, scholars, and wanderers who value flexibility, introspection, and growth. They believe that, just as nature shifts and transforms, so too must they remain open to the cycles within themselves and the world around them.

## Beliefs and Practices

The Flowing Tide embraces a philosophy that change is both inevitable and sacred. Followers see each stage of existence as a necessary step within a grander cycle, and they believe that resisting change only leads to suffering. Instead, they strive to accept every transition, whether joyous or sorrowful, as part of life’s natural flow. The cult encourages members to reflect on their past, accept the present, and move forward without regret, always ready to embrace the next phase.

- Acceptance of Life’s Cycles: Followers of Tirilan view all stages of life, including birth, growth, decay, and death, as equally important. They perform ceremonies to honor each phase, marking life’s transitions with rituals of gratitude and remembrance.
- Seasonal Rites: Followers observe four main seasonal rites, each dedicated to a different phase of the cycle. During these rites, they engage in activities that align with the season’s essence—planting in spring, celebrating life’s fullness in summer, preparing for decline in autumn, and meditating on impermanence in winter.
- Personal Transformations: Followers frequently undergo personal transformations, whether through lifestyle changes, spiritual reflection, or symbolic acts of letting go. These transformations might include discarding old belongings, changing their physical appearance, or adopting new philosophies, all as acts of renewal in Tirilan’s honor.

## Symbols and Sacred Artifacts

Tirilan’s primary symbol is a circle divided into four segments, each representing a season and a stage of life. This circle, called the Wheel of Transformation, embodies the endless nature of cycles and is worn as a pendant or charm by followers. The cult’s sacred artifacts are often crafted from natural materials and symbolize renewal and change.

- Seasonal Charms: Followers carry small charms representing the current season, such as a flower in spring, a leaf in autumn, a shell in summer, or a snowflake in winter. These charms serve as reminders of the present moment and encourage followers to embrace the season’s unique qualities.
- Renewal Stones: These are polished stones inscribed with the Wheel of Transformation symbol. Followers hold these stones during meditation or rites of change, using them to focus on letting go of the past and welcoming new phases of life.

## Rituals and Holy Days

The Cult of the Flowing Tide does not gather in temples or rigid structures; instead, they perform their rituals in natural spaces where Tirilan’s influence can be felt, such as forests, riversides, or open fields. Their rituals often involve offerings, meditative practices, and symbolic acts that align with nature’s cycles. The most important observances are the Seasonal Passages, which mark the shift from one season to the next and reflect the cycle of life, death, and rebirth.

## Key Rituals

- The Rite of Renewal: This ritual is performed at the beginning of each season to honor Tirilan’s influence over change. During the Rite of Renewal, followers gather to meditate on their personal growth and transformation, leaving behind symbolic items that represent aspects of themselves or their past that they wish to let go of.
- The Reflection of Seasons: Followers meditate on the previous season’s lessons and reflect on how they have changed over the past few months. They may keep journals to record their reflections, viewing each season as a chapter in their journey. This practice encourages followers to remember that just as seasons shift, so too do their lives, and each season brings new teachings.
- Ceremonies of Transition: The Flowing Tide holds special rites during life transitions, such as births, coming-of-age celebrations, and funerals. These ceremonies emphasize Tirilan’s teachings on the continuity of life, guiding participants to accept each transition as part of an unbroken cycle.

## The Seasonal Passages

Each season is marked by a Seasonal Passage that celebrates the unique aspects of that time of year and its place in the cycle. During the Seasonal Passages, followers of Tirilan gather to observe the shift in nature, conduct symbolic rituals, and express gratitude for the current phase.

- Spring Passage: Known as the Blooming, this passage celebrates new beginnings, growth, and potential. Followers plant seeds or place flowers at Tirilan’s shrines, symbolizing the promise of life and the renewal of energy. It is a time of goal-setting, where followers declare intentions for the coming months.
- Summer Passage: Called the Fullness, the summer passage celebrates abundance, vitality, and fulfillment. Followers engage in communal feasts, express gratitude for the bounty of life, and honor Tirilan’s gift of growth. They take time to reflect on what they have achieved and savor the joys of the present.
- Autumn Passage: Known as the Falling, the autumn passage focuses on letting go, preparing for rest, and accepting decline. Followers participate in rituals that involve releasing or shedding—symbolically letting go of what they no longer need. It is a time for quiet reflection, as they prepare for the coming winter.
- Winter Passage: The Veiled, or winter passage, is a solemn observance that emphasizes the importance of rest, introspection, and acceptance of endings. Followers gather to meditate on mortality and the cycles of life, embracing stillness and the quiet beauty of winter. It is seen as a time of healing and introspection, where followers retreat to renew themselves for the next cycle.

## Followers of Tirilan

Followers of Tirilan, the Ever-Flowing, are known as the Flowing Tide or simply as Tidewalkers. They are a diverse group, including healers, herbalists, sages, and nomads who believe that flexibility and adaptability are keys to fulfillment. Though the cult has no formal hierarchy, there are respected individuals within the Flowing Tide who embody Tirilan’s teachings and guide others in navigating life’s cycles.

## Types of Followers

- Tidewalkers: General followers of Tirilan, who live by the principle of acceptance and change. They are introspective, resilient, and often act as mentors to those struggling with transitions or uncertainties. Tidewalkers emphasize personal growth and live in harmony with nature’s cycles, often moving with the seasons.
- Cyclekeepers: These are revered members of the Flowing Tide who have dedicated themselves to guiding others through life’s transitions. They oversee rites of passage, help families with ceremonies for births, marriages, and funerals, and offer counsel to those experiencing upheaval. Cyclekeepers are seen as wise and compassionate, embodying Tirilan’s teachings on change and renewal.
- Seasonwalkers: Followers who travel between communities, embodying the essence of each season as they pass through. In spring, they bring seeds and stories of new beginnings; in summer, they celebrate vitality; in autumn, they teach the art of letting go; and in winter, they lead gatherings in meditation and quiet reflection. Seasonwalkers are seen as messengers of Tirilan’s will, connecting communities to the natural rhythms of life.

## Public Perception and Role in Society

The Flowing Tide is viewed with a sense of respect and mystery by the citizens of Aru’Mas. The Tidewalkers’ focus on acceptance and introspection is admired by some and misunderstood by others, as their emphasis on embracing change contrasts with the desire for stability held by many city dwellers. However, their wisdom and calming presence make them valued members of the community, especially during times of transition or hardship.

In society, followers of Tirilan often serve as healers, herbalists, counselors, and spiritual guides. They are known for their patience and their ability to offer comfort to those facing personal loss, illness, or life changes. While some may view them as overly detached due to their acceptance of decay and death, most appreciate their ability to see beauty and purpose in all aspects of life.

## Tirilan’s Influence on the World

Tirilan’s influence is present in all of Ahvantir’s natural cycles—the changing seasons, the growth and decay of plants, the rise and fall of rivers, and the stages of mortal life. Followers of Tirilan believe that their god is a constant force, neither benevolent nor malevolent, simply a presence that imparts wisdom through life’s rhythms. People may invoke Tirilan’s name during major life transitions, offering prayers for guidance or resilience in times of change.

Tirilan’s role as the Ever-Flowing serves as a reminder to embrace change, to see life’s fleeting moments as beautiful rather than mournful, and to live without attachment to any single stage of existence. Followers of Tirilan view their lives as part of a grander cycle, and through their devotion, they seek to live in harmony with the endless flow of transformation. Their practices, rites, and beliefs all center around this philosophy, helping those who follow Tirilan navigate the changing tides of their own lives.
