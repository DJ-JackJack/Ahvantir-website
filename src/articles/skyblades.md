# Skyblades

The Skyblades are the most commonly used mounts among the [[Skywardens]] of Aru’Mas, prized for their agility, loyalty, and keen senses. Unlike the other mounts of the [[Sunspear Legion]], Skyblades are not known for magical qualities or impressive size; rather, they are celebrated for their natural athleticism, ease of handling, and reliability. Skyblades serve as the primary mounts for new recruits and veterans alike, making them the backbone of the Skywardens’ aerial forces.

## Physical Attributes and Appearance

Skyblades are sleek, hawk-like creatures with lightweight, aerodynamic bodies built for speed and maneuverability. Their feathers are often a mottled mix of dark grays and browns, with a subtle sheen that gives them a streamlined, polished look in the sunlight. Although they are smaller than other mounts, their compact size and nimble structure allow them to navigate through the skies with precision and speed.

Their wings are long and narrow, optimized for rapid direction changes and fast descents. Their beaks are sharp and pointed, ideal for striking quickly and effectively in close quarters, while their talons are designed for gripping and stabilizing rather than brute force. This combination of natural features makes Skyblades ideal for scouting and rapid-response tasks, where speed and accuracy are essential.

Despite their smaller size, Skyblades possess a presence that commands respect. Their sharp, hawk-like eyes give them exceptional vision, allowing them to spot details from high above, which makes them excellent scouts. Their agility also means that they can evade larger threats in the air, outmaneuvering both other mounts and airborne dangers with ease.

## Temperament and Bonding with Skywardens

Skyblades are known for their friendly, loyal temperaments, making them relatively easy to bond with and train. They have an intelligent, curious nature and are quick to form trusting relationships with their riders. Unlike some of the more temperamental mounts, Skyblades are eager to follow commands and adapt quickly to their rider’s instructions, making them ideal for both new recruits and experienced riders.

Their straightforward personalities make them approachable, and they are often chosen as a Skywarden’s first mount. Skyblades enjoy the routine and camaraderie of the Legion, and they seem to thrive in structured environments. Once bonded, a Skyblade shows steadfast loyalty to its rider, responding instinctively to subtle commands and adapting to their rider’s style. While they may lack the magical flair of other mounts, their reliability and intelligence make them irreplaceable allies in both training and combat.

Skyblades are also known for their sharp intuition, sensing the emotions and intentions of their riders with ease. This sensitivity makes them well-suited for missions requiring delicate navigation or complex maneuvers, as they can anticipate their rider’s needs and adjust accordingly. They work best with riders who respect their intelligence, treating them as partners rather than mere beasts.

## Skyblades in Combat

In battle, Skyblades are valued for their speed and precision. While they do not possess the raw power of larger mounts, their agility and accuracy allow them to deliver precise strikes and perform rapid hit-and-run attacks. They are ideal for roles that require close-quarters maneuvering, such as urban scouting or ambush tactics, where their small size and speed make them difficult targets.

## Tactics and Combat Roles

- Reconnaissance and Scouting: Skyblades are primarily used as scouts, thanks to their sharp eyesight and natural agility. They excel at surveying enemy positions, spotting hidden threats, and providing valuable intelligence from high above. Their maneuverability allows them to navigate challenging terrain and report back with precision.
- Ambush and Hit-and-Run Attacks: Skyblades are highly effective in ambush scenarios. Their agility allows them to swoop down, deliver a quick strike with their sharp beaks or talons, and retreat before the enemy can respond. These hit-and-run tactics can disrupt enemy formations, sowing confusion and opening up opportunities for larger forces to strike.
- Urban and Close-Quarters Combat: In the narrow streets and crowded alleys of Aru’Mas, Skyblades excel with their ability to maneuver quickly and precisely. They are used in situations that require deft navigation, allowing the Skywardens to maintain aerial superiority even in close quarters.
- Evacuation and Rapid Extraction: Thanks to their speed, Skyblades are ideal for extracting individuals or allies from dangerous situations. They can move in and out quickly, retrieving personnel or delivering essential supplies to ground forces, ensuring swift evacuations in high-risk areas.
- Precision Support in Large Battles: During larger engagements, Skyblades provide crucial support by targeting specific enemy units or providing distraction for larger mounts. Their speed and adaptability allow them to respond to the changing dynamics of battle, delivering rapid support wherever it’s needed.

## Role in the Culture of [[Aru'Mas]]

The citizens of Aru’Mas hold Skyblades in high regard as symbols of vigilance, loyalty, and dedication. As the most common of the Skywarden mounts, Skyblades are often seen patrolling the skies or training with new recruits, and their presence is a familiar and reassuring sight to the people. They are admired not only for their skill and reliability but also for their approachability, as they symbolize the inclusiveness and unity of the Legion.

Skyblades are frequently depicted in the art of Aru’Mas, often shown flying in formation with other Skywardens, representing the cooperative spirit of the city. They appear on banners and murals as guardians who keep watch over the city’s streets and skies. Many believe that a sighting of a Skyblade during an important journey or event is a sign of good luck, and the citizens feel a sense of comfort seeing them soar above the city.

During festivals honoring the Skywardens, vendors sell small tokens and charms shaped like Skyblades, symbolizing loyalty and protection. These tokens are especially popular among young recruits and their families, who wear them as symbols of courage and trust. Skyblade feathers are also considered lucky keepsakes, collected by citizens and sold as talismans of protection.

## Legends and Lore of the Skyblades

Skyblades are part of numerous tales and legends in Aru’Mas, stories that celebrate their agility, loyalty, and courage. One such story is that of Swiftclaw, a legendary Skyblade who guided a group of lost travelers through dangerous territory. Swiftclaw’s speed and loyalty to their rider allowed them to escape ambushes, evade traps, and eventually lead the group to safety. To this day, Swiftclaw is revered among the Skywardens as a symbol of devotion and quick thinking.

A legend that resonates deeply within Aru’Mas is that of the Dawnwing, a Skyblade who led a charge during an ancient battle at the break of dawn. The Dawnwing and its rider darted through enemy lines, disrupting and confusing the opposition with swift, calculated strikes. This tale has made the sight of a Skyblade at dawn an enduring symbol of hope and resilience for the people of Aru’Mas.

## Skyblades as Guardians of the City

To the Skywardens and citizens of Aru’Mas, Skyblades represent vigilance, loyalty, and the enduring dedication of the Legion. They are the tireless watchers of the skies, ever-present and ever-vigilant, keeping Aru’Mas safe through their dedication and precision. Although they lack the magical abilities of other mounts, their natural skill and unwavering loyalty make them indispensable to the [[Sunspear Legion]].

Whether scouting the borders, patrolling the streets, or training alongside the Skywardens, Skyblades bring a dependable strength to the city’s defense. Their loyalty and intelligence make them not only partners in battle but cherished symbols of Aru’Mas’s strength and unity. To see a Skyblade soaring through the city is a reminder of the commitment and protection offered by the Skywardens, guardians who watch over the people with unbreakable loyalty and precision.
