# Friar Johan Veitr

"The Foolish Friar" of [[Temple Way]]

## Overview

Friar Johan Veitr is a Christian monk from 14th-century Germany who was inexplicably transported to the city of [[Aru'Mas]] through a tear in reality. Known for his persistent faith and unique perspective, Johan has become a cultural landmark in Temple Way, attracting attention from scholars, theologians, and curious citizens alike.

## History

Born in 1394 in Germany, Friar Johan Veitr was considered a less-than-ideal monk. Known for his laziness and lack of intellectual rigor, he often clashed with his brethren and eventually found himself expelled from his monastery. Wandering aimlessly for months, Johan was caught in a series of bizarre events that ended with him being thrust through a tear in reality and into Aru'Mas.

Initially disoriented, Johan interpreted his arrival as divine providence, believing God had chosen him to spread the teachings of Jesus Christ to this strange new land. Though the city’s officials were skeptical of his claims, they permitted him to set up a stall in Temple Way, where he began preaching his faith.

## Life in Aru'Mas

## The Stall in Temple Way

Johan's modest stall is a simple setup amidst the grandeur of Temple Way. It features a crude wooden cross, a few hand-copied passages from the Bible, and a small collection plate. Despite his humble resources, Johan passionately preaches about his monotheistic faith to anyone willing to listen.

## Reception by the People

The citizens of Aru'Mas view Johan as a curious oddity. Many attend his sermons out of fascination rather than genuine interest in conversion. His tales of a world without magic, governed solely by faith, intrigue scholars and theologians alike, while children are enchanted by his animated parables and stories.

Despite his efforts, Johan has not converted anyone to Christianity. Some citizens engage him in lively debates about the nature of divinity and faith, while others see his teachings as an amusing but harmless addition to the city’s religious tapestry.

## Cultural Impact

Over time, Johan has become a point of fascination for the people of Aru'Mas. Scholars from the [[Order of Wisdom]] often visit him to learn about the theology and history of his world. Artists and performers in the [[Aurora Song District]] have immortalized Johan in plays and songs, often portraying him as a figure of comedic resolve.

## Challenges and Persistence

Johan faces significant challenges in his mission. The deeply ingrained spiritual practices of Aru'Mas, rooted in pacts with spirits and the Pathite Pantheon, leave little room for his monotheistic worldview. Though his faith is respected as an intellectual curiosity, it is not widely embraced.

Additionally, Johan struggles with feelings of inadequacy and doubt, questioning whether he is truly capable of fulfilling his divine mission. Despite these hardships, he maintains a jovial demeanor and continues to preach, believing that persistence and kindness will eventually bear fruit.

## Legacy

Though Friar Johan has yet to convert a single soul, his presence has undeniably impacted the city. He has sparked conversations about the nature of faith and the diversity of belief systems, enriching the cultural and intellectual fabric of Aru'Mas. Whether or not his mission succeeds, Johan’s story stands as a testament to resilience and the power of faith in the face of overwhelming odds.
