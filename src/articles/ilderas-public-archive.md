# Ilderas Public Archive

In [[Upper Hearthstone]], the Ilderas Public Archive stands as a beacon of knowledge and enlightenment, welcoming scholars, students, and citizens from all walks of life. Established centuries ago by the Ilderas royal family, the Archive is both a public library and a revered institution dedicated to the preservation of history, culture, and magical knowledge. Within its grand halls and towering shelves lie vast collections of books, manuscripts, scrolls, and artifacts, offering an expansive record of the city’s storied past and its connection to the wider multiverse.

The Archive’s main collection is free to access, open to the people of Aru’Mas and travelers alike. This public access underscores the city’s commitment to education and the sharing of knowledge, though certain areas are restricted to protect sensitive or dangerous information. These restricted sections require special permissions, which are only granted under strict guidelines set by the city’s governing bodies and spiritual orders.

## Architecture and Layout of the Archive

The Ilderas Public Archive is an architectural marvel, built from white stone and adorned with intricate carvings of historical scenes, mystical symbols, and inscriptions from the Pathite Pantheon. Its walls are lined with tall arched windows that fill the interior with natural light, giving the space an almost cathedral-like atmosphere. The central hall, with its marble floors and rows of ornate wooden desks, is filled with quiet activity as scholars, students, and curious visitors pore over texts and notes.

The Archive has three primary levels, each dedicated to different collections and services:

- The General Collection (Ground Floor): The majority of the Archive’s collection is housed on the ground floor, open to the public and free to browse. This area includes books on history, geography, science, languages, and literature, with works contributed by scholars from across Ahvantir and beyond. Here, visitors can also find extensive records of Aru’Mas’s dealings with other worlds, along with treatises on the flora, fauna, and folklore of the region.
- The Adventurer’s Section (Second Floor, Restricted): This section is accessible only to adventurers who have been granted permission to pass through the [[Spiritsway Passage]] into Ahvantir. Here, they can study specialized information on Ahvantir’s creatures, [[Spirit Pacts|spirit pacts]], regional maps, and ancient lore—resources designed to prepare them for the challenges beyond the city walls. Many of these texts are written in detailed, practical language, providing adventurers with insights on survival, regional myths, and warnings about known spirit Claims. Access is limited to adventurers of proven merit, and regular inspections ensure that these materials do not fall into unqualified hands.
- The Arcane Repository (Third Floor, Highly Restricted): The Archive’s most secure area, the Arcane Repository is a heavily warded section that holds magical knowledge, including rare spell scrolls, arcane artifacts, and treatises on high-level spellcraft. Only those with a writ of access from either the [[Order of Wisdom]] or the [[Runeguard]] may enter this section. Access to the Arcane Repository is granted sparingly, usually to mages, researchers, or guardians with a specific need for advanced magical information. The wards in this area are strong enough to deter most magical tampering, and entry is supervised by Order of Wisdom scholars trained in arcane security. For those permitted inside, the Repository offers access to powerful spells, complex magical theories, and knowledge on Ahvantir’s unique magical properties.

## Restricted Access and Permissions

The Ilderas Public Archive’s restricted areas exist to protect both the public and the integrity of the knowledge within. The requirements for accessing these sections are stringent and involve multiple layers of authorization:

- Adventurer’s Access: To enter the Adventurer’s Section, an adventurer must have proven themselves trustworthy and capable by receiving permission to venture into Ahvantir. This approval is granted by the [[Adventurers Guild Association]] after a rigorous vetting process. Authorized adventurers are issued a pass-card specifically for the Archive, allowing entry to the Adventurer’s Section, where they can research the environment and prepare for their journey.
- Writ of Access for the Arcane Repository: Access to the Arcane Repository is tightly controlled due to the nature of the knowledge contained within. Only the Order of Wisdom or the Runeguard may issue a writ of access, each on the basis of different criteria:
- Order of Wisdom: Writs from the Order are granted to scholars, healers, and researchers engaged in studying high-level arcane theory or attempting to develop magical solutions to protect the city. The Order is particularly cautious in granting these writs, prioritizing those whose work directly benefits the people of Aru’Mas.
- Runeguard: As protectors of the city, the Runeguard issues writs for those with a critical need to research offensive and defensive magic. This often includes Legion mages, high-ranking [[Skywardens]], and select officials tasked with defending Aru’Mas. The Runeguard is selective in its approvals, ensuring only trusted personnel access these spells.

## Security Measures

The Ilderas Public Archive is protected by an extensive network of both physical and magical defenses. This includes a combination of magical wards, protective sigils, and physical guards stationed at the entrances to restricted areas. The Archive also employs arcane sentries, enchanted statues and constructs that patrol the halls and respond to intrusions or attempts to tamper with the restricted sections.

Additionally, each level of the Archive has Wardens of Knowledge, scholars and mages appointed by the Order of Wisdom. These Wardens act as both custodians and defenders of the Archive’s treasures. They are highly trained in both academic study and arcane combat, capable of neutralizing intruders or rogue spellcasters attempting to access restricted information without permission.

## The Role of the Archive in Aru’Mas’s Culture

The Ilderas Public Archive is more than a library; it is a cornerstone of Aru’Mas’s culture and intellectual life. Citizens see it as a testament to the city’s commitment to knowledge, a place where wisdom and curiosity are celebrated and preserved. The Archive’s doors are open to people from all backgrounds, fostering a sense of shared learning and connection among the city’s inhabitants.

The Archive also serves as a center for public lectures, debates, and events hosted by the Order of Wisdom. These gatherings often cover topics such as the history of Aru’Mas, discussions on Ahvantir’s unique magic, and readings of famous works by scholars and poets. During these events, the main hall becomes a vibrant hub of intellectual exchange, drawing people from all corners of the city to share in the pursuit of knowledge.

For adventurers, the Archive is an invaluable resource. Before embarking on journeys beyond the city, adventurers frequently spend days or even weeks studying in the Adventurer’s Section, equipping themselves with knowledge of the challenges that await. Many see their time spent in the Archive as an essential rite of passage, where they gain the understanding and wisdom needed to survive in the wilds of Ahvantir.

## Legends and Folklore of the Archive

Over the years, the Archive has inspired numerous stories and legends, passed down among citizens, scholars, and adventurers alike:

- The Lost Tome of Throk: According to legend, the [[Hand of Throk]], the shadowy organization that protects Aru’Mas from within, possesses a secret tome hidden somewhere within the Archive. It is said that this book contains information about corruption within the city’s upper echelons, written in a code known only to the Hand. Many have searched for this tome, but none have succeeded in finding it.
- The Phantom Librarian: A popular tale among visitors tells of a ghostly librarian who appears at night, guiding lost or late-night scholars to books they need but could never find themselves. This spirit, known as the Phantom Librarian, is believed to be the spirit of a former Warden of Knowledge, bound to the Archive out of devotion to her work. Many claim to have felt a gentle tug on their sleeves or noticed books mysteriously opened to just the right page.
- The Archive’s Hidden Chamber: Some believe that beneath the Archive lies a hidden chamber containing the city’s most guarded secrets, including ancient knowledge from before the founding of Aru’Mas. This chamber, said to be accessible only by the High Oracle of the Order of Wisdom, allegedly contains forbidden spells and artifacts of immense power, guarded by spells known only to the city’s greatest scholars.

## A Pillar of Knowledge and Legacy

The Ilderas Public Archive remains a monument to the enduring quest for wisdom, a place where scholars, adventurers, and citizens alike come together to learn and grow. Its restricted sections offer invaluable resources, accessible only to those with specific needs and the appropriate permissions, ensuring that powerful or dangerous knowledge is safeguarded. The Archive’s public areas foster a sense of shared curiosity and wonder, embodying the spirit of Aru’Mas’s dedication to progress and enlightenment.

Through its expansive collections, secured areas, and storied history, the Ilderas Public Archive stands as a sanctuary of learning, a resource for the city’s guardians, and a lasting legacy of the Ilderas family’s commitment to the pursuit of knowledge. For the people of Aru’Mas, the Archive is not only a place of study but a symbol of the boundless possibilities that come from honoring both knowledge and caution in equal measure.
