# Drakehawks

Among the elite mounts of the [[Skywardens]] of Aru’Mas, the Drakehawks are revered for their sheer power, durability, and commanding presence. These colossal, dragon-like birds serve as the heavy artillery of the [[Sunspear Legion]], providing the Skywardens with both strength and resilience in the most challenging of conflicts. Known for their raw physical strength and imposing appearance, Drakehawks are a formidable sight in the skies, embodying the might and endurance that the [[Sunspear Legion]] is known for.

## Physical Attributes and Appearance

Drakehawks are large, muscular creatures, possessing broad, powerful wings and an impressive wingspan that allows them to soar high and carry heavy loads. Their bodies are covered in thick, resilient feathers that range in color from deep, charcoal gray to rich, earthen brown, with a subtle metallic sheen that gives them a look of natural armor. Their feathers are layered in such a way that they provide protection akin to light armor, adding to their resilience in battle. Around their necks and chests, the feathers are denser and tougher, protecting their vital areas from attacks.

A notable feature of the Drakehawks is their dragon-like head, with a fierce beak that curves sharply downward, resembling the jaws of a dragon. This beak is both strong and sharp, capable of tearing through armor, making it a lethal weapon in close encounters. Their talons are also incredibly powerful, designed to crush rather than simply pierce, allowing them to carry large objects and, if necessary, deliver devastating strikes to ground targets.

Their wings are broad and thickly muscled, allowing them to sustain long flights even when heavily burdened. While not as fast or agile as other mounts, their wings give them a steady, reliable flight pattern, ideal for carrying both riders and additional equipment into battle. Due to their size and strength, Drakehawks are often used in missions that require heavy lifting, as they can bear additional gear, armor, or even multiple riders when necessary.

## Temperament and Bonding with Skywardens

Drakehawks are known for their strong, grounded temperament. They are calm, steadfast, and somewhat aloof, with a natural patience that makes them highly suitable for prolonged, arduous missions. Unlike some of the more agile or swift mounts, Drakehawks are not easily excitable or reactive; instead, they remain focused and steady, unphased by turbulence, loud noises, or the chaos of battle.

Drakehawks form deep, respectful bonds with their riders, but they do not bond lightly. They prefer riders who demonstrate endurance, discipline, and a calm demeanor, valuing steady leadership over flashy skills. Drakehawks are more likely to reject riders who are overly forceful or reckless, as they respect a mutual trust between themselves and their handlers.

Once bonded, Drakehawks are known for their loyalty, often protecting their riders at all costs. They are reliable and fearless, willing to brave the fiercest battles and hold their ground even in the face of overwhelming odds. Their presence brings confidence to their riders, as they embody a sense of unbreakable strength that makes them ideal partners in missions that demand resilience and perseverance.

## Drakehawks in Combat

In battle, Drakehawks serve as the Skywardens’ heavy support, bringing both physical strength and a near-impervious fortitude to the front lines. They are not built for speed or stealth, but they make up for it with their sheer power and resilience. Drakehawks are often deployed in missions that require carrying heavy armor or armaments and are essential for operations involving prolonged combat engagements or intensive defensive maneuvers.

## Tactics and Combat Roles

- Frontline Assault: Drakehawks are used in direct confrontations, especially when the Skywardens need to clear a path or break through enemy lines. Their powerful talons and beaks make them effective at close-quarters combat, where they can sweep in low over enemy troops, delivering crushing blows or clearing obstacles with brute force.
- Heavy Lifting and Supply Transport: Due to their immense strength, Drakehawks are often assigned to transport essential supplies, weapons, and reinforcement materials. They are capable of carrying equipment or provisions that other mounts cannot, making them critical for long missions where supplies may be limited. Their steady, reliable flight allows them to transport resources across difficult terrain without issue.
- Aerial Siege Support: In siege scenarios, Drakehawks are used to drop heavy payloads from above. They can carry and release large objects—such as boulders, barrels of flammable oil, or other siege equipment—upon enemy positions, creating chaos and weakening defenses. The sheer weight and force of these drops make them highly effective in large-scale battles, especially when targeting fortified positions.
- Defensive Combat and Ground Support: Drakehawks are naturally suited for defending ground forces due to their bulk and endurance. They often act as a shield for more vulnerable troops or mounts, providing a protective line that can withstand attacks. By hovering just above the ground, they can act as a defensive barrier, intercepting enemy attacks and keeping allies out of harm’s way.
- Rescue and Evacuation Missions: Thanks to their impressive strength, Drakehawks are also utilized for extraction and evacuation missions. When troops or personnel need to be quickly pulled out of a dangerous area, a Drakehawk can swoop down, lift multiple individuals, and carry them to safety. Their calm nature in high-stress situations makes them ideal for this role, as they do not panic under pressure.

## Role in the Culture of [[Aru'Mas]]

In Aru'Mas, Drakehawks are respected as symbols of endurance, resilience, and unwavering loyalty. They represent the steadfast strength that the city prides itself on, and their powerful, steady nature has led them to be associated with stability and protection. Citizens often speak of Drakehawks as guardians, seeing them as stalwart protectors who will hold the line and ensure the city’s safety no matter the odds.

Drakehawks are frequently depicted in Aru’Mas art and literature as legendary creatures that embody the spirit of the Sunspear Legion. Many stories depict them as the defenders of the city, swooping down to protect those in need and standing firm against powerful enemies. They are considered to be almost spiritual symbols of protection, and offerings of polished stones or iron charms are sometimes left at their nesting sites as tokens of gratitude and respect.

## Legends and Lore of the Drakehawks

The Drakehawks are surrounded by legends that tell of their origins and their connection to the earth and sky. One tale suggests that the first Drakehawks were born from the union of a great eagle and a mountain dragon, combining the eagle’s loyalty and the dragon’s might. This ancestry is said to be what grants them their strength and resilience, as well as their unique, dragon-like appearance.

Another popular story tells of a Drakehawk named Ironfeather, who, in a time of war, carried her rider across enemy lines to retrieve fallen allies. Despite the injuries she sustained, Ironfeather held her ground, allowing her rider to rescue others until they safely returned to Aru’Mas. To this day, Ironfeather’s name is invoked by Skywardens who seek courage and strength, especially in situations where they must endure great adversity.

There is also a legend of the Stone Drakehawk, a mythic creature said to sleep beneath the mountains of Ahvantir. According to the tale, this ancient Drakehawk will awaken if Aru’Mas ever faces an overwhelming threat, rising to defend the city in its hour of need. Children of Aru'Mas are often told this story as a bedtime tale, with the Stone Drakehawk symbolizing the city’s hidden strength and protection.

## Drakehawks as Pillars of Strength in the Skywardens

In their physical form and their symbolism, Drakehawks represent the strength and reliability that the Skywardens bring to the Sunspear Legion. They are more than just mounts—they are partners and protectors, embodying the city’s resilience and dedication to safeguarding its people. Their unwavering presence in the skies and their capacity to bear heavy burdens reflect the values of the Sunspear Legion, making them indispensable assets to Aru’Mas.

To see a Drakehawk soaring over the city or stationed in formation with the Skywardens is a reassurance to the citizens of Aru’Mas, a reminder that they are protected by creatures who are as enduring and steadfast as the city itself. Whether in battle, on patrol, or stationed in defense, the Drakehawks are both the heavy lifters and silent guardians of the skies, safeguarding the spirit of Aru’Mas with every beat of their powerful wings.
